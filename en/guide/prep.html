Preparation methods (herbal)

⚠ Educational background only — not medical advice. Many plants are unsafe in the wrong dose, wrong part, during pregnancy, or if confused with a look-alike. Never self-treat serious conditions; consult a qualified professional, and never gather a wild plant you cannot identify with certainty.
Infusion (منقوع)For soft leaves and flowers: pour just-boiled water over the herb, cover, steep 5–10 minutes, strain. Like a tea.
Decoction (مغلي)For hard roots, bark and seeds: simmer in water 10–20 minutes, then strain. Extracts what infusion cannot.
Poultice / compress (كمادة)Crushed fresh herb (poultice) or a cloth soaked in a warm infusion (compress) applied to the skin — external use.
Syrup (شراب)A strong infusion or decoction sweetened with honey or sugar to preserve it and soothe throats. Refrigerate.
Cold infusion / maceration (نقع بارد)Steep the herb in cold water for several hours — gentle, good for mucilaginous plants and delicate aromas.
