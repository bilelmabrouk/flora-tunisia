Tools & basic equipment

You do not need much to start. A few good tools, cared for, go a long way.

Essentials: a hand trowel and fork for planting; sharp bypass secateurs (pruners) for stems and light branches; a watering can with a fine rose, or a hose with an adjustable nozzle; and sturdy gloves. For a garden, add a spade, a digging fork, a rake and a pruning saw for thicker wood.

For pots and indoor plants: a small watering can, a mister for humidity-loving foliage, a moisture meter or just your finger, and a bag of quality potting mix. Keep saucers under pots but never let plants sit in standing water.

Watering kit matters most in our climate: a simple drip line or soaker hose on a timer saves water and keeps plants steady through the summer far better than occasional heavy soakings. Mulch (see the glossary) is your cheapest, most effective 'tool' against heat and evaporation.

Care for tools: rinse soil off, dry them, and wipe blades with a little oil to stop rust. Clean secateurs with alcohol between plants to avoid spreading disease. Sharp, clean tools make cleaner cuts that heal faster.
