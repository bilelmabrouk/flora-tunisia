Monthly jobs calendar

A month-by-month checklist for a typical Tunisian garden. Shift a few weeks later in the cool north-west (zone 2) and earlier in the warm south (zones 5–6).

MonthWhat to do
JanuaryPlant bare-root and dormant fruit trees, roses and olives. Prune deciduous trees and vines. Protect tender plants from frost in the north.
FebruaryFinish winter pruning. Start tomato, pepper and aubergine seeds under cover. Apply compost to beds.
MarchSow hardy vegetables outdoors. Divide perennials. Feed citrus as growth starts. Watch for aphids.
AprilPlant out hardened seedlings after frost risk passes. Mulch beds before heat. Sow warm-season crops.
MayIncrease watering as it warms. Stake tall plants. Deadhead spring flowers. Harvest early stone fruit.
JuneWater deeply early morning or evening. Mulch to fight evaporation. Shade tender pots. Harvest summer veg.
JulyPeak heat: water consistently, avoid feeding stressed plants. Harvest daily. Do not prune in full heat.
AugustKeep watering; check pots twice a day. Sow autumn vegetables late month. Collect seeds.
SeptemberBest planting month returns as heat eases. Plant perennials and shrubs. Divide overgrown clumps.
OctoberPlant trees and bulbs. Sow broad beans, peas and leafy greens. Reduce watering as rains start.
NovemberPlant olives and citrus in mild zones. Mulch for winter. Bring tender pots under cover in the north.
DecemberRest period. Prune dormant trees late month. Protect from frost. Plan next year and order seeds.
Bilel Mabrouk & Claude
