Tunisia by region

1

Northern / Tell coast

Climate: Mediterranean, mild wet winters and warm summers, the highest rainfall in the country.
Governorates: Tunis, Ariana, Ben Arous, Manouba, Bizerte, Nabeul
Good rainfall and mild frost risk make this the easiest zone for a wide range of plants, including many that struggle further south. Watch for waterlogging in heavy clay soils during winter.
2

North-West / Tell mountains

Climate: Sub-humid, cooler and wetter than the coast, occasional winter frost and even light snow at altitude.
Governorates: Béja, Jendouba, Le Kef, Siliana, Zaghouan
Best zone for plants that need a real winter chill or dislike summer heat. Protect tender species from occasional frost pockets in valleys.
3

Sahel / Central-East

Climate: Semi-arid coastal, hot dry summers moderated by the sea, mild winters, moderate rainfall.
Governorates: Sousse, Monastir, Mahdia, Sfax
Sea breeze softens summer heat but salt spray near the coast can stress leaves. Mulch well and water deeply but less often through summer.
4

Central interior / Steppe

Climate: Semi-arid to arid steppe, hot summers, cool winters, low and irregular rainfall, strong sun.
Governorates: Kairouan, Kasserine, Sidi Bouzid, Gafsa
Choose drought-tolerant species and plan for irrigation, since natural rainfall alone is not reliable here. Afternoon shade helps young plants through summer.
5

South-East / Pre-desert

Climate: Arid, very hot summers, mild winters, minimal rainfall, higher humidity near the coast and islands.
Governorates: Gabès, Medenine, Tataouine (incl. Djerba, Kerkennah)
Focus on heat- and salt-tolerant species; oasis-style plantings with shade layers work well. Water early morning or evening to cut evaporation loss.
6

South-West / Saharan oases

Climate: True desert climate, extreme summer heat, very low rainfall, cool nights in winter, oasis microclimates near water.
Governorates: Tozeur, Kébili
Rely on oasis shade and irrigation; palms and date-grove companions do best under the canopy of taller trees. Full open sun is punishing for anything but true desert natives.
