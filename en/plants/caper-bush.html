Caper bush

Capparis spinosa  ·  Family: Capparaceae
Exterior · Shrub
EN Caper bush  ·  FR Câprier  ·  AR نبتة الكبّار (القبّار)  ·  Capparis spinosa
Origin: Mediterranean & West Asia
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A sprawling Mediterranean native whose unopened flower buds are the pickled caper; thrives in heat, drought, rocks and old walls where little else grows.
Light
Full sun.
Water
Very low; deeply drought-tolerant.
Soil
Poor, rocky, sharply drained soil.
Temperature
Loves heat; hardy to light frost.
Humidity
Loves dry air.
Feeding
No feeding needed.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back hard in late winter.
Propagation
From seed (tricky) or cuttings.
Mature size
0.5–1.5 m, spreading.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Long-lived.
Bloom / fruit season
White flowers with purple stamens in summer.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to about −8 °C when dormant.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; buds and fruit are edible pickled.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) No fertilizer needed; poor, dry soil gives the best plants and buds.  Find fertilizer →
