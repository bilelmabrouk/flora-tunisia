Rex begonia

Begonia rex  ·  Family: Begoniaceae
Interior · Flowering
EN Rex begonia  ·  FR Bégonia rex  ·  AR بيغونيا ريكس  ·  Begonia rex
Origin: North-East India (Assam)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Indoor
In short Grown primarily for its stunning metallic, patterned leaves in silver, purple, and green — a foliage showpiece for bright Tunisian rooms. Needs high humidity and careful watering to thrive.
Light
Bright indirect light — avoid direct sun which bleaches the leaf colours.
Water
Keep soil lightly moist; let the top 2 cm dry before watering. Never overwater — root rot is the main killer.
Soil
Light, airy, well-draining mix with plenty of perlite; rhizomatous roots dislike dense soil.
Temperature
Ideal 18–25 °C; keep above 15 °C in winter.
Humidity
Needs high humidity (50–70%); use a pebble tray, never mist directly on leaves (causes spotting and mildew).
Feeding
Every 4 weeks in spring–summer with a half-dose balanced fertilizer.
Pruning & grooming
Remove old, damaged, or overly large leaves to encourage fresh growth.
Propagation
Leaf cuttings (whole leaf or sections) in moist perlite or water; rhizome division.
Mature size
20–50 cm tall and wide.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
2–4 years looking its best; renew from cuttings.
Bloom / fruit season
Small pink or white flowers in summer; secondary to the foliage.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; minimum 15 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Toxic if ingested (calcium oxalates); especially the roots. Keep from children and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced fertilizer at half the recommended dose every 4 weeks during active growth; avoid excess feeding which causes weak, leggy stems.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Pale, dull leaf coloursToo little or too much light.Find a bright spot with indirect light — no direct sun.
White powdery spots on leavesPowdery mildew from poor air circulation.Improve airflow; remove affected leaves; avoid wetting foliage.
Soft rotting stemsRoot rot from overwatering.Remove rotted tissue, dry the plant, repot in fresh airy mix.
Availability: Available in specialist plant shops in Tunisia; less common than standard begonias.
