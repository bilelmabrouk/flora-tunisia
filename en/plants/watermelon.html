Watermelon

Citrullus lanatus  ·  Family: Cucurbitaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Watermelon  ·  FR Pastèque  ·  AR دلاع  ·  Citrullus lanatus
Origin: Sub-Saharan Africa (wild ancestor from the Kalahari region)
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short Tunisia's summer king: the pastèque thrives in the long hot season, producing sweet heavy fruits from June to August across most of the country. Needs space, full sun, and a reliable water supply.
Light
Full sun; at least 8 hours daily for good fruit development.
Water
Moderate; water deeply and regularly until fruit sets, then reduce slightly; avoid wetting foliage.
Soil
Light sandy-loam, well-drained, rich in organic matter; pH 6-7.
Temperature
Warm-season crop; needs soil above 18 C to germinate; optimum 25-35 C.
Humidity
Prefers dry air; high humidity encourages fungal disease.
Feeding
High-nitrogen feed at planting, then switch to phosphorus-potassium-rich feed at flowering.
Pruning & grooming
Pinch tips to encourage lateral fruiting vines; limit to 2-3 fruits per plant.
Propagation
By seed sown directly after last cold spell, or started indoors 3 weeks early.
Mature size
Vines spread 2-5 m; fruits 3-15 kg depending on variety.
Growth rate
Fast; from seed to harvest in 70-90 days.
Lifespan
Annual; one growing season.
Bloom / fruit season
Yellow flowers from May; bees essential for pollination.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-sensitive; do not plant out until all cold risk has passed.
Toxicity & safety
Fully edible and non-toxic.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Start with a nitrogen-rich fertilizer to build strong vines, then switch to a phosphorus- and potassium-rich blend once flowers appear to boost fruit set and sweetness.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Fruits crack or splitIrregular watering; heavy rain after a dry period.Water consistently; use mulch to buffer moisture swings.
White powdery coating on leavesPowdery mildew, common in late summer humidity.Improve air circulation; spray with dilute bicarbonate solution; remove badly affected leaves.
Fruits fail to develop despite flowersPoor pollination; lack of bees or wet/cool conditions during bloom.Hand-pollinate by transferring pollen from male to female flowers with a brush.
Wilting during the day even with moist soilFusarium wilt fungus in the soil.Remove affected plants; rotate cucurbits to a new bed next season.
Availability: Widely grown across Tunisia; seeds and seedlings readily available in spring.
