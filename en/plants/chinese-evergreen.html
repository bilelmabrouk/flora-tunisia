Chinese evergreen

Aglaonema commutatum  ·  Family: Araceae
Interior · Foliage
EN Chinese evergreen  ·  FR Aglaonème  ·  AR أغلاونيما  ·  Aglaonema commutatum
Origin: SE Asia (Philippines, Indonesia)
Light
Shade / indirect
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Indoor
In short One of the toughest indoor foliage plants around — thrives in low light and forgives irregular watering, making it ideal for Tunisian interiors with little natural light.
Light
Low to medium indirect light; avoid direct sun which scorches leaves.
Water
Water when top 3–4 cm of soil dry out; reduce in winter. Dislikes soggy roots.
Soil
Well-draining peat-based or universal mix with perlite.
Temperature
Prefers 18–28 °C; sensitive to cold draughts and temperatures below 13 °C.
Humidity
Appreciates humidity; mist leaves or use a tray of pebbles and water.
Feeding
Balanced liquid fertilizer every 4–6 weeks in spring and summer only.
Pruning & grooming
Remove yellowed or damaged leaves at the base; no structural pruning needed.
Propagation
Stem cuttings in water or moist mix, or by division at repotting.
Mature size
30–90 cm tall indoors, spread to ~60 cm.
Growth rate
Slow to moderate.
Lifespan
Long-lived houseplant — many years with good care.
Bloom / fruit season
Rarely flowers indoors; small inconspicuous spadix if it does.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; keep above 13 °C at all times.
Toxicity & safety
Toxic if chewed — calcium oxalate crystals irritate mouth and gut; keep from pets and children.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced liquid fertilizer diluted to half-strength every 4–6 weeks during the growing season keeps foliage vibrant; stop feeding in autumn and winter.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellow leavesOver-watering or too-cold conditions.Let soil dry more between waterings; move away from cold draughts.
Brown leaf tipsLow humidity or fluoride in tap water.Mist regularly; use filtered or overnight tap water.
Pale or washed-out leavesToo much direct light bleaching the variegation.Move to a shadier spot away from direct sun.
Root rot / wilting despite wet soilWaterlogged soil lacking drainage.Repot into fresh well-draining mix; trim rotten roots; water less.
Availability: Commonly grown and sold in Tunisia; widely available in garden centres and markets.
