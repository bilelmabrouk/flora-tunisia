Swiss chard

Beta vulgaris (Cicla)  ·  Family: Amaranthaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Swiss chard  ·  FR Blette  ·  AR سلق  ·  Beta vulgaris (Cicla)
Origin: Mediterranean (probably Sicily/S Italy)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Moderate
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A heat- and cold-tolerant leaf vegetable with colourful stems, integral to Tunisian cuisine. Grows almost year-round along the coast and in the north.
Light
Full sun to partial shade; tolerates more shade than most vegetables.
Water
Regular watering; keep soil moist but not waterlogged.
Soil
Rich, deep, well-drained soil with compost; pH 6–7.5.
Temperature
Tolerant of heat and light frost; grows from 5–35 °C.
Humidity
Adaptable; coastal humidity not a problem.
Feeding
Balanced vegetable feed every 3 weeks during active growth.
Pruning & grooming
Harvest outer stalks and leaves from the base as needed.
Propagation
Direct sow or transplant; sow in spring and autumn for succession.
Mature size
50–70 cm tall.
Growth rate
Moderate — harvestable in 50–60 days.
Lifespan
Biennial grown as annual; can last 2+ years in mild climates.
Bloom / fruit season
Second year only; flower stalks should be removed to prolong harvest.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tolerates light frost to −5 °C; protect young plants in hard frost.
Toxicity & safety
Safe; high oxalate content — moderate intake for kidney-stone sufferers.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced vegetable fertilizer every 3 weeks, with emphasis on nitrogen, promotes lush leafy growth.  Find fertilizer →

Wilting or yellow leavesUnderwatering or nutrient deficiency.Water more regularly; apply balanced fertilizer.
Rust-coloured spots on leavesCercospora leaf spot fungus.Remove affected leaves; ensure good ventilation; avoid wetting foliage.
Availability: Commonly grown / sold in Tunisia; a kitchen-garden staple across the country.
