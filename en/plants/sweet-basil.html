Sweet basil

Ocimum basilicum  ·  Family: Lamiaceae
Exterior · Herb / aromatic
EN Sweet basil  ·  FR Basilic  ·  AR حبق  ·  Ocimum basilicum
Origin: Tropical Asia
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short Tunisia's most popular culinary herb — fragrant, fast-growing, and indispensable in Tunisian cooking from salads to brik. Loves heat and sun but needs steady watering in summer.
Light
Full sun — at least 6 hours; more sun means more flavour.
Water
Moderate: water regularly to keep soil moist; don't let it dry out in summer heat.
Soil
Rich, well-drained, slightly moist soil with good organic matter.
Temperature
Warmth-loving; best at 20–30 °C; damaged below 10 °C — treat as annual in cooler regions.
Humidity
Tolerates Tunisian coastal humidity; avoid waterlogging.
Feeding
Light balanced feed every 3–4 weeks during the growing season; excess nitrogen reduces flavour.
Pruning & grooming
Pinch out flower heads as they form to extend the harvest and keep bushy.
Propagation
Easily from seed sown after the last cold spell; or stem cuttings in water.
Mature size
30–60 cm tall depending on variety.
Growth rate
Fast — harvest-ready in 4–6 weeks from seed.
Lifespan
Annual; self-seeds readily if a few plants are allowed to flower.
Bloom / fruit season
Small white flowers summer to autumn; remove to keep leaf production going.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-sensitive; damaged at 5 °C, killed by frost — restart each spring.
Toxicity & safety
Fully safe; widely used in cooking for all ages.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced fertilizer applied every 3–4 weeks in spring and summer keeps growth lush; avoid high-nitrogen feeds that reduce scent.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Black stems at baseDamping-off fungus from overwatering or poor drainage.Remove affected plants; improve drainage; reduce watering.
Leaves turning yellowOverwatering, root bound in pot, or nutrient deficiency.Adjust watering; repot if needed; apply balanced fertilizer.
Leaves wilting in afternoon despite wateringExtreme heat stress — normal in Tunisian midsummer.Provide light shade 12–15 h; water early morning; mulch.
Sluggish growth after transplantTransplant shock or cold soil.Wait for warm soil (above 18 °C); water gently; avoid fertilizing until recovery.
Availability: Widely sold in Tunisian markets and nurseries throughout the warm season; seeds easily found year-round.
