Money tree

Pachira aquatica  ·  Family: Malvaceae
Interior · Foliage
EN Money tree  ·  FR Pachira  ·  AR باشيرا  ·  Pachira aquatica
Origin: Central and South America (swamps and riverbanks)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Indoor
In short A cheerful and easy indoor tree sold with a braided trunk — adaptable to Tunisian home conditions and popular as a gift plant symbolising good fortune.
Light
Bright indirect light; tolerates moderate shade. Rotate quarterly for even growth.
Water
Water thoroughly when top 5 cm dry out; the thick trunk stores water so it tolerates short droughts. Never waterlog.
Soil
Well-draining sandy or loam mix; perlite helps prevent root rot.
Temperature
Best at 16–29 °C; avoid cold draughts and temperatures below 10 °C.
Humidity
Appreciates moderate humidity; mist occasionally.
Feeding
Balanced liquid fertilizer every 4 weeks in spring and summer.
Pruning & grooming
Trim to shape in spring; remove dead or crossing branches.
Propagation
From seed or softwood cuttings in a warm environment.
Mature size
1–2 m indoors; trunk braiding done at nursery stage.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Long-lived if conditions are stable.
Bloom / fruit season
Rarely blooms indoors; flowers are large and showy if it does flower.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; keep above 10 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced liquid fertilizer every 4 weeks in spring and summer maintains healthy foliage; avoid over-feeding which causes excessive leggy growth.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellow leavesOver-watering or waterlogged roots.Reduce watering; ensure pot has drainage; check for root rot.
Leaf drop after purchaseAcclimatisation stress from change of environment.Place in stable bright spot; avoid moving; new leaves will emerge in weeks.
Leggy, stretched growthInsufficient light.Move closer to a bright window; rotate regularly.
Availability: Commonly sold in Tunisian garden centres and gift shops; often sold with braided trunks.
