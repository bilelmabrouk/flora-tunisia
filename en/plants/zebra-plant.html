Zebra plant ⇄ also: Exterior

Haworthia fasciata  ·  Family: Asphodelaceae
Interior · Succulent / cactus
EN Zebra plant  ·  FR Haworthia  ·  AR هاورثيا  ·  Haworthia fasciata
Origin: Eastern Cape, South Africa
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Indoor & out
In short A compact, zebra-striped succulent that thrives in low light and asks for almost nothing — one of the most forgiving houseplants for Tunisia's warm interiors.
Light
Bright indirect light; tolerates low light — avoid harsh midday sun which scorches leaves.
Water
Very low: water when soil is completely dry; every 3–4 weeks in summer, rarely in winter.
Soil
Free-draining cactus mix or standard potting soil with added perlite or coarse sand.
Temperature
Happy at 16–27 °C; avoid drafts and temperatures below 5 °C.
Humidity
Low; adapts well to dry indoor air common in Tunisian homes.
Feeding
Once a month in spring–summer with a dilute cactus fertilizer; skip in winter.
Pruning & grooming
Remove dead or damaged leaves at the base; no real pruning needed.
Propagation
By offsets (pups) from the base — detach and pot up in dry mix.
Mature size
Up to 15 cm tall and 10 cm wide; stays small.
Growth rate
Very slow.
Lifespan
Many years; multiplies steadily via offsets.
Bloom / fruit season
Small tubular white flowers on tall thin spikes in spring–summer.
Hardiness (min temp)
Keep above 5 °C; not frost-hardy.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A cactus fertilizer at half the recommended dose, applied once a month in spring and summer, is all it needs — more causes soft, etiolated growth.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Brown, mushy baseRoot rot from overwatering.Unpot, cut away rot, let dry 2 days, repot in dry gritty mix.
Pale or yellowish leavesToo much direct sun or over-watering.Move to bright indirect light; reduce watering frequency and check for root rot.
Mushy, translucent leavesOverwatering, especially in winter dormancy.Stop watering, remove damaged leaves, and let the soil dry out fully before resuming.
Tiny cottony white clusters at the leaf baseMealybug infestation.Dab with cotton soaked in rubbing alcohol; isolate the plant until clear.
Availability: Very common houseplant in Tunisia, sold in nurseries, souks, and supermarkets year-round.
