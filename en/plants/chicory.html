Chicory

Cichorium intybus  ·  Family: Asteraceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Chicory  ·  FR Chicorée  ·  AR شيكوري  ·  Cichorium intybus
Origin: Mediterranean Europe (native to Europe and Western Asia)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A hardy perennial with pretty blue flowers, grown in Tunisia both as a salad green and for its roots. Drought-tolerant and low-maintenance, it suits northern and central gardens.
Light
Full sun; tolerates partial shade.
Water
Low to moderate; drought-tolerant once established.
Soil
Deep, well-drained soil; tolerates poor and clay soils.
Temperature
Hardy; tolerates frost down to −15 °C.
Humidity
Adaptable; not sensitive to humidity.
Feeding
Minimal; a light balanced fertilizer once or twice a season.
Pruning & grooming
Cut leaves for salad; cut back after flowering to encourage new growth.
Propagation
Direct sow in spring; can divide established clumps.
Mature size
60–120 cm tall when flowering; rosette 30–40 cm wide.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Perennial — comes back each year.
Bloom / fruit season
Beautiful bright-blue flowers in summer (July–September).
Hardiness (min temp)
Very frost-hardy; survives cold winters in the north.
Toxicity & safety
Safe; mildly bitter taste.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Chicory needs very little fertilizer — a single balanced application in spring is sufficient for the whole season.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Bitter leaves unpleasant to eatNatural bitterness increases in heat or with age.Harvest young leaves in spring; blanch hearts before harvest.
Powdery mildew patchesFungal disease in warm humid conditions.Improve air circulation; remove affected leaves.
Root damage and wiltingRoot-feeding insects or root rot.Improve drainage; rotate planting location each year.
Availability: Available in Tunisia in garden centres and markets; also grows wild in fields.
