Tree philodendron ⇄ also: Exterior

Philodendron bipinnatifidum  ·  Family: Araceae
Interior · Foliage
EN Tree philodendron  ·  FR Philodendron arborescent  ·  AR فيلودندرون شجري  ·  Philodendron bipinnatifidum
Origin: South America (Brazil, Paraguay, Argentina)

Setting
Indoor & out
In short A bold, jungle-feel foliage plant whose huge, deeply lobed leaves make a statement indoors or in a sheltered Tunisian patio. Unfussy and fast-growing in warm conditions.
Light
Bright indirect light indoors; tolerates light shade. Outdoors needs partial shade in Tunisian summer.
Water
Water when the top 3–4 cm of soil dry out; reduce in winter. Avoid waterlogging.
Soil
Rich, well-drained mix with perlite and organic matter; good aeration is key.
Temperature
Thrives at 18–30 °C; protect from frost — not hardy below 5 °C.
Humidity
Prefers moderate to high humidity; mist leaves or set pot on a pebble tray in dry Tunisian summers.
Feeding
Monthly in spring–summer with a balanced foliage fertilizer; stop in winter.
Pruning & grooming
Remove dead or yellowing leaves at the base; no structural pruning needed.
Propagation
By stem cuttings or by separating basal offshoots; root in moist soil or water.
Mature size
1.5–3 m tall indoors; can reach 4 m outdoors in warm climates.
Growth rate
Fast in warmth and good light.
Lifespan
Long-lived — many years with good care.
Bloom / fruit season
Rarely flowers indoors; inflorescences occasional on mature outdoor plants.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; protect below 5 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Toxic if ingested (calcium oxalate crystals); irritating to skin and mucous membranes — keep away from children and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced foliage fertilizer (equal N-P-K) monthly during the growing season supports the large leaves; halve the dose in autumn.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellow lower leavesOver-watering or poor drainage.Let soil dry more between waterings; improve drainage.
Brown leaf tipsDry air or underwatering in hot weather.Increase humidity and water more regularly in summer.
Leggy, pale stemsInsufficient light.Move closer to a bright window or to a partly shaded outdoor spot.
Scale insects on stemsDry conditions indoors, especially in winter.Wipe off with a damp cloth; treat with insecticidal soap if persistent.
Availability: Commonly grown and sold in Tunisian garden centres; popular in coastal cities.
