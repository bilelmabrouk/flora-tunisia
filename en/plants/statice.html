Statice

Limonium sinuatum  ·  Family: Plumbaginaceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN Statice  ·  FR Statice  ·  AR قويسة  ·  Limonium sinuatum
Origin: Mediterranean
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A native Mediterranean wildflower beloved for its papery, ever-lasting blooms — cuts and dries beautifully, and thrives in salty coastal soils across all of Tunisia.
Light
Full sun — essential for good flowering and preventing disease.
Water
Low: drought-tolerant once established; water young plants; reduce after flowering.
Soil
Sandy, well-drained, slightly alkaline; tolerates salty and poor soils.
Temperature
Warm Mediterranean summers to cool winters; hardy to about −5 °C.
Humidity
Prefers dry air; high humidity encourages fungal disease.
Feeding
Light feed once in spring; too much nitrogen reduces flowering.
Pruning & grooming
Cut flower stems for drying before they fully open; remove old stems after flowering.
Propagation
From seed sown in early spring; transplant seedlings when large enough.
Mature size
30–60 cm tall.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Often grown as an annual; can be short-lived perennial in mild areas.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring to early summer (April–July in Tunisia).
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to about −5 °C; tolerates coastal wind and salt spray.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; safe for people and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A single light application of balanced fertilizer in spring is enough; excess feeding promotes foliage at the expense of flowers.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Grey mould on stems and flowersBotrytis fungus in damp, humid conditions.Improve air circulation; remove affected parts; avoid overhead watering.
Pale, washed-out flower colourToo little sun or nutrient-depleted soil.Move to full sun; apply a light balanced feed.
Root rot and wiltingOverwatering or heavy, poorly drained soil.Water sparingly; plant in sandy, well-drained mix.
Availability: Widely available in Tunisian garden centres; common as a cut and dried flower crop along the Sahel coast.
