Ribbon grass

Phalaris arundinacea 'Picta'  ·  Family: Poaceae
Exterior · Ornamental grass
EN Ribbon grass  ·  FR Baldingère panachée  ·  AR حشيشة الشريط  ·  Phalaris arundinacea 'Picta'
Origin: Europe, western Asia and North Africa (cultivar selected for variegated foliage)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
High
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A vigorous, white-striped grass that brightens shaded or moist corners and waterside plantings. It spreads readily so keep it contained; best in the cooler, wetter north of Tunisia.
Light
Full sun to partial shade; variegation is brighter with more sun.
Water
High; prefers consistently moist to wet soil; ideal near water features.
Soil
Rich, moisture-retentive soil; thrives in heavy clay or boggy ground.
Temperature
Very cold-hardy; survives hard frosts and dies back in winter, re-sprouting in spring.
Humidity
Prefers high humidity; suits the humid north of Tunisia.
Feeding
Light balanced fertilizer once in spring; avoid over-feeding which promotes invasive spread.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back hard to the ground in late winter; shear in midsummer if it flops or scorches.
Propagation
By division in spring or autumn; very easy.
Mature size
60-100 cm tall; spreads indefinitely by rhizomes if not contained.
Growth rate
Fast; can become invasive if not managed.
Lifespan
Perennial; indefinite lifespan with management.
Bloom / fruit season
Feathery pale plumes in late spring to early summer.
Hardiness (min temp)
Very hardy, to -30 C or below.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) One light application of balanced fertilizer in spring is plenty; richer feeding encourages more aggressive spread without improving the look.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Plant spreads aggressively out of boundsVigorous rhizome growth; natural behaviour of the species.Plant inside a buried root barrier; divide and remove excess clumps each spring.
Foliage loses variegation, turns all greenReversion to the plain green species; common in vigorous grasses.Dig out and discard the all-green shoots; keep only the white-striped ones.
Leaf tips turn brown in summer heatHeat and dry soil; the plant needs consistent moisture.Water more frequently; mulch heavily; shear back to fresh green growth.
Availability: Occasionally available in Tunisian nurseries, particularly those specialising in water garden or ornamental grass plants.
