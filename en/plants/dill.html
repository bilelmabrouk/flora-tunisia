Dill

Anethum graveolens  ·  Family: Apiaceae
Exterior · Herb / aromatic
EN Dill  ·  FR Aneth  ·  AR شبت  ·  Anethum graveolens
Origin: South-Western Asia
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A fast-growing, feathery annual herb beloved in Tunisian kitchens for its distinctive flavour in fish dishes and salads. Best sown directly in cool seasons; succession sowing ensures a year-round supply.
Light
Full sun — 6+ hours; shade causes floppy stems.
Water
Moderate: keep soil evenly moist; don't let it dry out completely.
Soil
Well-drained, moderately fertile; tolerates average garden soil.
Temperature
Cool to warm: best 15–25 °C; bolts in heat above 30 °C — grow as a winter-spring crop in Tunisia.
Humidity
Tolerates average humidity; good air circulation reduces disease risk.
Feeding
Minimal; a single light balanced feed at planting is usually enough.
Pruning & grooming
Harvest leaves regularly; remove flower heads to delay bolting.
Propagation
Direct sow only — deep taproot means it dislikes transplanting; sow autumn to spring.
Mature size
60–120 cm tall when flowering.
Growth rate
Fast — leaves ready in 3–4 weeks.
Lifespan
Annual; self-seeds if allowed to flower.
Bloom / fruit season
Flat yellow umbel flowers in spring; signals end of leaf harvest.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tolerates light frost; grows well in mild Tunisian winters.
Toxicity & safety
Safe; widely consumed in cooking and teas.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Dill rarely needs fertilizer in decent soil; if growth is weak, a light balanced feed once at planting is enough.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Premature bolting (shooting to flower)Heat, drought, or long days trigger bolting.Sow in cool season (autumn–winter in Tunisia); provide afternoon shade; water consistently.
Floppy, leaning stemsToo much shade or insufficient light.Move or sow in a sunnier spot; stake if needed.
Powdery white patches on foliagePowdery mildew in humid or overcrowded conditions.Thin out plants; improve airflow; avoid wetting leaves.
Availability: Widely available in Tunisian markets and nurseries, especially in cooler months; seeds found year-round in spice stalls.
