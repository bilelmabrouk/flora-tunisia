Sage-leaf rockrose

Cistus salviifolius  ·  Family: Cistaceae
Exterior · Ground-cover
EN Sage-leaf rockrose  ·  FR Ciste à feuilles de sauge  ·  AR لادن مفترش  ·  Cistus salviifolius
Origin: Mediterranean basin (including Tunisia)
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A native Tunisian groundcover shrub smothered in white papery flowers each spring. Completely drought-tolerant once established and thriving in poor, rocky soils — an ideal low-maintenance garden plant.
Light
Full sun; essential for flowering and compact growth.
Water
Very low once established; natural rainfall in most of Tunisia is enough.
Soil
Poor, rocky, well-drained, alkaline or slightly acid; dislikes clay and rich soils.
Temperature
Tolerates frost to −10 °C and heat above 40 °C with ease.
Humidity
Adapted to dry Mediterranean air; good salt tolerance near the coast.
Feeding
No feeding necessary; extra nutrients cause lush growth and fewer flowers.
Pruning & grooming
Light trim after flowering to keep shape; never cut into old wood.
Propagation
Semi-hardwood cuttings in late summer, or seed in autumn.
Mature size
60–100 cm tall and 1–1.5 m wide.
Growth rate
Moderate; fills in over 2–3 seasons.
Lifespan
10–15 years; performance declines on very old woody plants.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring (March–May), abundant white flowers with yellow stamens.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy; tolerates frost and prolonged summer heat and drought.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; the fragrant resin is used traditionally but not harmful.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) No fertilizer is recommended; this native Mediterranean plant thrives on poor soils and extra feeding upsets its natural balance, promoting rank growth over flowers.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Sudden wilting and deathRoot rot from overwatering, especially in summer.Cistus dislikes summer watering; if in a garden irrigation zone, ensure it is excluded or kept very dry.
Leggy, open growth after a few yearsNatural ageing of woody-based shrub.Light trim after flowering only; replace with young plants every 8–10 years.
Few or no flowersToo much shade or over-fertilised soil.Move to full sun; stop all fertilizer applications.
Availability: Native to Tunisia; found growing wild in northern garrigue and available in nurseries across the country.
