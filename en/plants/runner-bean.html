Runner bean

Phaseolus coccineus  ·  Family: Fabaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Runner bean  ·  FR Haricot d'Espagne  ·  AR فاصوليا حمراء  ·  Phaseolus coccineus
Origin: Central America (Mexico and Guatemala highlands)
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A vigorous climbing bean best suited to the cooler, more humid north of Tunisia; its scarlet flowers are also ornamental and the long flat pods are prolific when grown on a sturdy trellis.
Light
Full sun to light partial shade; some afternoon shade helps in summer heat.
Water
Regular and consistent; keep soil moist especially at flowering — drought causes pod drop.
Soil
Rich, well-drained, moisture-retentive soil with plenty of organic matter; pH 6–7.
Temperature
Prefers cool to mild temperatures 12–22 °C; suffers above 30 °C; frost-tender.
Humidity
Moderate to high; needs more atmospheric moisture than common beans.
Feeding
Balanced vegetable fertilizer every 3 weeks; low in nitrogen to favour pods over foliage.
Pruning & grooming
Pinch shoot tips once reaching the top of the support to encourage lateral pods.
Propagation
Direct sow 5 cm deep after last frost; or start indoors 3–4 weeks before the last frost date.
Mature size
Climbs 2.5–3.5 m; needs strong support.
Growth rate
Fast; pods ready to harvest 65–80 days from sowing.
Lifespan
Annual in Tunisia (perennial in frost-free climates).
Bloom / fruit season
Vivid scarlet (rarely white or pink) flowers from spring to summer.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; do not plant until temperatures are consistently above 10 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Safe when cooked; raw pods may cause mild gastric discomfort.

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Flowers drop without setting podsHigh temperatures (above 30 °C) during flowering or inadequate pollination.Provide light shade in peak summer; water consistently; encourage bees by planting nearby nectar plants.
Rust-coloured spots and pustules on leavesBean rust (Uromyces phaseoli).Remove affected leaves; improve airflow; avoid leaf wetting when watering.
Aphid colonies on shoot tips and undersides of leavesBlack bean aphid (Aphis fabae) and others.Spray with water or dilute soap solution; pinch badly infested tips; encourage natural predators.
Availability: Less common than French beans in Tunisia; seeds available at specialist garden centres and online.
