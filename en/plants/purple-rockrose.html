Purple rockrose

Cistus × purpureus  ·  Family: Cistaceae
Exterior · Shrub
EN Purple rockrose  ·  FR Ciste pourpre  ·  AR لادن بنفسجي  ·  Cistus × purpureus
Origin: Mediterranean (garden hybrid of C. ladanifer × C. incanus)
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A showy hybrid rockrose bearing large deep-pink flowers with a dark crimson basal blotch — a Mediterranean favourite combining beauty with extreme drought-hardiness for sunny Tunisian gardens.
Light
Full sun; flowering diminishes quickly in shade.
Water
Very drought-tolerant once established; no regular irrigation needed.
Soil
Well-drained, poor to moderately fertile; tolerates limestone.
Temperature
Hardy to about −5 to −8 °C; suitable for coastal Tunisia.
Humidity
Prefers dry Mediterranean conditions.
Feeding
No fertiliser required; avoid high-nitrogen feeds.
Pruning & grooming
Deadhead spent flowers; light trim after bloom; avoid cutting old wood.
Propagation
From semi-ripe cuttings in summer (does not come true from seed).
Mature size
0.8–1.2 m tall and wide.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
8–12 years; may be short-lived in humid winters.
Bloom / fruit season
Late spring to early summer (April–June); large pink flowers with a dark blotch.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −5 to −8 °C; well suited to Tunisian coastal and Sahel zones.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) No fertiliser; a light organic mulch at the base in spring is beneficial for moisture retention.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Wilting despite recent wateringRoot rot from excess moisture.Check drainage; reduce watering frequency immediately.
Fewer flowers each yearNatural ageing of the plant.Replace with a fresh cutting-grown plant every 8–10 years.
Leaf scorch in summerReflected heat from walls or paving in very hot inland areas.Avoid planting against south-facing white walls in inland Tunisia.
