Hottentot fig

Carpobrotus edulis  ·  Family: Aizoaceae
Exterior · Ground-cover
EN Hottentot fig  ·  FR Griffe de sorcière  ·  AR مخلب الساحرة  ·  Carpobrotus edulis
Origin: South Africa (Cape region)
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A tough, salt-tolerant succulent groundcover that rapidly stabilises coastal and rocky slopes with its large pink or yellow daisy flowers. Extremely drought-resistant and perfectly at home along Tunisia's coastline.
Light
Full sun only; shade causes etiolation and no flowers.
Water
Extremely low; survives on rainfall alone on the coast; water only in extreme summer drought.
Soil
Any well-drained, sandy or rocky soil; salt-tolerant; dislikes clay or wet feet.
Temperature
Best above 5 °C; mild frost tolerated briefly; thrives in heat.
Humidity
Excellent salt and sea-spray tolerance; coastal conditions ideal.
Feeding
No fertilizer needed in normal garden conditions.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back spreading stems to control spread; trim after flowering.
Propagation
Very easily from stem cuttings; stick directly in sandy soil and water once.
Mature size
15–30 cm tall, can spread several metres wide.
Growth rate
Fast; can colonise a bank within a season.
Lifespan
Long-lived perennial in frost-free coastal settings.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring (March–June), large showy pink, magenta, or yellow flowers.
Hardiness (min temp)
Marginally frost-hardy; protect from hard frost below −3 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Edible fruit (hence 'edulis'); leaves and fruit non-toxic.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) No fertilizer is needed; this plant thrives on poor soils and feeding encourages invasive, leafy growth at the expense of flowers.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellowing or rotting stems at baseOverwatering or poor drainage.Remove rotted stems; replant healthy cuttings in drier, sandier soil.
No flowers in springToo much shade or plant is too old and woody.Ensure full sun; cut back old stems and allow fresh growth; replace every 3–4 years.
Mealybugs on leaf jointsDry, hot conditions favour sap-sucking pests.Spray with insecticidal soap or diluted neem oil in the cooler morning hours.
Availability: Widely grown along Tunisia's coast and on roadsides; cuttings easy to obtain locally.
