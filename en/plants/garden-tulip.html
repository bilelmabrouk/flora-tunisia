Garden tulip

Tulipa gesneriana  ·  Family: Liliaceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN Garden tulip  ·  FR Tulipe  ·  AR توليب  ·  Tulipa gesneriana
Origin: Central Asia (complex hybrid)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Moderate
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short Tulips succeed in Tunisia only in the cooler north and northwest, where winter chilling is sufficient. Plant pre-chilled bulbs in December for February–April blooms; they are often treated as annuals.
Light
Full sun; essential for upright stems and good flower development.
Water
Moderate during active growth; reduce as foliage yellows; dry dormancy in summer.
Soil
Well-drained, sandy loam; bulbs rot in waterlogged conditions.
Temperature
Requires 6–10 weeks of 4–9 °C (pre-chill bulbs in fridge if winter is mild).
Humidity
Low; dislikes wet, humid conditions.
Feeding
Apply a balanced bulb fertilizer at planting and once as leaves emerge.
Pruning & grooming
Deadhead after flowering; allow foliage to yellow and die completely before removing.
Propagation
From bulb offsets or fresh bulbs each season; seed takes 5–7 years to flower.
Mature size
20–70 cm tall depending on variety.
Growth rate
Fast in cool spring weather.
Lifespan
Perennial bulb; in Tunisia often replanted annually due to heat.
Bloom / fruit season
February to April in northern Tunisia.
Hardiness (min temp)
Bulbs hardy to −10 °C; top growth hardy to about −5 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Toxic if eaten; can cause contact dermatitis (tulip fingers) — wear gloves when handling.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Apply a balanced bulb fertilizer at planting time and a second dose as leaves emerge in spring; avoid high-nitrogen feeds which weaken stems.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Bulbs fail to flowerInsufficient winter chilling — common problem in southern Tunisia.Pre-chill bulbs in the refrigerator (not freezer) for 8 weeks before planting.
Stunted, floppy stemsWarm temperatures during bud development, or planted too shallow.Plant at 2–3 × bulb depth; grow in coolest available spot.
Tulip fire — brown scorch on leaves and flowersBotrytis tulipae fungus in cool wet conditions.Remove and destroy affected parts; avoid crowding; do not replant bulbs from infected soil.
Availability: Bulbs sold in Tunisian garden centres and markets in autumn; pre-chilled bulbs sometimes available; often imported.
