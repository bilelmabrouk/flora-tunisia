Garden pea

Pisum sativum  ·  Family: Fabaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Garden pea  ·  FR Petit pois  ·  AR جلبانة  ·  Pisum sativum
Origin: Near East and Mediterranean basin
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A cool-season Tunisian staple sown from October to February; the sweet pods and peas are harvested in 70–90 days and the roots improve the soil for the next crop.
Light
Full sun is best; tolerates partial shade but pods are fewer.
Water
Even moisture; water regularly but do not waterlog; critical at flowering and pod-fill.
Soil
Well-drained, fertile, slightly alkaline to neutral soil; pH 6–7.5.
Temperature
Cool-season; optimal 10–18 °C; tolerates light frost; stops growing above 25 °C.
Humidity
Moderate; good spacing and airflow reduces mildew and fungal issues.
Feeding
Minimal nitrogen (plant fixes its own); add phosphorus and potassium at planting.
Pruning & grooming
Pinch tips of tall varieties to encourage branching; no pruning for dwarf types.
Propagation
Direct sow 3–5 cm deep, 5–8 cm apart; rows 30–45 cm apart.
Mature size
Dwarf 40–60 cm; tall climbing varieties 1.5–2 m.
Growth rate
Moderate; harvest in 70–90 days.
Lifespan
Annual (cool-season).
Bloom / fruit season
White (or pink in some ornamental types) flowers from late winter to spring.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to about −5 °C; sow in autumn to early winter in Tunisia.
Toxicity & safety
Fully safe; pods and peas are edible raw or cooked.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A low-nitrogen, phosphorus- and potassium-rich fertilizer at sowing supports strong pods without excessive foliage.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Powdery white coating on leaves and podsPowdery mildew (Erysiphe pisi), common towards season end.Improve spacing; spray with dilute bicarbonate; harvest remaining pods.
Plants wilt and collapse despite moist soilRoot rot (Fusarium or Pythium); common in poorly drained or waterlogged soil.Improve drainage; rotate crops; do not replant peas in the same spot for 3–4 years.
Pea moth grubs inside podsPea moth (Cydia nigricana) lays eggs on flowers.Cover plants with fine mesh netting during flowering; rotate planting location.
Availability: Seeds widely available in Tunisian markets and garden centres from September; several local varieties exist.
