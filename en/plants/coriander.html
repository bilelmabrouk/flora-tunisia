Coriander

Coriandrum sativum  ·  Family: Apiaceae
Exterior · Herb / aromatic
EN Coriander  ·  FR Coriandre  ·  AR قزبر  ·  Coriandrum sativum
Origin: Mediterranean & Western Asia
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short An indispensable herb in Tunisian cooking — fresh leaves (قزبر) and dried seeds both used in countless dishes. Best sown in cool seasons to avoid bolting; rewarding and quick from seed.
Uses Both leaf (cilantro) and seed are core Tunisian flavourings for tabil and stews.
Parts used: Leaves and seeds.
Preparation: Leaves fresh; seeds dried and ground into spice mixes (tabil).
Light
Full sun to partial shade; afternoon shade helps slow bolting in Tunisian heat.
Water
Moderate: keep soil consistently moist, especially during germination and in summer.
Soil
Well-drained, moderately fertile, light; loamy soil best.
Temperature
Cool to warm: best 15–25 °C; bolts quickly above 30 °C — grow as a winter/spring crop in Tunisia.
Humidity
Tolerates moderate humidity; ensure air circulation to avoid fungal issues.
Feeding
Light nitrogen feed once at establishment; do not over-feed.
Pruning & grooming
Harvest outer leaves regularly to promote continuous growth; delay flowering as long as possible.
Propagation
Direct sow in place — dislikes transplanting; sow in autumn or early spring in Tunisia.
Mature size
20–60 cm tall when in flower.
Growth rate
Fast — leaves ready in 3–4 weeks.
Lifespan
Annual; self-seeds abundantly.
Bloom / fruit season
White to pale pink umbrella flowers in spring or autumn; leads to seed harvest.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tolerates light frost; damaged by hard frost; thrives in cool Tunisian winters.
Toxicity & safety
Safe; both leaves and seeds widely eaten.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A single light nitrogen-balanced feed at planting is usually enough; coriander in rich garden soil rarely needs extra fertilizer.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Bolting (flowering) prematurelyHigh temperatures or drought stress.Sow in cooler months (autumn–spring in Tunisia); provide afternoon shade; water consistently.
Damping off (seedlings collapse)Overwatering in cool conditions causing fungal stem rot.Improve drainage; reduce watering; sow in batches to ensure continuous supply.
Pale, stunted growthNutrient-poor soil or too much shade.Add compost to soil; move to a sunnier spot.
Availability: Widely sold in Tunisian markets and nurseries; seeds can also be purchased from grocery stalls for sowing.
