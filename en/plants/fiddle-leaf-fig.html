Fiddle-leaf fig

Ficus lyrata  ·  Family: Moraceae
Interior · Foliage
EN Fiddle-leaf fig  ·  FR Ficus lyre  ·  AR فيكوس الكمان  ·  Ficus lyrata
Origin: West Africa (tropical rainforest)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Demanding
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Indoor
In short A dramatic indoor statement tree with huge violin-shaped leaves — stunning in bright Tunisian rooms but demanding: hates draughts, moves, and inconsistent watering.
Light
Needs bright, consistent indirect light — ideally near a large south or east-facing window.
Water
Water when top 3–5 cm dry out; use a consistent schedule. Never let roots sit in water.
Soil
Rich, very well-draining loam with perlite.
Temperature
Prefers 18–27 °C; cold below 15 °C or draughts cause leaf drop.
Humidity
Likes moderate to high humidity; mist or use a humidifier.
Feeding
Balanced liquid fertilizer every 2–3 weeks in spring and summer.
Pruning & grooming
Minimal pruning; shape in early spring. Notch the trunk to encourage branching.
Propagation
Air layering (most reliable); tip cuttings root slowly.
Mature size
1–2.5 m indoors with care.
Growth rate
Slow to moderate.
Lifespan
Long-lived with stable conditions.
Bloom / fruit season
Does not bloom in household conditions.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; must stay above 15 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Mildly toxic — latex sap irritates skin; harmful if eaten by pets or children.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Feed every 2–3 weeks in spring and summer with a balanced liquid fertilizer; consistent nutrition during the growing season helps produce large healthy leaves.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Sudden large leaf dropMoved location, cold draught, inconsistent watering.Stabilise location and watering routine; be patient — it will recover.
Brown spots on leavesBacterial infection (dark irregular spots) or over-watering (light brown edges).Remove affected leaves; improve air flow; adjust watering.
Root-bound, no new growthPot too small for the root system.Repot in spring into a pot 2–3 cm larger with fresh mix.
Availability: Available in larger Tunisian garden centres; less common than other ficus; best bought in spring or summer.
