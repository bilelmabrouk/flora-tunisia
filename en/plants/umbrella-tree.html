Umbrella tree ⇄ also: Exterior

Schefflera actinophylla  ·  Family: Araliaceae
Interior · Foliage

Setting
Indoor & out
In short A fast-growing, architectural indoor tree with glossy umbrella-like leaf clusters — perfect for bright Tunisian living rooms. Tolerates low humidity and grows quickly with minimal care.
Light
Bright indirect light; tolerates some shade. More light = bushier, better growth.
Water
Water when the top 2–3 cm dry out; reduce in winter. Do not let it sit in water.
Soil
Well-draining loam or potting mix; add perlite if drainage is slow.
Temperature
Best at 16–28 °C; protect from frost and cold draughts.
Humidity
Average; tolerates dry air typical of Tunisian interiors.
Feeding
Monthly in spring–summer with a balanced foliage fertilizer.
Pruning & grooming
Pinch growing tips to encourage bushiness; cut leggy stems back hard in spring.
Propagation
Stem cuttings in summer with bottom heat, or air layering.
Mature size
1–3 m indoors; can reach 15 m outdoors in warm climates.
Growth rate
Fast in good light and warmth.
Lifespan
Long-lived; many years as an indoor tree.
Bloom / fruit season
Rarely flowers indoors.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; minimum 10 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Toxic if ingested (calcium oxalates, irritant); keep away from children and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced liquid fertilizer monthly during the growing season keeps the glossy foliage lush; skip winter feeding entirely.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Dropping leaves suddenlyEnvironmental shock: temperature change, draught, or moving the plant.Place in a stable spot away from draughts and A/C vents; keep temperature consistent.
Yellowing leavesOver-watering or root rot.Check drainage, reduce watering, and let soil dry out more between waterings.
Sticky leaves with small insectsScale insects or mealybugs.Wipe leaves with a damp cloth; use diluted neem oil or insecticidal soap spray.
Availability: Widely available in Tunisian plant shops and garden centres.
