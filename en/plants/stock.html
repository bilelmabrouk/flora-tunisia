Stock

Matthiola incana  ·  Family: Brassicaceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN Stock  ·  FR Giroflée  ·  AR منثور  ·  Matthiola incana
Origin: Mediterranean region
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short One of the most intensely fragrant bedding plants — stock fills Tunisian spring evenings with a spicy clove scent. Sow in autumn for February–May blooms in pink, purple, red or white.
Light
Full sun; tolerates light afternoon shade.
Water
Moderate; keep evenly moist; avoid waterlogging.
Soil
Well-drained, fertile, slightly alkaline to neutral soil.
Temperature
Cool-season; best 10–18 °C; sow in autumn (Sept–Nov in Tunisia) for spring bloom.
Humidity
Average; good airflow reduces disease.
Feeding
Light balanced fertilizer every 3–4 weeks during growth.
Pruning & grooming
Deadhead to prolong flowering; remove whole spike when exhausted.
Propagation
From seed; sow in place or in trays in autumn for spring planting.
Mature size
30–60 cm tall; dwarf and tall varieties available.
Growth rate
Moderate in cool months; fast-flowering.
Lifespan
Annual or biennial.
Bloom / fruit season
February to May in Tunisia.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy annual; tolerates light frost to about −5 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A light balanced fertilizer every 3–4 weeks during cool-season growth is sufficient; heavy feeding promotes leafy growth at the expense of flowers.  Find fertilizer →

Downy mildew on leavesCool, humid, poorly ventilated conditions.Space plants; avoid wetting foliage; remove affected leaves.
Flowering very early, plants shortSown too late or in warm weather — plant rushes to flower.Sow earlier in autumn (September–October); ensure cool germination conditions.
Availability: Seed packets and seedlings available in Tunisian garden centres in autumn; popular fragrant bedding plant.
