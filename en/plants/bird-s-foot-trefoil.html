Bird's-foot trefoil

Lotus corniculatus  ·  Family: Fabaceae
Exterior · Ground-cover
EN Bird's-foot trefoil  ·  FR Lotier corniculé  ·  AR قرن الغزال  ·  Lotus corniculatus
Origin: Europe and North Africa (native in Tunisia)
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A cheerful native Tunisian wildflower with bright yellow pea flowers that naturalises easily in dry meadows and sunny banks. Drought-tolerant, nitrogen-fixing, and a great nectar source — perfect for a low-maintenance wild garden.
Light
Full sun; flowers poorly in shade.
Water
Very low once established; native to Tunisian dry meadows — survives on rainfall alone.
Soil
Poor, well-drained, sandy or loamy; tolerates clay and alkaline soils; fixes nitrogen.
Temperature
Very hardy; tolerates frost below −20 °C and summer heat above 40 °C.
Humidity
Adapts to coastal and dry inland conditions.
Feeding
No feeding needed; extra nitrogen suppresses this nitrogen-fixing legume.
Pruning & grooming
Mow or cut back after seeding to keep tidy and encourage dense regrowth.
Propagation
By seed (scarify and sow in autumn or spring); self-seeds readily.
Mature size
10–40 cm tall, spreading indefinitely if left.
Growth rate
Moderate; spreads by seed and short rhizomes.
Lifespan
Short-lived perennial (3–5 years); self-seeds to maintain cover.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring to late summer (April–August), masses of bright yellow pea-flowers.
Hardiness (min temp)
Extremely hardy; very frost and drought tolerant.
Toxicity & safety
Generally safe; avoid eating large amounts of raw plant material.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) No fertilizer needed; being a nitrogen-fixing legume it feeds itself and nearby plants — adding nitrogen suppresses its own growth and flowering.  Find fertilizer →

Aphids on stems and flowersCommon spring pest on legumes.Blast off with water; encourage natural predators such as ladybirds.
Plant dying out in patchesNatural short lifespan; end of plant cycle.Allow self-seeding; scatter fresh seed on bare patches in autumn.
