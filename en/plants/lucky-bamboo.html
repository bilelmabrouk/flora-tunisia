"Lucky bamboo" (a Dracaena) ⇄ also: Interior

Dracaena sanderiana  ·  Family: Asparagaceae
Exterior · Bamboo
EN "Lucky bamboo" (a Dracaena)  ·  FR Bambou de la chance  ·  AR خيزران الحظ  ·  Dracaena sanderiana
Origin: Central Africa (Cameroon to Congo)
Light
Shade / indirect
Water
Moderate
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Indoor & out
In short Not a true bamboo but a compact African Dracaena sold throughout Tunisia as a good-luck houseplant — grown in water or soil in bright indirect light, needing minimal care.
Light
Bright indirect light; avoid direct sun which scorches the leaves. Tolerates low light.
Water
In water: change weekly; use non-chlorinated water. In soil: water when top 2 cm dry.
Soil
If in soil: light, well-drained mix; often grown in pebbles and water.
Temperature
15–32 °C; protect from cold drafts below 10 °C.
Humidity
Average indoor humidity; benefits from occasional misting in dry conditions.
Feeding
Very light: one or two drops of dilute liquid fertilizer in the water every 2–3 months.
Pruning & grooming
Trim yellowed leaves; stems can be cut to shape — new growth emerges from cut points.
Propagation
Stem cuttings placed in water root easily.
Mature size
30–100 cm depending on training style.
Growth rate
Slow to moderate.
Lifespan
Several years indoors with proper care.
Bloom / fruit season
Rarely blooms indoors.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; strictly indoor or warm sheltered outdoor use in Tunisia.
Toxicity & safety
Toxic to cats and dogs (saponins); keep out of reach of pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) For water-grown plants, add just one or two drops of a dilute balanced liquid fertilizer every two to three months — more causes root burn.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellow leavesOver-fertilizing, fluoride in tap water, or too much direct sun.Use non-chlorinated water; reduce feeding; move out of direct sun.
Brown leaf tipsChlorine or fluoride in water, or low humidity.Leave tap water overnight before use; mist lightly.
Slimy roots or foul-smelling waterBacterial growth in stagnant water.Change water completely; clean the vase; rinse roots gently.
Pale, stretched growth in low lightInsufficient light causing etiolation.Move to a brighter spot with indirect light.
Availability: Very commonly sold in Tunisian garden centres, florists, and markets; widely available as a decorative houseplant.
