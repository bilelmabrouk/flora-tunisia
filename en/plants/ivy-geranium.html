Ivy geranium

Pelargonium peltatum  ·  Family: Geraniaceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN Ivy geranium  ·  FR Géranium lierre  ·  AR جيرانيوم لبلابي  ·  Pelargonium peltatum
Origin: South Africa (coastal Cape region)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short The trailing ivy-leaved geranium that cascades magnificently from Tunisian balcony boxes and hanging baskets, flowering prolifically in coastal heat from spring to autumn.
Light
Full sun to bright partial shade; best in 5–6 hours direct sun; tolerates coastal heat well.
Water
Moderate: allow soil to dry slightly between waterings; very drought-tolerant; avoid waterlogging.
Soil
Well-drained light potting mix; slightly poor soil promotes flowering over foliage.
Temperature
Ideal 15–28 °C; tolerates brief heat spikes up to 38 °C in coastal areas; frost-tender.
Humidity
Low to average; excellent for dry coastal Tunisian conditions.
Feeding
Flowering-plant fertilizer every 2 weeks in spring–summer; reduce in winter.
Pruning & grooming
Pinch tips in early spring to promote branching; deadhead spent flowers.
Propagation
From stem cuttings (8–10 cm) in spring or late summer; easy to root.
Mature size
Trailing stems to 60–90 cm; spreads 40–60 cm wide.
Growth rate
Moderate to fast.
Lifespan
Perennial in frost-free zones; often grown as an annual.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring through autumn; peak in late spring–early summer.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; bring indoors or protect below 2 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Mildly toxic to cats and dogs if ingested.

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Botrytis grey mould on flowersWet conditions and poor air circulation.Improve spacing; remove affected flowers; water at the base only.
Stunted, distorted new growthAphid infestation on new shoots.Knock aphids off with water; use insecticidal soap if severe; avoid excess nitrogen fertilizer.
Trailing stems becoming bare and leggyInsufficient light or lack of pruning over time.Pinch back stems regularly to encourage bushiness; move to a brighter spot.
Yellowing lower leaves and weak growthOverwatering or root rot in poorly draining soil.Let soil dry between waterings and repot in well-draining mix if roots are affected.
Availability: A very popular trailing balcony plant in Tunisia, sold every spring in nurseries, souks, and supermarket garden sections, especially for hanging pots.
