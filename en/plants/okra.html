Okra

Abelmoschus esculentus  ·  Family: Malvaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Okra  ·  FR Gombo  ·  AR ملوخية / بامية  ·  Abelmoschus esculentus
Origin: West Africa or Ethiopia (exact origin debated)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A heat-loving African vegetable perfectly suited to Tunisian summers — okra is drought-tolerant, easy to grow, and produces beautiful hibiscus-like flowers followed by edible pods for Tunisian stews and tagines.
Light
Full sun — 8+ hours; one of the most sun-loving vegetables.
Water
Low to moderate; drought-tolerant once established; water deeply but allow soil to partially dry between waterings.
Soil
Well-drained, loose soil; tolerates poor soils but produces best in fertile ground.
Temperature
Very heat-loving; best at 24–35 °C; does not germinate well below 18 °C.
Humidity
Tolerates dry heat; high humidity can encourage stem rot.
Feeding
Light feeding; excess nitrogen promotes too much foliage at the expense of pods.
Pruning & grooming
Remove older lower leaves as plants grow; harvest pods regularly to encourage continued production.
Propagation
Direct sow after the last cold spell; soak seeds overnight before planting to improve germination.
Mature size
1–2 m tall; upright habit.
Growth rate
Fast once the weather is warm.
Lifespan
Annual.
Bloom / fruit season
Large creamy-yellow hibiscus-like flowers in summer; pods follow immediately.
Hardiness (min temp)
Very frost-tender; sow in Tunisia from April (south) to May (north).
Toxicity & safety
Fully safe; all parts edible.

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Pods become tough and fibrousHarvested too late; pods over-mature quickly in Tunisian summer heat.Harvest daily when pods are 7–10 cm long for best tenderness.
Poor germinationCold soil or hard seed coat.Sow only when soil is above 18 °C; soak seeds in water overnight before planting.
Aphids and spider mites in dry summerHot, dry conditions favour both pests.Spray with neem-oil solution or diluted soap; water regularly to reduce heat stress on plants.
Availability: Seeds commonly available in Tunisian markets and garden centres in spring; a traditional crop grown throughout the country.
