Mountain clematis

Clematis montana  ·  Family: Ranunculaceae
Exterior · Climber
EN Mountain clematis  ·  FR Clématite des montagnes  ·  AR كليماتيس جبلي  ·  Clematis montana
Origin: Himalayas and western China
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short A spectacularly floriferous spring climber smothered in small pink or white flowers — best suited to the cooler north-west highlands of Tunisia where it can enjoy cold winters and good rainfall.
Light
Full sun to part shade; tolerates more shade than most clematis.
Water
Moderate; needs regular watering; does not tolerate prolonged drought.
Soil
Well-drained, fertile, humus-rich, preferably alkaline.
Temperature
Very hardy to −20 °C; needs cold winters for best bloom.
Humidity
Prefers moderate to high humidity; not for arid zones.
Feeding
Monthly spring–summer with a balanced fertilizer.
Pruning & grooming
Group 1 — prune only to tidy immediately after flowering; do not cut into old wood.
Propagation
Semi-ripe cuttings in summer or layering.
Mature size
Can reach 6–12 m; very vigorous.
Growth rate
Very fast once established.
Lifespan
Long-lived perennial climber.
Bloom / fruit season
April–June; masses of small 4-petalled flowers, typically pink or white.
Hardiness (min temp)
Very hardy to −20 °C.
Toxicity & safety
All parts toxic if ingested; sap irritates skin.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced fertilizer monthly from late winter to early summer encourages maximum flowering; do not feed in autumn or winter.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Wilting despite wateringClematis wilt fungus or overwatered roots.Cut back hard below soil; improve drainage; new shoots usually emerge.
Failure to bloom after hard pruneGroup 1 clematis flowers on previous year's wood; hard pruning removes all flower buds.For group 1, only tidy; never cut back hard. Expect no flowers for a season if you did.
Availability: Available in specialist nurseries; most suitable for northern Tunisia highlands.
