Mexican bush sage

Salvia leucantha  ·  Family: Lamiaceae
Exterior · Shrub
EN Mexican bush sage  ·  FR Sauge du Mexique  ·  AR مريمية مكسيكية  ·  Salvia leucantha
Origin: Mexico and Central America (highland regions)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A dramatic late-summer showpiece with long velvety purple and white flower spikes that light up Tunisian gardens from summer's end through winter — drought-tough, pollinator-friendly, and very easy.
Light
Full sun; flowering is dramatically reduced in shade.
Water
Low to moderate: drought-tolerant once established; occasional deep watering in summer.
Soil
Well-drained, poor to moderately fertile; excellent in sandy or rocky soils.
Temperature
Hardy to about −5 °C; dies back to the ground in frost but regrows in spring.
Humidity
Prefers low humidity; good air flow prevents fungal issues.
Feeding
Minimal; one light application in spring is more than enough.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back to 20–30 cm in late winter to encourage vigorous new growth and more flower spikes.
Propagation
From semi-hardwood cuttings in late summer, or by division in spring.
Mature size
1–1.5 m tall and wide.
Growth rate
Fast.
Lifespan
Short-lived perennial: 3–5 years; easily replaced from cuttings.
Bloom / fruit season
Late summer through winter; long velvety purple and white spikes.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender: dies back at 0 °C but reshoots from the crown; not for region 2 winters.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; aromatic foliage safe for children and pets.

Frost-killed stemsCold below −5 °C killing above-ground growth.Cut back dead stems in spring; the crown usually survives and regrows.
Availability: Available in Tunisian garden centres, especially those specialising in Mediterranean and drought-tolerant plants.
