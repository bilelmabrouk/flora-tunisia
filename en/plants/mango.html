Mango

Mangifera indica  ·  Family: Anacardiaceae
Trees · Fruit / nut
EN Mango  ·  FR Manguier  ·  AR مانڨو  ·  Mangifera indica
Origin: South and South-East Asia (India, Myanmar, Andaman Islands)
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Demanding
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A tropical fruit tree that can succeed only in Tunisia's warmest frost-free zones — the southern coast and Saharan oases. Demanding in heat and care but rewarding with its iconic fruit.
Light
Full, unobstructed sun all day; even partial shade reduces yield significantly.
Water
Moderate during growth; reduce watering during the dry winter rest before flowering to encourage blooming.
Soil
Deep, well-drained, loamy; tolerates sandy soils; cannot tolerate waterlogging.
Temperature
Requires year-round warmth above 15 °C; any frost kills young trees; mature trees damaged by frost.
Humidity
Prefers low humidity, especially when flowering; high humidity promotes anthracnose.
Feeding
Regular feeding with a tropical fruit fertilizer during growth; reduce at pre-flowering dry period.
Pruning & grooming
Prune after harvest to shape and thin the canopy; remove water shoots and crossed branches.
Propagation
By grafting onto mango seedling rootstock (ensures known variety); seed grown trees take 6–10 years.
Mature size
8–20 m in the tropics; in Tunisia often kept to 4–6 m with pruning.
Growth rate
Moderate to fast; grafted trees fruit from year 3–5.
Lifespan
Long-lived — 100–200 years in ideal conditions.
Bloom / fruit season
Flower panicles appear in December–March during a dry cool period; fruit ripens in summer.
Hardiness (min temp)
Very frost-tender; even −1 °C can kill flowers and young fruit; suitable only for regions 5 and 6.
Toxicity & safety
Fruit is safe and nutritious; sap and skin of unripe fruit can cause allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive people.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Use a tropical fruit-tree fertilizer (balanced NPK plus trace elements) during growth flushes; withhold fertilizer during the pre-flowering dry period to encourage blooming.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Black spots on fruit and leaf tips (anthracnose)Anthracnose fungus (Colletotrichum gloeosporioides) in humid conditions.Apply copper-based fungicide preventatively; ensure good air movement in canopy; avoid wetting foliage.
Gummy, sticky secretions on young shootsMango hopper (Idioscopus spp.) infestation — sucking insects.Spray with insecticidal soap or neem oil at bud burst and early flower stage.
Flower panicles fail to open fully, remain smallInsufficient dry rest period before flowering, or powdery mildew on flowers.Ensure 6–8 weeks without irrigation before flowering period; apply sulphur dust if mildew present.
Availability: Uncommon in Tunisian nurseries; available mainly in the south near Gabès and Djerba.
