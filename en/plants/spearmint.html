Spearmint

Mentha spicata  ·  Family: Lamiaceae
Exterior · Herb / aromatic
EN Spearmint  ·  FR Menthe verte  ·  AR نعناع  ·  Mentha spicata
Origin: Mediterranean and western Asia
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Moderate
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short Tunisia's everyday mint — used fresh in tea, cooking, and salads. Fast-growing, easy to contain in a pot, and happy in sun or partial shade throughout the country.
Uses A hugely popular kitchen and tea herb; cooling, digestive and refreshing.
Parts used: Leaves and sprigs.
Preparation: Fresh in tea (Tunisian mint tea), salads and cooking; also dried.
Light
Full sun to partial shade; tolerates afternoon shade in hot summers.
Water
Moderate: keep soil consistently moist; water every 1–2 days in peak summer heat.
Soil
Rich, moist, well-drained loam with compost; tolerates most garden soils.
Temperature
Best at 15–25 °C; dies back in frost but roots survive to −15 °C; regrows in spring.
Humidity
Moderate to high; benefits from regular watering in dry Tunisian summers.
Feeding
A light nitrogen-rich fertilizer once a month during the growing season.
Pruning & grooming
Harvest regularly to keep bushy; cut flower stems to extend leaf production.
Propagation
Very easy — by stem cuttings in water or soil, or by division of roots.
Mature size
30–60 cm tall; spreads vigorously by runners.
Growth rate
Fast — can become invasive; best grown in a container.
Lifespan
Perennial; regrows each spring from roots.
Bloom / fruit season
Small purple-pink flower spikes in summer; pinch off to keep leafy.
Hardiness (min temp)
Roots are cold-hardy to −15 °C; top growth dies in frost and regrows.
Toxicity & safety
Safe for consumption; excessive internal use in medicinal doses can be harmful.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A nitrogen-rich herb fertilizer once a month in the growing season produces lush, flavourful leaves; avoid overfeeding which dilutes the aromatic oils.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Mint rust (orange pustules on leaves)Puccinia menthae fungal rust disease.Remove affected leaves; improve airflow; do not compost infected material.
Wilting despite moist soilMint verticillium wilt or root rot.Remove and destroy affected plants; do not replant mint in the same spot for several years.
Invasive spreading beyond its areaVigorous underground runners (stolons).Grow in a buried pot or raised bed; trim runners regularly at the edges.
Availability: Extremely common throughout Tunisia — sold in every market and garden centre; often grown from divisions shared between neighbours.
