Winter squash / pumpkin

Cucurbita maxima  ·  Family: Cucurbitaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Winter squash / pumpkin  ·  FR Potiron  ·  AR قرع  ·  Cucurbita maxima
Origin: South America (Andes region)
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A fast-growing sprawling vine producing large long-storing squashes that are a Tunisian kitchen staple. Adaptable to every region of the country; needs space, sun, and consistent water.
Light
Full sun; at least 6-8 hours daily.
Water
Moderate; water deeply and regularly, especially during fruit development; avoid wetting leaves.
Soil
Rich, well-drained loam with plenty of compost; pH 6-7.
Temperature
Warm-season crop; sow after soil warms above 15 C; grows well at 20-35 C.
Humidity
Tolerates moderate humidity; avoid overhead watering to limit mildew.
Feeding
Start with nitrogen-rich feed; switch to potassium-phosphorus blend at flowering.
Pruning & grooming
Limit to 2-3 main vines; remove extra shoots to channel energy into fewer larger fruits.
Propagation
By seed sown directly or started under cover 2-3 weeks early.
Mature size
Vines 3-6 m; fruits range from 2 kg to over 20 kg.
Growth rate
Fast; 90-120 days from seed to harvest.
Lifespan
Annual.
Bloom / fruit season
Yellow flowers May-July; separate male and female flowers on the same plant.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; plant out only after all cold risk passes.
Toxicity & safety
Fully edible; seeds, flesh, and flowers all used in cooking.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Mix well-rotted compost deeply into the planting hole, then feed with a nitrogen-rich fertilizer early in the season and switch to a potassium-rich blend at flowering to develop sweet firm fruits.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
White powder on leaves and stemsPowdery mildew, very common on cucurbits in late summer.Improve air circulation; spray with diluted bicarbonate; remove worst-affected leaves.
Fruits rot at the blossom endBlossom-end rot due to calcium deficiency caused by irregular watering.Water consistently; mulch to retain moisture; ensure soil pH is not too low.
Vine wilts suddenlySquash vine borer or root rot from waterlogging.Check stem base for entry holes (borer) or soggy soil (rot); improve drainage; remove affected plants.
Availability: Widely grown across all Tunisian regions; seeds easily available before the spring planting season.
