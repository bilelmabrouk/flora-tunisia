Lantana

Lantana camara  ·  Family: Verbenaceae
Exterior · Shrub
EN Lantana  ·  FR Lantanier  ·  AR لانتانا  ·  Lantana camara
Origin: Tropical America (Central and South America, Caribbean)
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short A sun-loving, drought-proof shrub that flowers non-stop in mixed yellow, orange, red, and pink clusters from spring until frost — one of the easiest and most colourful shrubs for sunny Tunisian gardens and roadside plantings.
Light
Full sun; essential — shade greatly reduces flowering.
Water
Very drought-tolerant once established; water only in the first season.
Soil
Any well-drained soil; tolerates poor, sandy, or stony soils.
Temperature
Frost-tender; best above 5 °C; may die back in cold winters but resprouts if roots survive.
Humidity
Tolerates dry and coastal conditions well.
Feeding
Light balanced fertiliser in spring; avoid heavy feeding which reduces flowers.
Pruning & grooming
Prune hard in late winter to 20–30 cm to stimulate compact new growth; deadhead regularly.
Propagation
From semi-ripe cuttings in summer.
Mature size
1–2 m tall and wide; can spread more in ideal conditions.
Growth rate
Fast.
Lifespan
Perennial in frost-free zones; 10–20 years.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring to first cold snap (April–November); continuous multicoloured clusters; individual florets change colour as they age.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; best in coastal and southern Tunisia; avoid the Tell mountains.
Toxicity & safety
Toxic: unripe berries and leaves are harmful — keep away from children and livestock.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A light balanced fertiliser in spring is enough; excessive feeding — especially nitrogen — produces lush leaves but far fewer flowers.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Lace bug damage — silvery stippling on leavesLantana lace bug (Teleonemia scrupulosa) feeding on leaf undersides.Spray undersides of leaves with insecticidal soap or horticultural oil.
Whitefly infestationWhitefly feeding on underside of leaves in warm dry conditions.Spray with insecticidal soap; introduce natural predators if available.
Frost diebackCold temperatures below 5 °C killing above-ground growth.Cut back dead material in spring; roots usually survive to resprout.
Reduced flowering in shadeInsufficient sunlight.Move to full sun; lantana needs at least 6 hours of direct sun daily.
