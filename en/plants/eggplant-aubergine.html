Eggplant / aubergine

Solanum melongena  ·  Family: Solanaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Eggplant / aubergine  ·  FR Aubergine  ·  AR بَاذنجان  ·  Solanum melongena
Origin: South and Southeast Asia (India to Southeast Asia)
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A beloved Tunisian kitchen vegetable — aubergines love the long hot summer, producing glossy purple fruit abundantly from July onwards; essential for makloub, moussaka, and salads.
Light
Full sun — 8 hours or more; one of the most heat-loving vegetables.
Water
Moderate and consistent; inconsistent watering leads to bitter fruit and shedding.
Soil
Rich, well-drained, slightly acidic (pH 5.8–6.5); add compost before planting.
Temperature
Thrives at 22–35 °C; fruit set poor below 18 °C or above 35 °C for extended periods.
Humidity
Moderate; tolerates the dry heat of Tunisian summers well.
Feeding
Balanced feed at transplant; high-potassium formula during fruiting for large, glossy fruit.
Pruning & grooming
Remove yellowing lower leaves; some gardeners limit fruit to 4–6 per plant for larger size.
Propagation
From seed indoors 8–10 weeks before transplanting; needs warm soil (18 °C+) to germinate.
Mature size
60–100 cm tall; bushy.
Growth rate
Moderate; fastest in warm, sunny conditions.
Lifespan
Annual; can be short-lived perennial in frost-free conditions.
Bloom / fruit season
Purple star-shaped flowers spring to summer; fruit ready 70–90 days after transplant.
Hardiness (min temp)
Very frost-tender; plant in Tunisia from April (south) to May (north).
Toxicity & safety
Fruit fully safe when cooked; raw fruit contains small amounts of solanine — safe in normal quantities.

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Fruit turning brown and bitterOver-mature fruit or irregular watering.Harvest regularly before fruit over-matures; water consistently.
Flea beetles making small holes in leavesCommon Tunisian summer pest on young plants.Cover young plants with row covers; apply kaolin clay dust as a deterrent.
Verticillium wilt — sudden wiltingSoilborne fungal pathogen, common after repeated solanaceae crops.Rotate crops — do not plant aubergines, tomatoes, or peppers in the same bed two years in a row.
Availability: Seedlings and seeds very widely available in Tunisian markets and garden centres from March to May; one of the most grown vegetables in Tunisian home gardens.
