Broad bean / fava

Vicia faba  ·  Family: Fabaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Broad bean / fava  ·  FR Fève  ·  AR فول  ·  Vicia faba
Origin: Near East and North Africa
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A Tunisian winter staple sown from October to January; its white-and-black flowers fix nitrogen in the soil and the fat beans are a kitchen cornerstone from lablabi to mashed ful.
Light
Full sun; tolerates light shade in hot climates but yields less.
Water
Moderate; water regularly but avoid waterlogging, especially in heavy soils.
Soil
Fertile, well-drained loam to clay-loam; pH 6–7.5; tolerates heavier soils than most vegetables.
Temperature
Cool-season crop; best at 10–20 °C; survives light frost; suffers above 25 °C.
Humidity
Average; good airflow reduces chocolate spot and botrytis.
Feeding
Light nitrogen at planting only; the plant fixes its own nitrogen — excess N reduces yield.
Pruning & grooming
Pinch growing tips once plants are 30 cm tall to deter blackfly and improve yield.
Propagation
Direct sow seeds 4–5 cm deep, 20 cm apart, in rows 45 cm apart.
Mature size
60–120 cm tall depending on variety.
Growth rate
Moderate; harvest-ready 90–130 days from sowing.
Lifespan
Annual (cool-season).
Bloom / fruit season
White flowers with a dark spot in late winter to early spring.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to about −5 °C; best sown in autumn or early winter in Tunisia.
Toxicity & safety
Safe for most people; raw or large amounts may trigger favism in G6PD-deficient individuals.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Apply a phosphorus- and potassium-rich fertilizer at planting; avoid excess nitrogen as the plant fixes its own from the air.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Black clusters of insects on shoot tipsBlack bean aphid (Aphis fabae).Pinch and remove affected tips; encourage ladybirds; spray with dilute soap solution.
Brown or chocolate-coloured spots on leaves and podsChocolate spot fungal disease (Botrytis fabae), favoured by damp conditions.Improve airflow; avoid overhead watering; remove infected material.
Plants yellow and stuntedBean leaf roll virus or root damage from waterlogged soil.Remove infected plants; ensure good drainage; rotate crops yearly.
Availability: Seeds and seedlings widely available at Tunisian markets and garden centres in autumn.
