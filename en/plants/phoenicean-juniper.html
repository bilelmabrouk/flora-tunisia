Phoenicean juniper

Juniperus phoenicea  ·  Family: Cupressaceae
Trees · Olive / native
EN Phoenicean juniper  ·  FR Genévrier de Phénicie  ·  AR عرعر فينيقي  ·  Juniperus phoenicea
Origin: Mediterranean basin native
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A tough, drought-tolerant evergreen juniper found throughout Tunisia's garrigues and coastal cliffs. Extremely low-maintenance and long-lived, it is ideal for dry, rocky Tunisian landscapes.
Light
Full sun; thrives on exposed rocky hillsides.
Water
Very low; extremely drought-tolerant; no regular watering once established.
Soil
Poor, rocky, calcareous, or sandy soils; must be well-drained.
Temperature
Hardy to −15 °C; handles heat and coastal conditions.
Humidity
Tolerates both dry and coastal humid conditions.
Feeding
Not needed; occasionally a light mineral fertilizer for container plants.
Pruning & grooming
Minimal; remove dead wood; do not cut into bare old wood.
Propagation
From seed (stratify 3 months cold); or semi-ripe cuttings.
Mature size
2–8 m, often multi-stemmed or shrubby.
Growth rate
Slow.
Lifespan
Centuries.
Bloom / fruit season
Small female cones ripen to red berries in 2 years; not showy.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −15 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Berries mildly toxic if consumed in quantity; generally considered safe in garden settings.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Established plants need no feeding at all; young container plants benefit from a very light mineral fertilizer in spring.  Find fertilizer →
