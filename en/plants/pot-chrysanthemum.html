Pot chrysanthemum ⇄ also: Exterior

Chrysanthemum × morifolium  ·  Family: Asteraceae
Interior · Flowering
EN Pot chrysanthemum  ·  FR Chrysanthème  ·  AR أقحوان  ·  Chrysanthemum × morifolium
Origin: Asia (ancient Chinese and Japanese hybrid)
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Indoor & out
In short The quintessential autumn colour plant — masses of daisy-like blooms in orange, red, yellow, pink, or white for weeks. A classic seasonal purchase for Tunisian homes and balconies in autumn.
Light
Full sun to bright light; minimum 6 hours of sun a day for best flowering.
Water
Keep soil evenly moist; water regularly, especially in warm weather. Do not let dry out.
Soil
Rich, well-draining potting mix; avoid compacted, heavy soils.
Temperature
Best at 10–20 °C; cooler temperatures prolong flowering. Heat above 25 °C shortens bloom.
Humidity
Average; good air circulation prevents fungal diseases.
Feeding
Weekly with a flower fertilizer while in bud and bloom; stop once flowers finish.
Pruning & grooming
Remove faded flowers to extend the display. After flowering, cut back to about 10 cm.
Propagation
Stem tip cuttings in spring; division in spring.
Mature size
25–45 cm tall as a pot plant.
Growth rate
Fast during the growing season.
Lifespan
Often treated as a seasonal plant; can be kept and rebloomed with correct care.
Bloom / fruit season
Autumn (September–November) in Tunisia; day-length sensitive.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tolerates light frost to about −5 °C when established outdoors.
Toxicity & safety
Mildly toxic if ingested; sap causes skin irritation in some people — keep from pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A high-phosphorus flower fertilizer weekly during the bud and bloom stage maintains colour and extends the display; stop after all flowers have faded.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Wilting despite moist soilRoot rot or very warm temperatures.Check roots; move to a cooler spot; improve drainage.
Grey mould on flowers and leavesBotrytis (grey mould) from high humidity and poor air flow.Improve air circulation; remove affected parts; avoid wetting foliage.
Aphids on new shoots and budsCommon on chrysanthemums, especially warm and dry spells.Spray with insecticidal soap or neem oil; blast with water spray first.
Availability: Very widely available in autumn in Tunisian plant shops, florists, and markets.
