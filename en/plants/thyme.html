Thyme

Thymus vulgaris  ·  Family: Lamiaceae
Exterior · Herb / aromatic
EN Thyme  ·  FR Thym  ·  AR زعتر  ·  Thymus vulgaris
Origin: Western Mediterranean
Light
Full sun
Water

Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A compact, drought-tough Mediterranean sub-shrub that thrives in all Tunisian climates. An essential kitchen herb, it needs almost no care once established — just sun and well-drained soil.
Light
Full sun essential — 6+ hours; more sun strengthens flavour and aroma.
Water
Low: drought-tolerant; water only when soil is fully dry; excess water kills it.
Soil
Poor to moderately fertile, very well-drained; sandy or stony soils are ideal.
Temperature
Wide tolerance: grows from 5 °C to 35 °C; handles light frost well.
Humidity
Prefers dry conditions; high humidity can cause stem rot — ensure air circulation.
Feeding
Minimal: a small amount of balanced fertilizer in spring; do not over-feed.
Pruning & grooming
Clip lightly after flowering to keep compact; avoid cutting into old wood.
Propagation
Easy from cuttings, division, or seed.
Mature size
20–40 cm tall; spreading mound.
Growth rate
Slow to moderate.
Lifespan
Short-lived perennial: 3–5 years; renew from cuttings.
Bloom / fruit season
Tiny pink to purple flowers April–June; leave some for pollinators.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −10 °C; fully frost-tolerant across Tunisia.
Toxicity & safety
Safe; widely used in cooking and herbal teas.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Thyme thrives in poor soil; if needed, a small dose of balanced fertilizer in early spring is sufficient — avoid nitrogen-rich feeds.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Stems rotting at baseOverwatering or heavy, poorly draining soil.Allow soil to dry fully between waterings; improve drainage with sand or grit.
Leggy, sprawling growthInsufficient light or no pruning.Move to full sun; trim back by one-third after each bloom.
Loss of flavour and scentOld plant past its prime or over-fertilized.Renew from cuttings; reduce fertilizer to near zero.
Availability: Widely grown and sold throughout Tunisia; common in herb stalls, markets, and nurseries.
