Zonal geranium ⇄ also: Interior

Pelargonium × hortorum  ·  Family: Geraniaceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN Zonal geranium  ·  FR Géranium  ·  AR جيرانيوم  ·  Pelargonium × hortorum
Origin: South Africa (garden hybrid)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Indoor & out
In short Tunisia's most popular balcony and bedding plant — vivid red, pink, or white flower clusters almost year-round, tolerates heat, drought, and coastal wind with minimal care.
Light
Full sun to bright partial shade; 5+ hours of sun daily ensures strong blooming.
Water
Moderate: allow top 2–3 cm to dry between waterings; very drought-tolerant; avoid waterlogging.
Soil
Well-drained loam or potting mix; not too rich; pH 5.8–6.5.
Temperature
Ideal 15–27 °C; tolerates up to 35 °C in coastal areas; frost-tender below −2 °C.
Humidity
Low to average; good airflow prevents fungal disease.
Feeding
Flowering-plant fertilizer every 2 weeks during active growth; reduce or stop in winter.
Pruning & grooming
Deadhead regularly; cut back by one-third in autumn to keep compact.
Propagation
Easy from stem cuttings (10 cm) in spring or late summer; root in well-drained mix.
Mature size
30–60 cm tall and wide.
Growth rate
Moderate to fast.
Lifespan
Perennial; treat as annual in regions with hard frost.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring through autumn; can bloom almost year-round in coastal Tunisia.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tolerates light frost to about −2 °C; protect in cooler inland areas.
Toxicity & safety
Mildly toxic to dogs and cats if ingested; keep away from pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A flowering-plant fertilizer with moderate nitrogen and higher phosphorus–potassium every two weeks in spring and summer maintains continuous blooming; stop feeding in winter.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellow leaves with green veinsIron or magnesium deficiency, or overwatering.Use a balanced fertilizer; check drainage and reduce watering.
Grey fuzzy mould on stems and leavesBotrytis (grey mould) from poor ventilation and humidity.Remove affected parts; space plants more; water at the base and avoid wetting leaves.
Sticky leaves with clusters of tiny green insectsAphid infestation on new growth.Spray off with water; use insecticidal soap for heavy infestations.
Soft, blackened stem at soil levelStem or crown rot from overwatering and poor drainage.Reduce watering, improve drainage, and remove badly affected plants to stop spread.
Availability: One of the most iconic and widely sold balcony and terrace plants in Tunisia, available everywhere from small souk stalls to large garden centres.
