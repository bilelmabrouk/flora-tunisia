Butterfly bush

Buddleja davidii  ·  Family: Scrophulariaceae
Exterior · Shrub
EN Butterfly bush  ·  FR Arbre aux papillons  ·  AR شجرة الفراشات  ·  Buddleja davidii
Origin: Central & south-western China
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A fast-growing shrub with long fragrant flower spikes in purple, white, or pink that attract butterflies and bees throughout summer. Easy, drought-tolerant, and great for Tunisian northern and inland gardens.
Light
Full sun; needs strong sun for dense flowering.
Water
Moderate; water when the top 3–4 cm dry out; established plants are drought-tolerant.
Soil
Well-drained soil of any fertility; tolerates poor, chalky or stony soils.
Temperature
Tolerates frost to −15 °C and heat to 38 °C; suits most of Tunisia.
Humidity
Average; tolerates dry summer conditions well.
Feeding
Light balanced fertilizer in early spring; avoid excess nitrogen.
Pruning & grooming
Hard prune to 30–50 cm in late winter (Feb–Mar) — this is essential for large flowers.
Propagation
Softwood cuttings in spring or hardwood cuttings in autumn.
Mature size
2–4 m if not pruned; 1–2 m with annual hard pruning.
Growth rate
Very fast — regrows strongly each spring.
Lifespan
10–15 years with annual pruning.
Bloom / fruit season
Dense, fragrant spikes June–September; cutting faded spikes prolongs flowering.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −15 °C; suits all Tunisian regions; may struggle in extreme southern heat.
Toxicity & safety
Generally regarded as non-toxic, though not edible. Safe in gardens with children.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced fertilizer applied once in early spring is enough; avoid high nitrogen which produces lush growth but fewer flowers.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Sparse, short flower spikesLack of hard annual pruning.Prune to 30–50 cm from the ground each late winter without fail.
Wilting in summer heatDrought stress; soil dried out too far.Water deeply; mulch the base to retain moisture.
Cottony white masses on stemsMealy bug infestation.Wipe off with cotton dipped in alcohol; treat with horticultural oil.
Availability: Available in Tunisian garden centres, especially in northern regions; less common in the south.
