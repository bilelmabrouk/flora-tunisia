Crape myrtle ⇄ also: shrubs

Lagerstroemia indica  ·  Family: Lythraceae
Trees · Ornamental / shade
EN Crape myrtle  ·  FR Lilas des Indes  ·  AR ليلك الهند  ·  Lagerstroemia indica
Origin: China and SE Asia
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A tough, drought-tolerant small tree or large shrub that explodes with pink, red or white crinkled flowers all summer long. One of the most heat-tolerant ornamentals for Tunisian gardens.
Light
Full sun is essential; at least 6 hours daily for best flowering.
Water
Low to moderate; very drought-tolerant once established; water young plants weekly.
Soil
Any well-drained soil; tolerates clay, sand and mild alkalinity.
Temperature
Thrives in heat; survives down to about −10 °C when dormant in winter.
Humidity
Prefers dry air; high humidity promotes powdery mildew.
Feeding
Balanced fertilizer once in spring; excess nitrogen reduces flowering.
Pruning & grooming
Light shaping after flowering; avoid the 'crape murder' severe lopping — it is unnecessary.
Propagation
Hardwood cuttings in winter or semi-hardwood in summer; also from seed.
Mature size
Shrub to small tree 3–8 m depending on variety.
Growth rate
Moderate to fast.
Lifespan
Decades; very long-lived.
Bloom / fruit season
June to September with prolonged summer display.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to about −10 °C when fully dormant; top-growth may die back in cold winters and re-sprout.
Toxicity & safety
Not toxic to humans or pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced or slightly phosphorus-rich fertilizer once in spring supports the long summer flowering; no feed after midsummer.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
White powder on leavesPowdery mildew, common in warm humid spells.Ensure good air circulation; choose mildew-resistant varieties; treat with sulfur-based spray if severe.
Few or no flowersToo much shade or excess nitrogen.Relocate to full sun; use a low-nitrogen, high-phosphorus feed.
Aphids on new shootsSap-sucking aphids, especially in spring.Knock off with a jet of water; use insecticidal soap if heavy infestation.
Bark peeling in patchesNatural bark shedding — characteristic of the species.No action needed; the mottled trunk is ornamental.
Availability: Widely available in Tunisian garden centres; sold as container or bare-root plants in winter–spring.
