Christmas cactus

Schlumbergera truncata  ·  Family: Cactaceae
Interior · Flowering
EN Christmas cactus  ·  FR Cactus de Noël  ·  AR كاكتوس الميلاد  ·  Schlumbergera truncata
Origin: Brazil (Atlantic Forest, coastal mountains)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Low
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Indoor
In short A forest cactus (not a desert one) that bursts into vivid pink, red, or white flowers in late autumn and winter — brightening Tunisian homes during the cooler months. Tolerant and long-lived with minimal care.
Light
Bright indirect light; some morning sun is fine but avoid harsh midday sun.
Water
More than a typical cactus: water when the top 2 cm dry out (every 7–10 days in summer). Reduce in autumn to trigger blooming, then resume when buds appear.
Soil
Well-draining, slightly rich mix; add perlite. Better drained than regular houseplant mix.
Temperature
Ideal 15–22 °C; a cooler period (10–15 °C) in autumn for 4–6 weeks triggers blooming.
Humidity
Prefers moderate humidity; tolerates average conditions.
Feeding
Every 2–3 weeks with a flower fertilizer from spring until early autumn; stop to trigger bloom.
Pruning & grooming
Pinch or twist off 2–3 stem segments after flowering to shape and encourage branching.
Propagation
Stem segment cuttings (2–3 pads) left to dry then planted in moist mix; root in 3–4 weeks.
Mature size
20–40 cm tall; trailing to 60 cm.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Very long-lived — 20–30 years with proper care.
Bloom / fruit season
Autumn–winter (October–January) in Tunisia; triggered by shorter days and cooler nights.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; minimum 10 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced or phosphorus-rich fertilizer every 2–3 weeks from spring to late summer; stop in early autumn to allow the plant to rest and set buds for winter.  Find fertilizer →
