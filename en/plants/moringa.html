Moringa (drumstick tree)

Moringa oleifera  ·  Family: Moringaceae
Trees · Ornamental / shade
EN Moringa (drumstick tree)  ·  FR Moringa (arbre de vie)  ·  AR المورينغا (شجرة الرواق)  ·  Moringa oleifera
Origin: Sub-Himalayan India
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A very fast, drought-hardy tree grown for its nutritious edible leaves and long seed pods; thrives in Tunisian heat and poor soil.
Light
Full sun.
Water
Low; drought-tolerant once established.
Soil
Well-drained, even poor sandy soil.
Temperature
Loves heat; tender to frost.
Humidity
Dry air fine.
Feeding
Light feed for leaf harvests.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back to keep leaves within reach.
Propagation
From seed or cuttings, very easy.
Mature size
5–10 m if unpruned; kept lower for harvest.
Growth rate
Very fast.
Lifespan
Medium-lived.
Bloom / fruit season
Cream flowers spring–summer, then pods.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to about −1 °C; reshoots from base.
Toxicity & safety
Leaves, pods and flowers are edible; mature seeds/root in excess are not.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A light balanced feed keeps leaf harvests coming; it needs little on poor soil.  Find fertilizer →
