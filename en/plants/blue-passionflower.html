Blue passionflower

Passiflora caerulea  ·  Family: Passifloraceae
Exterior · Climber
EN Blue passionflower  ·  FR Passiflore bleue  ·  AR زهرة الآلام  ·  Passiflora caerulea
Origin: South America (Argentina, Brazil, Paraguay)
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short One of the most exotic-looking climbers available in Tunisia, with intricate blue-white flowers all summer and orange edible fruits in autumn; fast-growing and surprisingly frost-hardy.
Light
Full sun; most flowers in maximum light.
Water
Moderate; water regularly in summer, reduce in winter.
Soil
Well-drained, moderately fertile soil; tolerates poorer soils.
Temperature
Hardy to about −10 °C; survives in all Tunisian regions.
Humidity
Tolerates coastal humidity and dry inland air.
Feeding
Low-nitrogen flowering fertilizer every 3–4 weeks in the growing season.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back hard in late winter to remove dead or frost-damaged stems.
Propagation
Semi-hardwood cuttings in summer; seed in spring.
Mature size
Up to 10 m; vigorous and spreads via underground runners.
Growth rate
Very fast.
Lifespan
Long-lived perennial; spreads to form colonies.
Bloom / fruit season
Late spring to early autumn (May–September); exotic intricate flowers.
Hardiness (min temp)
Surprisingly hardy to −10 °C; dies back but regenerates from roots.
Toxicity & safety
Fruit is edible when orange-ripe; leaves and unripe fruit mildly toxic.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Use a low-nitrogen, high-potassium fertilizer; too much nitrogen grows leaves at the expense of flowers.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
No flowers on mature plantToo much nitrogen or insufficient sun.Switch to a high-potassium fertilizer; place in full sun.
Runners invading bordersUnderground stolons spread widely.Install a root barrier; dig out unwanted runners regularly.
Caterpillar leaf damagePassionfruit butterfly or similar caterpillars.Hand-pick; use biological controls if severe.
Frost kill above groundHard frost damages stems; roots usually survive.Cut back to ground; mulch roots; new shoots emerge in spring.
