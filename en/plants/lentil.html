Lentil

Lens culinaris  ·  Family: Fabaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Lentil  ·  FR Lentille  ·  AR عدس  ·  Lens culinaris
Origin: Near East and Mediterranean basin
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short One of the oldest crops in the Mediterranean world, lentils grow well across all of Tunisia in cool, dry conditions and require minimal inputs — a low-maintenance protein staple for any kitchen garden.
Light
Full sun; needs a long sunny growing season.
Water
Low; drought-tolerant; water only during germination and early growth; reduce once established.
Soil
Well-drained, light to medium loam; tolerates poor, alkaline soils; pH 6–8.5.
Temperature
Cool-season; optimal 15–25 °C; tolerates light frost; suffers above 30 °C during flowering.
Humidity
Prefers dry air; humid weather encourages fungal diseases.
Feeding
Minimal; apply phosphorus at sowing; avoid nitrogen as the plant fixes its own.
Pruning & grooming
Not required; low bushy habit.
Propagation
Direct sow 2–3 cm deep, 5–10 cm apart, in rows 20–30 cm wide.
Mature size
20–50 cm tall; low and bushy.
Growth rate
Moderate; harvest 80–110 days from sowing.
Lifespan
Annual.
Bloom / fruit season
Small white or pale violet flowers from late winter to spring.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tolerates light frost; sow in autumn to early winter in Tunisia.
Toxicity & safety
Safe when cooked; raw lentils should not be eaten in large quantities.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A single application of phosphorus-rich fertilizer at sowing is sufficient; additional nitrogen fertilizer is counterproductive as the plant fixes its own.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Brown lesions on stems and leaves causing die-backAscochyta blight fungus; spreads in wet, cool conditions.Ensure good airflow; remove infected material; rotate crops annually.
Plants turn yellow and die prematurelyVascular wilt (Fusarium or Rhizoctonia); favoured by poor drainage.Improve drainage; rotate with non-legume crops for 3–4 years.
Stunted plants with notched leaf edgesWeevil larvae feeding on roots and adults chewing leaves.Rotate crops; shake plants at dawn to dislodge adult weevils.
Availability: Seeds available at markets and agricultural cooperatives across Tunisia, especially in autumn.
