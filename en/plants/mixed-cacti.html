Mixed cacti

Mixed cacti (Cereus, Mammillaria, Parodia)  ·  Family: Cactaceae
Interior · Succulent / cactus
EN Mixed cacti  ·  FR Cactus divers  ·  AR كاكتوس  ·  Mixed cacti (Cereus, Mammillaria, Parodia)
Origin: Americas (Mexico, S America, Caribbean)
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Indoor
In short Mixed cacti are the ultimate low-maintenance indoor plants for sunny Tunisian homes — they love the heat and drought and ask only for a bright window and restrained watering.
Light
Maximum direct sun; a south-facing window is ideal — inadequate light causes etiolation.
Water
Minimal: water deeply every 2–4 weeks in summer, once a month or less in winter; let soil dry completely between waterings.
Soil
Specialized cactus mix or sandy loam with 50% grit/perlite for rapid drainage.
Temperature
Thrives at 20–35 °C; most species tolerate down to 5–10 °C but prefer no frost.
Humidity
Very low; dry indoor air is perfect — avoid humid locations.
Feeding
A cactus fertilizer once a month in spring and summer; none in autumn–winter.
Pruning & grooming
Not applicable; remove dead or shrivelled pads/stems with clean gloves.
Propagation
Offsets or cuttings; allow cut surfaces to callous for several days before planting.
Mature size
Varies: Mammillaria stays under 15 cm; Cereus can reach 1–2 m given time.
Growth rate
Slow.
Lifespan
Decades; some Cereus species live centuries.
Bloom / fruit season
Mammillaria and Parodia bloom annually with colourful flowers; Cereus flowers at night.
Hardiness (min temp)
Most frost-tender; keep above 5 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic (spines are a physical hazard, not chemical).
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A cactus-specific fertilizer (high potassium, low nitrogen) at half dose in spring and summer supports healthy spines and flowering without causing soft growth.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Pale, elongated, leaning stemInsufficient light.Move to a brighter window; rotate pot for even growth.
Brown mushy baseRoot and crown rot from overwatering.Cut above the rot, dry for a week, reroot in dry cactus mix.
Small white cottony tufts in spine clustersMealybug infestation.Dab affected spots with rubbing alcohol on a cotton swab; isolate from other plants.
No flowers despite years of growthInsufficient light, too-large pot, or lack of a cool winter rest period.Give maximum sun, keep slightly pot-bound, and let it rest cooler and drier in winter.
Availability: Very common and inexpensive, sold as small potted mixed cacti in nurseries, souks, and supermarkets across Tunisia.
