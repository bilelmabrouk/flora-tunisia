Hyssop

Hyssopus officinalis  ·  Family: Lamiaceae
Exterior · Herb / aromatic
EN Hyssop  ·  FR Hysope  ·  AR زوفا  ·  Hyssopus officinalis
Origin: Southern Europe & Western Asia
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A drought-hardy aromatic sub-shrub with vivid blue-violet flowers that attract bees and butterflies. Suits northern and inland Tunisian gardens and has long been valued in herbal medicine and cuisine.
Light
Full sun; thrives in open positions with 6+ hours daily.
Water
Low — very drought-tolerant once established; water sparingly, allowing soil to dry between waterings.
Soil
Poor to average, very well-drained, slightly alkaline soil; rocky or sandy is ideal.
Temperature
Best at 15–25 °C; tolerates frost to −15 °C and heat up to 35 °C.
Humidity
Prefers dry conditions; high humidity encourages fungal disease.
Feeding
No regular feeding needed; a light balanced fertilizer in spring every 1–2 years is sufficient.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back by half after flowering to maintain compact, bushy shape.
Propagation
By seed in spring, or softwood cuttings in early summer.
Mature size
30–60 cm tall and wide.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Short-lived woody perennial; 4–7 years.
Bloom / fruit season
Dense spikes of blue-violet (or white/pink) flowers July–September; excellent for pollinators.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −15 °C; best in northern and north-western Tunisia; may need protection from very hot southern summers.
Toxicity & safety
Generally safe in culinary amounts; the essential oil should not be taken internally in large doses.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Hyssop prefers lean soil; a very light balanced fertilizer once in spring is all it needs.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Grey mould on stems and flowersBotrytis fungus in humid, poorly ventilated conditions.Improve air circulation; remove affected parts; reduce moisture.
Sparse, leggy growthInsufficient sunlight or lack of pruning.Relocate to full sun; cut back hard after flowering each year.
Aphids on flower spikesTender shoot tips attract aphids in spring.Spray with diluted soap water; hyssop's strong scent deters many pests naturally.
Availability: Occasionally available in Tunisian garden centres and herb markets; more reliably sourced as seeds.
