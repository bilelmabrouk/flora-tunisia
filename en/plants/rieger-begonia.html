Rieger begonia

Begonia × hiemalis  ·  Family: Begoniaceae
Interior · Flowering
EN Rieger begonia  ·  FR Bégonia d'hiver  ·  AR بيغونيا  ·  Begonia × hiemalis
Origin: Garden hybrid (Begonia socotrana × tuberous begonias)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Indoor
In short A winter-flowering begonia with masses of double blooms in orange, red, pink, or yellow — one of the best seasonal colour plants for cool Tunisian interiors from autumn through spring. Needs bright light and careful watering.
Light
Bright indirect light; some morning sun is beneficial but avoid direct midday sun.
Water
Keep soil evenly moist but never wet; water at the base, not on leaves or crown. Let top 1–2 cm dry slightly.
Soil
Rich, light, well-draining potting mix; ensure no waterlogging.
Temperature
Best at 14–20 °C; cooler nights prolong blooms. Avoid temperatures above 25 °C in flower.
Humidity
Moderate; avoid wetting foliage. Good air circulation prevents powdery mildew.
Feeding
Weekly with a diluted flower fertilizer (high phosphorus) while in bloom.
Pruning & grooming
Deadhead spent flowers regularly; cut stems back after flowering to encourage reblooming.
Propagation
Stem or leaf cuttings; seed (complex for hybrids — best left to specialists).
Mature size
25–40 cm tall and wide.
Growth rate
Moderate; treated as a seasonal plant.
Lifespan
Often grown as a seasonal plant; can be kept 2–3 years with pruning.
Bloom / fruit season
Autumn through spring in Tunisia; flowers can last 3–4 months.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; minimum 10 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Toxic if ingested (especially the roots/tubers); keep away from children and pets.

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do

Flower buds dropping before openingTemperature too high (above 22 °C) or sudden change in environment.Move to a cooler spot (14–18 °C); avoid moving the plant while in bud.
Stem rot at soil levelOverwatering or water pooling at the crown.Reduce watering; always water at the base and ensure drainage.
