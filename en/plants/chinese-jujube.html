Chinese jujube

Ziziphus jujuba  ·  Family: Rhamnaceae
Trees · Fruit / nut
EN Chinese jujube  ·  FR Jujubier  ·  AR عنّاب  ·  Ziziphus jujuba
Origin: China and Central Asia

Setting
Outdoor
In short The jujube is an incredibly tough and adaptable fruit tree well-loved in Tunisia, producing sweet red-brown dates in late summer. It tolerates drought, heat, alkaline soils and neglect better than almost any other fruit tree.
Light
Full sun — thrives in intense heat; more sun means sweeter fruit.
Water
Very drought-tolerant; water young trees regularly; established trees cope with minimal irrigation.
Soil
Tolerates almost any soil — sandy, clay, alkaline, even slightly saline; excellent drainage preferred.
Temperature
Thrives in 25–40 °C; dormant trees hardy to about −15 °C; late frost can damage new growth.
Humidity
Prefers low humidity; high humidity at fruit ripening can cause cracking and rot.
Feeding
Light fertilization in spring — tolerates poor soil; avoid over-feeding.
Pruning & grooming
Prune lightly in winter to remove dead wood and maintain shape; suckers should be removed promptly.
Propagation
By root cuttings, suckers, or grafting for named varieties; seed-grown trees are variable.
Mature size
4–8 m naturally; manageable at 3–4 m with pruning.
Growth rate
Moderate — first fruit in 2–3 years.
Lifespan
Very long-lived — productive for many decades.
Bloom / fruit season
May–July; small yellow-green flowers, heavily scented, attracting bees.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −15 °C dormant; young shoots can be frost-nipped.
Toxicity & safety
Fruit, bark and leaves are safe and used medicinally.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Jujube is undemanding — a light dressing of a balanced fruit-tree fertilizer in spring is all it needs; over-feeding reduces fruit quality.  Find fertilizer →
