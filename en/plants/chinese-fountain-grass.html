Chinese fountain grass

Pennisetum alopecuroides  ·  Family: Poaceae
Exterior · Ornamental grass
EN Chinese fountain grass  ·  FR Pennisetum blanc  ·  AR الثيوم الأبيض  ·  Pennisetum alopecuroides
Origin: East Asia (China, Japan, Korea, Southeast Asia)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A compact, cool-season-tolerant ornamental grass with creamy-white to silvery plumes in late summer and autumn, well-suited to northern and coastal Tunisian gardens.
Light
Full sun preferred; tolerates very light shade without major loss of flowering.
Water
Low to moderate; more drought-tolerant than P. setaceum; water every 2 weeks in hot summer.
Soil
Well-drained, moderately fertile loam or sandy loam; not for heavy clay.
Temperature
More cold-tolerant than P. setaceum; handles temperatures down to −10 °C.
Humidity
Adaptable; fine in coastal humid air or drier inland areas.
Feeding
Light balanced fertilizer once in spring; avoid excess feeding.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back to 15–20 cm in late winter; the dry winter plumes are attractive and can be left.
Propagation
By division in spring or from seed; cultivars by division only.
Mature size
60–120 cm tall; rounded, arching clump.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Long-lived perennial if divided regularly; 5–10 years.
Bloom / fruit season
Fluffy white to cream or pinkish plumes from late summer through autumn.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to about −10 °C; suitable for all northern Tunisian winters.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) One light balanced fertilizer application in early spring is sufficient; this grass does not need heavy feeding.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Clump dying in the centreNatural ageing; common after 4–5 years.Divide in spring; replant healthy outer sections.
Few or no plumes in summerInsufficient warmth accumulation; this grass needs hot summers to flower well.Place in the hottest, sunniest spot in the garden; avoid cool shade.
Yellowing foliageOverwatering or nutrient-deficient soil.Reduce watering; apply a light balanced feed in spring.
Availability: Available in Tunisian garden centres, especially in the north; less common than P. setaceum.
