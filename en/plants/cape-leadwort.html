Cape leadwort ⇄ also: climber

Plumbago auriculata  ·  Family: Plumbaginaceae
Exterior · Shrub
EN Cape leadwort  ·  FR Plumbago  ·  AR رصاصية  ·  Plumbago auriculata
Origin: South Africa (Western Cape to KwaZulu-Natal)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short One of Tunisia's most-loved flowering shrubs — plumbago produces a near-constant cascade of sky-blue flowers from spring to autumn and is perfect for fences, walls, and sunny borders.
Light
Full sun: best flowering in full sun; tolerates light shade but fewer flowers.
Water
Low to moderate: drought-tolerant once established; water regularly for best flowering.
Soil
Well-drained, moderately fertile; tolerates most soils including sandy and rocky.
Temperature
Frost-tender below −2 °C; thrives in coastal and southern Tunisian heat.
Humidity
Tolerates coastal conditions well; avoid excessive dampness at the roots.
Feeding
Every 3–4 weeks in spring–summer with a flowering-plant fertilizer.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back by one-half to two-thirds in late winter or early spring to encourage a new flowering flush.
Propagation
Easily from semi-hardwood cuttings in summer.
Mature size
1–3 m as a shrub; can scramble to 4–5 m when supported.
Growth rate
Fast.
Lifespan
Perennial; many years in a frost-free garden.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring through autumn, almost continuously in warm conditions.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender: protect below 0 °C; not suitable for region 2 without protection.
Toxicity & safety
Sap and leaves mildly toxic; can cause skin and eye irritation; keep from children and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A flowering-plant fertilizer every 3–4 weeks during the growing season prolongs the long blooming period; reduce to once a month in cooler months.  Find fertilizer →

SymptomLikely causeWhat to do

Sticky leaves, sooty mouldWhitefly or aphid infestation.Spray with neem oil or insecticidal soap; repeat weekly until resolved.
Frost damage: blackened stems in winterFrost below −2 °C killing the tender stems.Cut back to healthy wood in spring; protect the crown with mulch or fleece over winter.
Availability: Widely available in Tunisian garden centres; very commonly used on coastal fences, walls, and pergolas.
