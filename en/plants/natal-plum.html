Natal plum

Carissa macrocarpa  ·  Family: Apocynaceae
Exterior · Shrub
EN Natal plum  ·  FR Carissa  ·  AR كاريسا  ·  Carissa macrocarpa
Origin: South Africa (KwaZulu-Natal coastal bush)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short A tough, salt-tolerant South African shrub with fragrant white flowers and edible red fruits, perfect for coastal Tunisian gardens as a dense hedge or accent plant.
Light
Full sun to partial shade; flowers best in full sun.
Water
Moderate; drought-tolerant once established; water every 1–2 weeks in summer.
Soil
Well-drained sandy or loamy soil; excellent salt and alkaline tolerance.
Temperature
Best 18–30 °C; tolerates brief frost to −3 °C; suited to coastal and southern Tunisia.
Humidity
Tolerates coastal salt spray and humidity; also adapts to drier conditions.
Feeding
Balanced fertilizer in spring and summer; not demanding.
Pruning & grooming
Responds well to clipping as a formal hedge; wear gloves — branches are thorny.
Propagation
Semi-ripe cuttings in summer; can be air-layered.
Mature size
2–4 m tall and wide.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Long-lived — 20+ years.
Bloom / fruit season
Fragrant star-shaped white flowers mainly spring and autumn; sporadic throughout the year.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −3 °C; best in regions 1, 3, 5, 6; protect from severe frost inland.
Toxicity & safety
All parts except ripe fruit are toxic; ripe red fruits are edible and sweet. Caution with children around unripe fruit and seeds.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced fertilizer in spring and again in early summer is sufficient; this plant is not demanding.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellowing leavesOverwatering or iron chlorosis in alkaline soil.Improve drainage; apply chelated iron if soil is very alkaline.
Sooty mould on leavesScale insects or mealybugs secreting honeydew.Treat pests with horticultural oil; wipe off mould with a damp cloth.
Sparse floweringToo much shade or insufficient feeding.Move to a sunnier spot; apply fertilizer in spring.
Availability: Available in Tunisian garden centres, especially in coastal regions; increasingly popular as a salt-tolerant hedge.
