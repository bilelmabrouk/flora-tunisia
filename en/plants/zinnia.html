Zinnia

Zinnia elegans  ·  Family: Asteraceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN Zinnia  ·  FR Zinnia  ·  AR زينيا  ·  Zinnia elegans
Origin: Mexico
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short The go-to summer annual for Tunisian gardens — heat-loving, drought-tolerant, and producing vivid pom-pom flowers in every colour from spring through autumn.
Light
Full sun is essential — minimum 6–8 hours daily; shading causes lanky stems and less blooming.
Water
Moderate: water at the base when the top 3–4 cm of soil dry out; avoid wetting foliage.
Soil
Any well-drained garden soil; tolerates average to poor soils well.
Temperature
Heat-loving; ideal 25–35 °C; sow after last frost; survives Tunisia's summer heat well.
Humidity
Average; always water at the base — wet foliage encourages powdery mildew.
Feeding
A flowering-plant fertilizer every 3–4 weeks encourages continuous blooming.
Pruning & grooming
Deadhead regularly and pinch young plants to encourage bushiness.
Propagation
Easy from seed sown directly in the ground in spring once soil warms.
Mature size
30–90 cm tall depending on variety.
Growth rate
Fast — blooms 6–8 weeks from seed.
Lifespan
Annual; grows spring to first frost or summer heat burnout.
Bloom / fruit season
Late spring through autumn (May–October in Tunisia).
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; sow once temperatures are reliably above 15 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A phosphorus-rich flowering-plant fertilizer every 3–4 weeks from first bud until autumn keeps blooms large and abundant through Tunisia's long hot summer.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
White powdery coating on leavesPowdery mildew from humid air on wet foliage.Water only at the base; space plants; remove infected leaves.
Stunted, distorted growthAphid or thrip infestation.Remove by hand or use a strong water jet; repeated treatment may be needed; introduce ladybirds.
Weak, floppy stems that flop overToo much shade or excess nitrogen fertilizer.Move to full sun, stake if needed, and switch to a bloom-boosting low-nitrogen fertilizer.
Flowers fading quickly and fewer new budsSpent flowers left on the plant, or heat stress during peak summer.Deadhead consistently; provide light afternoon shade and extra water during heatwaves.
Availability: A hugely popular summer bedding annual in Tunisia, sold as seedlings in every nursery, souk, and supermarket garden section from spring onward.
