Trailing ice plant

Lampranthus spectabilis  ·  Family: Aizoaceae
Exterior · Ground-cover
EN Trailing ice plant  ·  FR Lampranthus  ·  AR لامبرانتوس  ·  Lampranthus spectabilis
Origin: South Africa (Western Cape)
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A spectacular spring-flowering succulent groundcover that explodes with vivid pink, magenta, or purple daisy flowers along Tunisia's sunny coastal banks and slopes. Extremely drought-tolerant and almost maintenance-free.
Light
Full sun only; flowers close in shade and plant becomes leggy.
Water
Very low; essentially drought-proof once established; do not water in summer.
Soil
Sandy, rocky, freely draining, low-fertility; no clay or waterlogged spots.
Temperature
Best above 5 °C; frost damages stems below −3 °C but regrows from roots.
Humidity
Excellent salt and wind tolerance; perfect for coastal conditions.
Feeding
Rarely needed; one light feed in early spring encourages better flowering.
Pruning & grooming
Trim back straggling stems after flowering to maintain shape and density.
Propagation
Very easy from stem cuttings any time; stick in dry sand and withhold water for 2 weeks.
Mature size
20–30 cm tall, spreading 60–100 cm wide.
Growth rate
Moderate to fast; covers a slope in 1–2 seasons.
Lifespan
Long-lived perennial in frost-free coastal areas.
Bloom / fruit season
Late winter to spring (February–May), masses of brilliant pink, magenta, or purple flowers.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; stems damaged below −3 °C; roots usually survive.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and animals.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A single light application of a balanced fertilizer in early spring is sufficient; heavy feeding causes lush, floppy growth and reduces the dramatic flower display.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Rotting stems and dying patchesOverwatering especially in summer, or clay soil retaining moisture.Remove affected stems; replant in pure sandy soil; water only in winter–spring.
No flowers in springShade, old woody plant, or very dry summer stress preventing bud set.Move to full sun; cut back after flowering; allow a few waterings in late summer.
Aphids on new flower budsSoft new growth in spring attracts aphids.Blast off with a strong jet of water; spray with insecticidal soap if persistent.
Availability: Commonly sold in garden centres along Tunisia's coast; easy to propagate from cuttings obtained locally.
