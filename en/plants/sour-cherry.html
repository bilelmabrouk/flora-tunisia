Sour cherry

Prunus cerasus  ·  Family: Rosaceae
Trees · Fruit / nut
EN Sour cherry  ·  FR Griottier  ·  AR كرز حامض  ·  Prunus cerasus
Origin: South-eastern Europe and south-western Asia (hybrid origin: P. avium × P. fruticosa)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short More adaptable than sweet cherry, the sour cherry tolerates cooler as well as moderately warm conditions and is largely self-fertile. Its tart fruit is prized for jams, syrups and cooking in northern Tunisia.
Light
Full sun to light partial shade; more shade-tolerant than sweet cherry.
Water
Moderate; regular watering in establishment and summer; fairly drought-tolerant once mature.
Soil
Adaptable to a range of soils; well-drained, moderately fertile; tolerates slightly clay soils better than sweet cherry.
Temperature
Hardy to −30 °C; needs 600–1200 chilling hours but many varieties suit temperate to semi-arid conditions.
Humidity
Moderate; prefers drier air during ripening to avoid brown rot.
Feeding
Light balanced fertilizer in early spring; avoid excess nitrogen.
Pruning & grooming
Prune in summer; thin crowded branches; remove old unproductive wood to stimulate new growth.
Propagation
Grafting onto Mahaleb or Colt rootstock; most varieties self-fertile — no cross-pollinator needed.
Mature size
4–8 m; compact compared to sweet cherry.
Growth rate
Moderate; fruiting from year 3–5.
Lifespan
20–40 years.
Bloom / fruit season
White flowers in March–April; bright red-to-dark fruit in June–July.
Hardiness (min temp)
More frost-hardy than sweet cherry; also tolerates moderate continental conditions (region 4).
Toxicity & safety
Fruit safe; stones contain amygdalin — avoid consuming in large quantity.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A light balanced fertilizer in early spring is sufficient; sour cherries are less demanding than sweet cherries and produce well on moderate fertility.  Find fertilizer →

Availability: Available in some Tunisian nurseries, mainly in the north; less widespread than apricot or apple.
