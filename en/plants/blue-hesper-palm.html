Blue hesper palm

Brahea armata  ·  Family: Arecaceae
Trees · Palm / statement
EN Blue hesper palm  ·  FR Palmier bleu  ·  AR نخيل أزرق  ·  Brahea armata
Origin: Baja California, Mexico (arid rocky slopes)
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short The blue hesper palm is prized for its striking silver-blue fan fronds and extreme drought tolerance, making it one of the best choices for dry Tunisian gardens from the Sahel to the south. Slow-growing but virtually indestructible.
Light
Full sun obligatory — thrives in intense desert sun.
Water
Very low: one of the most drought-tolerant palms; water deeply once a month in summer after establishment, rarely in winter.
Soil
Excellent drainage essential; thrives in rocky, sandy, or poor soils; tolerates limestone and alkaline conditions.
Temperature
Tolerates heat above 45 °C and frost to −10 °C; extremely wide temperature range.
Humidity
Prefers dry air; tolerates coastal moisture but dislikes wet conditions.
Feeding
Minimal — a single annual slow-release palm fertilizer application suffices.
Pruning & grooming
Remove only dead fronds; spines on the frond petioles are sharp — use thick gloves.
Propagation
From seed; germinates slowly — 2–6 months.
Mature size
Up to 10–15 m tall; crown 3–5 m wide.
Growth rate
Very slow — 15–30 cm per year.
Lifespan
Very long-lived — centuries.
Bloom / fruit season
Long, drooping inflorescences up to 5 m in spring–early summer; small round edible fruits.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −10 °C; suitable across most of Tunisia except deep frost zones.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; small fruits edible.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A single annual application of slow-release palm fertilizer in spring is all this drought-adapted palm requires; over-feeding promotes rank growth and reduces its ornamental blue colouration.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Fronds losing blue colour, turning greenerToo much water or fertilizer; or inadequate sun exposure.Reduce watering and feeding; ensure full sun position.
Scale insects on trunk and frond basesScale insects — occasional in dry conditions.Spray with neem oil or diluted alcohol; scrub off manually.
Fronds browning from tip in summerNormal response to extreme heat and dryness — not a disease.Trim brown tips if desired; one deep watering per month in peak heat.
Availability: Occasionally available in specialised Tunisian nurseries; sought after for dry and rocky garden projects.
