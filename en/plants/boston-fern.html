Boston fern

Nephrolepis exaltata  ·  Family: Nephrolepidaceae
Interior · Foliage
EN Boston fern  ·  FR Fougère de Boston  ·  AR سرخس بوسطن  ·  Nephrolepis exaltata
Origin: Pantropical (widespread in humid tropical regions worldwide)
Light
Shade / indirect
Water
High
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Indoor
In short A lush cascading fern that purifies air and adds softness to any room — needs consistent moisture and humidity, making it best suited to cooler, more humid Tunisian interiors.
Light
Bright to moderate indirect light; avoid direct sun which scorches fronds.
Water
Keep evenly moist at all times — never let it dry out completely. Water frequently in summer.
Soil
Moisture-retaining but well-aerated mix; peat or coir with perlite.
Temperature
Best at 16–24 °C; suffers above 27 °C with low humidity.
Humidity
High humidity is essential; place on a pebble tray, mist daily, or use a humidifier.
Feeding
Balanced liquid fertilizer every 3–4 weeks at half strength in spring and summer.
Pruning & grooming
Remove brown or dying fronds from the base to keep plant tidy.
Propagation
By division or from runners (stolons); very easy.
Mature size
30–60 cm tall, fronds spreading to 60–90 cm.
Growth rate
Moderate to fast.
Lifespan
Several years; can decline in dry conditions but revives with improved humidity.
Bloom / fruit season
Ferns do not flower — reproduce by spores.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; keep above 10 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic — safe for humans, cats, and dogs.

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Brown, crispy frondsLow humidity or under-watering — the most common problem.Increase humidity immediately; water more consistently.
Yellowing frondsOver-watering or nutrient deficiency.Check soil drainage; resume fertilizing if not fed recently.
Fronds dropping or plant goes bareHeat stress, very dry air, or repotting shock.Move to a cooler, more humid spot; mist heavily; new fronds should emerge.
Availability: Commonly available in Tunisian garden centres, especially in spring; suits coastal and northern regions best.
