London plane

Platanus × hispanica  ·  Family: Platanaceae
Trees · Ornamental / shade
EN London plane  ·  FR Platane  ·  AR دلب  ·  Platanus × hispanica
Origin: hybrid (P. orientalis × P. occidentalis), origin uncertain, naturalised widely
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short The iconic avenue tree of Tunisian cities — massive, pollution-tolerant, and wonderfully cooling in summer. Best for large public spaces; too big for small gardens.
Light
Full sun; thrives in open, exposed positions.
Water
Moderate when young; established trees tolerate drought and periodic flooding.
Soil
Deep, fertile, well-drained loam; tolerates clay and compacted urban soils.
Temperature
Hardy; tolerates −15 °C and heat above 40 °C.
Humidity
Adaptable; performs well in both humid coastal and drier interior climates.
Feeding
Young trees benefit from a balanced fertilizer in spring; mature trees rarely need feeding.
Pruning & grooming
Pollard in late winter if needed to control size; otherwise remove dead wood only.
Propagation
By hardwood cuttings in winter, or from seed.
Mature size
15–30 m tall; massive canopy spread.
Growth rate
Fast — 60–90 cm per year when young.
Lifespan
Very long-lived — centuries.
Bloom / fruit season
Inconspicuous catkins in spring; non-ornamental.
Hardiness (min temp)
Fully hardy; no cold damage expected in Tunisia.
Toxicity & safety
Safe; fallen bark fibres can irritate respiratory tracts — wear a mask when raking.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Apply a slow-release balanced fertilizer around the root zone in early spring for young trees; established specimens rarely need supplementary feeding.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Tan-brown patches on leaves (anthracnose)Fungal disease in cool, wet spring weather.Rake and destroy fallen leaves; ensure good air circulation; trees usually recover on their own.
White powdery coating on young shootsPowdery mildew in hot, dry conditions with poor air flow.Improve air circulation; remove heavily affected shoots; water stress makes it worse.
Scale insects on bark; sticky honeydewScale insects, common in warm urban environments.Scrub with soapy water on accessible branches; encourage natural predators.
Premature leaf drop in summerSevere drought stress or disease pressure.Deep irrigation during prolonged drought; the tree normally releafs.
Availability: Widely planted in Tunisian public spaces; young trees available from tree nurseries.
