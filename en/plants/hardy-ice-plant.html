Hardy ice plant

Delosperma cooperi  ·  Family: Aizoaceae
Exterior · Ground-cover
EN Hardy ice plant  ·  FR Pourpier vivace  ·  AR نبتة الجليد  ·  Delosperma cooperi
Origin: South Africa (Drakensberg highlands)
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A sparkling, succulent groundcover that produces a carpet of vivid purple-pink daisy flowers all summer long. More frost-hardy than most ice plants, it thrives from the Tunisian coast to the cool northern hills.
Light
Full sun; shade dramatically reduces flowering.
Water
Very low; highly drought-tolerant; water only in very dry spells in summer.
Soil
Sandy, rocky, very well-drained; dies in heavy clay or waterlogged conditions.
Temperature
Tolerates frost to −15 °C when dry; heat and drought present no problem.
Humidity
Prefers dry air; high humidity combined with shade causes rot.
Feeding
A single light balanced feed in early spring is enough; do not over-feed.
Pruning & grooming
Light trim after flowering; remove any dead or straggly stems in spring.
Propagation
Easy from stem cuttings spring–autumn; root in dry sand within 2 weeks.
Mature size
5–10 cm tall, spreading 30–60 cm wide.
Growth rate
Moderate; dense mats form within 1–2 seasons.
Lifespan
Long-lived perennial; indefinite in free-draining conditions.
Bloom / fruit season
Late spring through autumn (April–September), abundant vivid purple-pink flowers.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-hardy to −15 °C (dry); more vulnerable if wet in winter.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and animals.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) One light balanced feed in early spring is sufficient; feeding too often or with a nitrogen-rich product weakens the plant's succulence and reduces flowering.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Rotting stems and patches of dead plantOverwatering or waterlogged winter soil.Remove dead parts; replant cuttings in very well-drained sandy soil; stop all watering in winter.
Sparse flowers despite full sunOver-fertilised or soil too rich.Stop feeding; if needed, transplant to a leaner soil mix.
Mealybugs hiding in leaf axilsWarm dry conditions; common on succulents.Apply diluted neem oil or insecticidal soap; repeat every 7–10 days until clear.
Availability: Available in garden centres and online across Tunisia; increasingly popular in coastal and northern gardens.
