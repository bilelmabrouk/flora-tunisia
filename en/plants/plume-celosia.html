Plume celosia

Celosia argentea var. plumosa  ·  Family: Amaranthaceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN Plume celosia  ·  FR Célosie plumeuse  ·  AR عرف الديك ريشي  ·  Celosia argentea var. plumosa
Origin: Tropical Africa and Asia
Light
Full sun
Water

Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short The feathery-plumed cousin of cockscomb — equally heat-loving and drought-tolerant, with silky spires of red, orange, yellow or pink that light up Tunisian summer borders.
Light
Full sun — 6+ hours daily for vivid plume colour.
Water
Low to moderate: let the surface dry between waterings; avoid wetting the plumes.
Soil
Light, well-drained soil; sandy loam with a little compost is ideal.
Temperature
Best at 22–35 °C; frost-sensitive — plant outdoors after cold nights are over.
Humidity
Prefers low humidity; humid, still air causes stem rot.
Feeding
Balanced fertilizer monthly; excessive nitrogen reduces flower quality.
Pruning & grooming
Remove faded plumes to encourage side-shoots; no hard pruning needed.
Propagation
Sow seed in spring; germinates in 7–10 days at 22–25 °C.
Mature size
30–60 cm tall, 15–20 cm wide.
Growth rate
Fast — flowers about 8–10 weeks from sowing.
Lifespan
Annual — replant each season.
Bloom / fruit season
Late spring through autumn; most vibrant in summer.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; minimum 15 °C for outdoor planting.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced flowering-plant fertilizer once a month during summer is enough; over-feeding with nitrogen makes plants leafy and reduces plume size.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Stem rot at the baseOver-watering combined with poor drainage.Let soil dry; improve drainage; remove affected plants.
Plumes losing colour prematurelyToo much shade or exhausted soil.Move to full sun; top-dress with compost.
Grey mould on plumes in humid weatherBotrytis fungal disease favoured by still, humid air.Space plants for better airflow; remove affected plumes promptly; avoid overhead watering.
Availability: Sold as seed packets and seedlings in Tunisian garden centres and summer markets, valued for its long-lasting feathery cut flowers.
