Foxtail agave

Agave attenuata  ·  Family: Asparagaceae
Exterior · Succulent / cactus
EN Foxtail agave  ·  FR Agave queue de lion  ·  AR أغاف  ·  Agave attenuata
Origin: Mexico (central highlands)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short The gentlest agave — no terminal spines, soft grey-green leaves, and a spectacular arching flower spike. Ideal for coastal Tunisian gardens where children or pets are present.
Light
Full sun to partial shade; tolerates more shade than most agaves.
Water
Very low; monthly in summer, none in winter. More drought-sensitive than A. americana.
Soil
Well-drained sandy or loamy soil; add grit for heavy soils.
Temperature
Prefers mild 10–30 °C; frost-tender — protect below 2 °C; best for coastal areas.
Humidity
Tolerates moderate coastal humidity; dislikes prolonged wet air.
Feeding
A light cactus fertilizer once in spring; none otherwise.
Pruning & grooming
Remove dead lower leaves; divide and remove pups if overcrowded.
Propagation
By offsets; also from bulbils that form on the flower spike.
Mature size
1–1.5 m tall rosette; flower spike arches to 2.5–3 m.
Growth rate
Slow.
Lifespan
Monocarpic; lives 10–20 years, flowers once, then dies; pups carry on.
Bloom / fruit season
A dramatic arching spike with yellow-green flowers; spring–summer, once in lifetime.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; protect below 2 °C. Suitable for regions 1, 3, 5 in Tunisia.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic — no terminal spines; one of the safest agaves.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A single light cactus fertilizer application in spring is optional; established plants in garden soil rarely need feeding.  Find fertilizer →
