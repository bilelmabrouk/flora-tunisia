Common myrtle

Myrtus communis  ·  Family: Myrtaceae
Exterior · Shrub
EN Common myrtle  ·  FR Myrte  ·  AR ريحان / مرسين  ·  Myrtus communis
Origin: Mediterranean basin (native to Tunisia and surrounding countries)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A fragrant, native Mediterranean shrub treasured in Tunisian culture — its aromatic leaves, white flowers, and dark berries make it both an ornamental and a traditional plant.
Light
Full sun preferred; tolerates light partial shade, especially in hot inland areas.
Water
Low to moderate: drought-tolerant once established; water occasionally in summer heat.
Soil
Well-drained, slightly alkaline to neutral; grows in poor, rocky Mediterranean soils.
Temperature
Hardy to about −10 °C; well adapted to all Tunisian climates except the hottest Saharan south.
Humidity
Prefers moderate humidity; excellent in coastal conditions.
Feeding
Light feed once in spring with a balanced or organic fertilizer.
Pruning & grooming
Clip lightly to shape after flowering; tolerates moderate pruning; avoid cutting into old wood.
Propagation
From semi-hardwood cuttings in summer, or layering.
Mature size
1–3 m tall and wide depending on pruning.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Very long-lived; centuries in the wild.
Bloom / fruit season
White, fragrant flowers in late spring–summer; followed by blue-black berries in autumn.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −10 °C; tolerates brief frost.
Toxicity & safety
Generally considered safe; berries sometimes used in traditional cooking.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A light application of organic compost or a balanced fertilizer in spring is all it needs; avoid over-feeding which can reduce flower fragrance.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellowing and dropping leavesOver-watering or waterlogged soil.Improve drainage and let soil dry out between waterings.
Powdery white coating on leavesPowdery mildew in humid, still air.Improve air circulation; remove infected leaves; apply diluted neem or baking soda spray.
No flowers or very fewInsufficient sun or heavy pruning done too late (cutting off flower buds).Ensure full sun; prune only immediately after flowering, never in late winter or spring.
Availability: Widely available in Tunisian nurseries; a native species also found growing wild in the northern hills.
