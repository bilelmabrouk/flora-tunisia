Trailing lantana ⇄ also: Interior

Lantana montevidensis  ·  Family: Verbenaceae
Exterior · Shrub
EN Trailing lantana  ·  FR Lantana retombant  ·  AR لانتانا متدلية  ·  Lantana montevidensis
Origin: South America (Uruguay, Argentina)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Indoor & out
In short A low, spreading groundcover that smothers itself in lavender-purple flowers from spring to autumn — virtually indestructible in Tunisian heat and coastal dryness.
Light
Full sun — the more sun, the more flowers; tolerates very little shade.
Water
Low: water only when soil dries out; very drought-tolerant once established.
Soil
Any well-drained soil, including sandy or rocky; dislikes waterlogged ground.
Temperature
Thrives at 20–35 °C; frost-tender below 0 °C.
Humidity
Low to average; copes well with dry coastal conditions.
Feeding
Light feed once in spring and once mid-summer; avoid excess nitrogen.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back by one-third after main flowering flush to encourage reblooming.
Propagation
Easily rooted from semi-hardwood cuttings in summer.
Mature size
20–50 cm tall, spreading 1–2 m wide.
Growth rate
Fast.
Lifespan
Perennial; several years with minimal care.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring through autumn, almost continuously in warm conditions.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; protect or treat as annual where temperatures drop below 0 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Berries are toxic if eaten, especially to children and pets; avoid ingestion.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A flowering-plant fertilizer (rich in phosphorus and potassium, low in nitrogen) once in spring supports continuous blooming without promoting excess foliage.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Few or no flowersToo much shade or excess nitrogen.Move to full sun; switch to a low-nitrogen flowering fertilizer.
Yellowing leavesOver-watering or waterlogged soil.Reduce watering; improve drainage.
Whitefly infestationCommon pest in hot, humid conditions.Spray with diluted neem oil or insecticidal soap; improve air circulation.
Leggy, sparse growthInsufficient light or lack of pruning.Move to brighter spot and cut back by one-third to promote bushy growth.
Availability: Commonly grown and sold in Tunisian garden centres; widely used for slopes, borders, and rockeries.
