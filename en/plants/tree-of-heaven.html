Tree of heaven

Ailanthus altissima  ·  Family: Simaroubaceae
Trees · Ornamental / shade
EN Tree of heaven  ·  FR Ailante  ·  AR عيلان  ·  Ailanthus altissima
Origin: N and central China, Taiwan
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short The ultimate survivor tree — virtually indestructible, growing in walls, rubble, and the harshest Tunisian urban sites. Its fast growth and tolerance of any condition make it useful but it can become invasive; manage deliberately.
Light
Full sun; grows rapidly even in moderate shade.
Water
Very low; extraordinarily drought-tolerant.
Soil
Tolerates any soil including rubble, chalk, clay, acid, alkaline, compacted.
Temperature
Hardy to about −20 °C; tolerates extreme heat.
Humidity
Tolerates dry to humid; truly adaptable.
Feeding
Never needed; self-sufficient in the poorest soils.
Pruning & grooming
Coppice annually to control height and produce large ornamental leaves; wear gloves.
Propagation
From seed (very prolific) or root cuttings; manages itself aggressively.
Mature size
15–30 m as a tree; easily coppiced to shrub height.
Growth rate
Extremely fast — 1–2 m per year.
Lifespan
Moderate, 30–70 years, but self-seeds freely.
Bloom / fruit season
Greenish-yellow small flowers in summer; attractive to bees but unpleasant scent.
Hardiness (min temp)
Fully hardy everywhere in Tunisia; possibly the most climate-tolerant tree in the list.
Toxicity & safety
Sap and leaves may cause dermatitis in sensitive individuals; generally safe.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Tree of heaven never needs fertilizer and grows vigorously on the poorest soils; feeding will only increase the suckering and invasive potential.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Invasive seedlings everywhere in the gardenOne tree can produce hundreds of thousands of wind-dispersed seeds.Remove flower clusters before seed set; pull seedlings when young; install root barriers.
Root suckers appearing metres from the trunkExtensive root system with aggressive suckering.Install a deep root barrier at planting; remove suckers as soon as they appear.
Skin irritation when handling cut woodSap in cut wood and leaves contains irritating compounds.Always wear gloves and long sleeves when pruning or cutting; wash skin immediately after.
Availability: Self-seeding prolifically across Tunisia; young trees easy to find in the wild; available in some nurseries.
