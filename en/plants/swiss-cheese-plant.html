Swiss cheese plant

Monstera deliciosa  ·  Family: Araceae
Interior · Foliage
EN Swiss cheese plant  ·  FR Monstera  ·  AR مونستيرا  ·  Monstera deliciosa
Origin: Central America (Mexico to Panama)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Indoor
In short The iconic split-leaf houseplant — fast-growing, forgiving, and architectural, it thrives in bright Tunisian interiors and is one of the most rewarding indoor plants around.
Light
Bright indirect light; tolerates moderate shade but fenestrations (holes) reduce. Avoid harsh direct noon sun.
Water
Allow top 3–5 cm to dry between waterings; reduce in winter. Sensitive to waterlogging.
Soil
Rich, well-draining mix with perlite and a little bark or coir.
Temperature
Thrives at 18–30 °C; damaged below 13 °C.
Humidity
Appreciates higher humidity; mist or wipe large leaves regularly.
Feeding
Balanced liquid fertilizer every 3–4 weeks spring to early autumn.
Pruning & grooming
Remove yellowed leaves; trim aerial roots or tuck them into the pot.
Propagation
Stem cuttings with at least one node in water or moist mix.
Mature size
1–3 m indoors; leaves can exceed 60 cm wide on mature plants.
Growth rate
Fast in good conditions.
Lifespan
Very long-lived — decades.
Bloom / fruit season
Rarely flowers indoors; outdoors may produce edible cream-green spadix.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; keep above 13 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Toxic — calcium oxalate crystals irritate mouth, throat, and gut; keep from pets and children.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced liquid fertilizer every 3–4 weeks in spring and summer supports the large leaves; a fertilizer with some calcium and magnesium gives the best results.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellow leavesOver-watering is the most likely cause.Allow soil to dry out more; check that drainage is working.
No splits or holes in new leavesPlant is too young, or insufficient light.Provide more light; fenestrations develop naturally as the plant matures.
Brown leaf tipsLow humidity or inconsistent watering.Mist leaves or increase ambient humidity; water more consistently.
Leggy growth reaching toward windowInsufficient light from the current position.Rotate pot regularly; move closer to light source.
Availability: Very commonly sold in Tunisian garden centres and online; one of the most popular houseplants nationwide.
