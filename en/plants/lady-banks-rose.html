Lady Banks' rose

Rosa banksiae  ·  Family: Rosaceae
Exterior · Climber
EN Lady Banks' rose  ·  FR Rosier de Banks  ·  AR وردة بانكس  ·  Rosa banksiae
Origin: China (central and western)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A thornless, incredibly vigorous climbing rose that cascades with masses of tiny white or yellow rosettes in spring; one of the best choices for Tunisian pergolas, walls, and arches.
Light
Full sun; needs at least 6 hours daily for good flowering.
Water
Low to moderate; drought-tolerant once established.
Soil
Well-drained, fertile soil; benefits from compost at planting.
Temperature
Hardy to about −15 °C; grows well in all Tunisian regions.
Humidity
Tolerates both coastal and dry inland air.
Feeding
Light rose fertilizer in early spring; avoid late-summer feeding.
Pruning & grooming
Prune after flowering (never before — flowers on old wood); remove dead or crossing stems.
Propagation
Semi-hardwood cuttings in summer.
Mature size
6–12 m; one of the largest climbing roses.
Growth rate
Fast to very fast.
Lifespan
Exceptionally long-lived; over 100 years recorded.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring (March–May); single flush but very profuse.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −15 °C; copes with all Tunisian climates.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; hips are not produced on thornless forms.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A specialised rose fertilizer in early spring before bud break encourages strong growth; a second application after flowering maintains vigour.  Find fertilizer →
