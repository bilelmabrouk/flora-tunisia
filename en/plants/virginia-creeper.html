Virginia creeper

Parthenocissus quinquefolia  ·  Family: Vitaceae
Exterior · Climber
EN Virginia creeper  ·  FR Vigne vierge  ·  AR عريشة العذراء  ·  Parthenocissus quinquefolia
Origin: Eastern North America
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short A vigorous self-clinging deciduous climber grown primarily for its spectacular scarlet autumn foliage; extremely hardy and suitable for cool northern and inland Tunisian gardens.
Light
Full sun to deep shade; autumn colour is best in sun.
Water
Low to moderate; drought-tolerant once established.
Soil
Almost any soil; tolerates poor, dry, or clay soils.
Temperature
Very hardy to −25 °C; fine even in cold Tunisian mountain winters.
Humidity
Tolerates any humidity level.
Feeding
Light annual feed in spring; generally self-sufficient.
Pruning & grooming
Trim in late winter to keep in bounds; remove from gutters and windows.
Propagation
Hardwood cuttings in winter or semi-hardwood in summer.
Mature size
Up to 15 m; keep to desired size by pruning.
Growth rate
Fast to very fast.
Lifespan
Very long-lived; 30+ years.
Bloom / fruit season
Inconspicuous greenish flowers in summer; mainly grown for foliage.
Hardiness (min temp)
Extremely frost-hardy; one of the toughest climbers.
Toxicity & safety
Berries and sap toxic if ingested; can cause skin irritation.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A light balanced feed in early spring is all that is needed; over-feeding creates excessive, unmanageable growth.  Find fertilizer →

SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Stems invading gutters/windowsUnchecked vigorous growth.Prune back hard every late winter; inspect regularly.
Poor autumn colourToo much shade or mild autumn temperatures.Plant in more sun; no remedy for warm weather — colour varies by year.
Japanese beetle or caterpillar damageLeaf-eating insects in summer.Hand-pick caterpillars; apply a suitable insecticide if severe.
