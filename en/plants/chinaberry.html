Chinaberry

Melia azedarach  ·  Family: Meliaceae
Trees · Ornamental / shade
EN Chinaberry  ·  FR Lilas de Perse  ·  AR الزنزلخت  ·  Melia azedarach
Origin: Indian subcontinent and SE Asia; naturalised across the Mediterranean including Tunisia
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Very toxic
Setting
Outdoor
In short A fast-growing, extremely drought-tolerant shade tree long naturalised in Tunisia, with fragrant lilac flowers in spring and persistent yellow berries — beautiful but highly toxic.
Light
Full sun; grows in any exposed position.
Water
Very low once established; one of Tunisia's most drought-tolerant trees.
Soil
Thrives in poor, sandy or rocky soils; tolerates mild salinity and alkalinity.
Temperature
Wide range: −10 °C to 45 °C; adapts everywhere in Tunisia.
Humidity
Very tolerant of dry or humid conditions.
Feeding
Rarely needed; a little general fertilizer at planting helps establishment.
Pruning & grooming
Prune in late winter to maintain shape and remove deadwood; responds well to hard cutting.
Propagation
Easily from seed sown in spring; or from root suckers.
Mature size
10–15 m tall with a broad spreading crown.
Growth rate
Very fast — can exceed 1 m per year.
Lifespan
Short to medium — 25–50 years; tends to become brittle with age.
Bloom / fruit season
March to May; small fragrant lilac flowers in panicles.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to about −10 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Highly toxic: all parts, especially the berries, are dangerous to humans, livestock and pets; plant with care where children play.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Almost no feeding required; at most a general-purpose fertilizer at planting time to help the young tree establish.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Premature leaf dropSummer drought stress or sudden cold snap.Water deeply during extreme heat; protect very young trees from late frost.
Powdery mildew on foliageWarm, humid, poorly ventilated conditions.Prune to open the canopy; apply a sulfur-based fungicide if severe.
Bark splitting near baseRoot waterlogging or mechanical damage.Improve drainage; avoid disturbing soil near the trunk with machinery.
Availability: Very common throughout Tunisia; self-seeds freely; available at most nurseries.
