Spanish bayonet

Yucca aloifolia  ·  Family: Asparagaceae
Exterior · Succulent / cactus
EN Spanish bayonet  ·  FR Yucca aloès  ·  AR يوكا  ·  Yucca aloifolia
Origin: South-eastern USA and Caribbean
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short A striking, architectural yucca with dangerously sharp leaf tips and bold white flower clusters — tough, drought-resistant, and excellent for coastal Tunisian gardens needing year-round structure.
Light
Full sun is ideal; tolerates partial shade but grows more slowly.
Water
Very low: water monthly in summer; none in winter for established plants.
Soil
Sandy, well-drained; tolerates poor, dry, and slightly saline soils.
Temperature
Best at 10–35 °C; tolerates light frost to about −10 °C once established.
Humidity
Tolerates dry air and salt spray — excellent for coastlines.
Feeding
A light balanced fertilizer once in spring; avoid overfeeding.
Pruning & grooming
Remove dead leaves at the base using thick gloves; cut flower stalks after bloom.
Propagation
By stem cuttings or basal offsets; let cut end callus before planting.
Mature size
2–5 m tall with age; trunk develops over time.
Growth rate
Slow to moderate.
Lifespan
Very long-lived — decades.
Bloom / fruit season
White to cream bell-shaped flowers on tall panicles in summer.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to about −10 °C once established; very wind-tolerant.
Toxicity & safety
Leaves are sharp; sap mildly irritant; the plant can cause injury — keep clear of paths and play areas.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A light balanced fertilizer once in spring is enough; over-fertilizing causes excessive soft growth prone to pests.  Find fertilizer →

Brown leaf tipsUnderwatering or dry air in peak summer.Water more thoroughly in summer; mulch around the base to retain moisture.
Yellowing lower leavesNormal — old leaves naturally die off; overwatering if widespread.Remove dead leaves; if widespread, reduce watering.
Scale insects on leavesCommon in warm, dry summers.Wipe with damp cloth or apply neem oil spray.
Availability: Available in Tunisian garden centres and landscape nurseries; popular along the coastal regions.
