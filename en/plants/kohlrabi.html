Kohlrabi

Brassica oleracea var. gongylodes  ·  Family: Brassicaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Kohlrabi  ·  FR Chou-rave  ·  AR كرنب لفتي  ·  Brassica oleracea var. gongylodes
Origin: Northern Europe and Mediterranean (developed from wild cabbage)
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A fast-maturing crunchy cool-season vegetable that produces an edible swollen stem in as little as 50-60 days. Easier than most brassicas and well-suited to Tunisian autumn and spring gardens.
Light
Full sun; tolerates brief partial shade.
Water
Moderate; keep soil evenly moist; irregular watering causes the stem to crack.
Soil
Fertile, well-drained loam; slightly alkaline pH (6.5-7); work in compost before planting.
Temperature
Cool-season crop; sow in Sep-Oct for a winter crop, or Feb-Mar for a spring crop.
Humidity
Average; avoid very high humidity which favours fungal diseases.
Feeding
Balanced fertilizer before planting; one nitrogen-rich top-dressing at mid-growth.
Pruning & grooming
Remove older yellowing lower leaves; harvest before the stem exceeds 8-10 cm diameter.
Propagation
By seed sown directly or transplanted; thin to 15-20 cm spacing.
Mature size
Leaves 30-50 cm; edible swollen stem 5-10 cm diameter.
Growth rate
Fast; 50-70 days to harvest.
Lifespan
Annual.
Bloom / fruit season
Yellow flowers in second year; not desired in cultivation.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to -5 C; tolerates light frost.
Toxicity & safety
Fully edible and non-toxic.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Incorporate compost before planting and apply a balanced fertilizer at the start; one nitrogen-rich top-dressing at mid-growth is usually sufficient for fast even development of the swollen stem.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Swollen stem cracks or splitsIrregular watering, especially after dry spells.Water consistently; mulch to retain moisture.
Leaves with irregular holesCaterpillars (cabbage white butterfly larvae) or slugs.Hand-pick caterpillars; cover with fine mesh; use slug repellent if needed.
Woody tough fleshHarvested too late; stem exceeds 10 cm diameter.Harvest when stems are tennis-ball sized (6-8 cm); do not delay.
Availability: Seeds available in Tunisian nurseries and markets in autumn and late winter; the vegetable is not commonly known in traditional Tunisian cuisine but is gaining interest.
