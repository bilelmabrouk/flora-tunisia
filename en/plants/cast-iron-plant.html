Cast iron plant

Aspidistra elatior  ·  Family: Asparagaceae
Interior · Foliage
EN Cast iron plant  ·  FR Aspidistra  ·  AR أسبيديسترا  ·  Aspidistra elatior
Origin: China and Japan

Setting
Indoor
In short The ultimate low-maintenance houseplant — tolerates deep shade, dust, irregular watering, and Tunisian summer heat indoors without complaint.
Light
Deep to moderate shade; one of the best plants for dim corners. Avoid direct sun.
Water
Water every 1–2 weeks; let soil nearly dry between waterings. Very drought-tolerant.
Soil
Any well-draining universal potting mix.
Temperature
Adaptable: 7–27 °C; survives occasional cold but dislikes extreme heat above 30 °C.
Humidity
Tolerates dry air; no special humidity needed.
Feeding
Light feeding — balanced fertilizer once a month in spring and summer only.
Pruning & grooming
Cut off old yellowed leaves at the base; no other pruning required.
Propagation
Division of rhizomes at repotting in spring.
Mature size
50–70 cm tall, slowly spreading clump.
Growth rate
Very slow.
Lifespan
Decades — extremely long-lived.
Bloom / fruit season
Rarely; tiny purple flowers at soil level, usually hidden.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to about −5 °C; fine in all Tunisian indoor environments.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Feed sparingly — a balanced liquid fertilizer at half strength once a month in spring and summer is plenty; over-feeding causes weak floppy growth.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellow leavesOver-watering or waterlogged soil.Allow soil to dry more; check drainage holes are clear.
Brown tipsDry air, fluoride in water, or physical damage.Use settled tap water or filtered water; avoid brushing leaves.
Pale streaks on leavesToo much direct sunlight.Move to a shadier position.
Availability: Commonly grown and sold in Tunisia; available in garden centres and specialist nurseries.
