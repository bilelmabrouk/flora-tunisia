Evergreen clematis

Clematis armandii  ·  Family: Ranunculaceae
Exterior · Climber
EN Evergreen clematis  ·  FR Clématite d'Armand  ·  AR كليماتيس  ·  Clematis armandii
Origin: Central and western China

Water

Setting
Outdoor
In short An elegant evergreen clematis with large glossy leaves and white vanilla-scented flowers in late winter — a refined choice for shaded walls and pergolas in northern Tunisia.
Light
Sun to part shade; roots need shade, top in sun — classic clematis rule.
Water
Moderate; keep root zone cool and moist. Mulch heavily.
Soil
Fertile, humus-rich, well-drained, slightly alkaline soil.
Temperature
Hardy to about −10 °C; best in northern and north-west Tunisia.
Humidity
Benefits from moderate humidity; may struggle in very arid areas.
Feeding
Monthly spring–summer with a flowering-plant fertilizer.
Pruning & grooming
Prune lightly after flowering (Group 1 clematis); remove dead or weak stems only.
Propagation
Layering or semi-ripe cuttings in summer.
Mature size
Climbs 4–8 m.
Growth rate
Moderate to fast.
Lifespan
Long-lived perennial climber.
Bloom / fruit season
February–April; fragrant white flowers in clusters.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −10 °C; protect from prolonged hard frost.
Toxicity & safety
All parts toxic if eaten; sap irritates skin — wear gloves.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced flowering-plant fertilizer applied monthly from late winter to midsummer encourages abundant bloom; stop feeding in autumn.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Clematis wilt (sudden collapse)Fungal disease (Phoma clematidina) entering via stem wound.Cut back to healthy tissue below ground; disinfect tools; new shoots usually re-grow.
Leaf edges browning and dryingDry hot wind or root moisture stress.Mulch root zone; water deeply; provide windbreak if in exposed site.
No flowersIncorrect pruning (cutting off flowering wood) or too much shade.Only prune lightly after bloom; move to a sunnier spot.
Availability: Available from specialist nurseries in Tunisia; best in the north.
