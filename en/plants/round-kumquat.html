Round kumquat

Fortunella japonica  ·  Family: Rutaceae
Trees · Citrus
EN Round kumquat  ·  FR Kumquat rond  ·  AR كمكوات  ·  Fortunella japonica
Origin: China (Guangdong region)
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short The round kumquat is a tough little citrus whose tiny sweet-skinned fruits are eaten whole — peel and all. Well-suited to coastal Tunisian gardens in a sunny pot or border.
Light
Full sun — at least 6–8 h daily for good fruiting and flavour development.
Water
Moderate: water when the top few cm of soil dry out; avoid waterlogging, especially in pots.
Soil
Slightly acidic, well-drained loam or citrus mix; pH 5.5–6.5 ideal.
Temperature
Hardy to about −5 °C once established; thrives at 15–30 °C.
Humidity
Tolerates coastal humidity well; dislikes very dry heated air indoors.
Feeding
Every 3–4 weeks in growing season with a citrus fertilizer; monthly in autumn, stop in winter.
Pruning & grooming
Minimal — thin crowded interior branches after harvest to maintain airflow.
Propagation
Semi-hardwood cuttings in summer; grafting is most reliable for fruiting.
Mature size
1.5–3 m in the garden; 1–1.5 m in a pot.
Growth rate
Slow to moderate.
Lifespan
Long-lived; 20–30 years with proper care.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring flush (April–June); orange-red fruits ripen in late autumn–winter.
Hardiness (min temp)
More cold-tolerant than most citrus; withstands brief frosts to −5 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; fruits are edible whole.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A citrus-formula fertilizer (with nitrogen, potassium, and chelated iron) applied every 3–4 weeks during spring and summer promotes healthy foliage and a heavy fruit set.  Find fertilizer →

SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellowing leaves, poor growthIron or nitrogen deficiency, often from alkaline soil.Apply iron chelate and acidify soil; check drainage.
Sticky honeydew and sooty mould on leavesAphids, scale insects, or mealybugs.Spray with neem oil or diluted soap solution; remove by hand if few.
Fruits stay small and taste bitterInsufficient light or potassium deficiency.Ensure full sun; supplement with potassium-rich fertilizer.
Availability: Available in Tunisian garden centres, mainly in spring; less common than lemon but found in most coastal towns.
