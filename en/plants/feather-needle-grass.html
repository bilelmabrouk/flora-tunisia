Feather/needle grass

Stipa sp.  ·  Family: Poaceae
Exterior · Ornamental grass
EN Feather/needle grass  ·  FR Stipe  ·  AR ستيبا  ·  Stipa sp.
Origin: Mediterranean basin and Eurasian steppe; several species native to Tunisia
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A genus of graceful drought-hardy feather grasses native to Tunisia, producing shimmering plumes in spring and early summer — perfect for naturalistic, low-maintenance Tunisian landscapes.
Light
Full sun; adapted to open, exposed Mediterranean habitats.
Water
Very low; native to dry and semi-arid zones; watering only during establishment; rain-fed thereafter.
Soil
Well-drained to dry; limestone, sandy or stony soils; native to poor rocky Tunisian soils.
Temperature
Hardy to severe frost; thrives in all Tunisian temperature extremes.
Humidity
Highly tolerant of dry air; found naturally in arid Tunisian steppes.
Feeding
No fertilizer needed for native species in the ground; avoid rich soils.
Pruning & grooming
Light grooming in late winter; remove old flower stalks after seed dispersal.
Propagation
By seed collected fresh in summer and sown in autumn or spring; by careful division.
Mature size
Variable 30–100 cm depending on species; airy upright clumps.
Growth rate
Moderate; established plants are long-lived.
Lifespan
Long-lived perennial if undisturbed.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring to early summer: elegant long-awned or feathery spikelets that catch the light.
Hardiness (min temp)
Fully hardy across all Tunisian regions.
Toxicity & safety
Generally non-toxic; some species' sharp awns can lodge in animal fur or skin.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Native Stipa species need no fertilizer in the ground; in containers, a very light balanced feed once every two years is enough.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Clump yellows in summerNatural summer semi-dormancy; also overwatering in heat.Reduce watering in summer; the grass recovers in autumn. Good drainage is key.
Sharp awns stuck in pet fur or clothingNormal dispersal mechanism of feather grass seeds.Keep pets and children away during and after seeding; remove plumes before seed matures if needed.
No recovery after transplantingStipa dislikes root disturbance; best planted from containers.Always transplant when young from containers; water well at planting and shade for a week.
Availability: Native species sometimes available through forestry nurseries or specialist wildflower suppliers; ornamental cultivars in larger garden centres.
