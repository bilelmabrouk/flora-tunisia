Rose grape

Medinilla magnifica  ·  Family: Melastomataceae
Interior · Flowering
EN Rose grape  ·  FR Médinille  ·  AR ميدينيلا  ·  Medinilla magnifica
Origin: Philippines (tropical montane forest)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Moderate
Care
Demanding
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Indoor
In short One of the most spectacular indoor flowering plants — cascading clusters of pink flowers hanging beneath large glossy leaves. Demanding of humidity and warmth, but unforgettable in bloom. Best suited to humid coastal Tunisian homes.
Light
Bright indirect light; no direct sun. A bright north or east-facing window is ideal.
Water
Keep soil consistently moist during growing season; reduce slightly in winter. Never let dry out completely.
Soil
Rich, well-aerated mix with perlite and orchid bark; very good drainage essential.
Temperature
18–28 °C; do not allow below 15 °C. A brief cooler period (15–18 °C) in autumn encourages spring blooms.
Humidity
Needs high humidity (60–80%); use a humidifier or group with other plants — critical in dry Tunisian summers.
Feeding
Every 2–3 weeks in spring–summer with a high-phosphorus flower fertilizer.
Pruning & grooming
Remove spent flower clusters; cut back untidy stems after blooming.
Propagation
Semi-hardwood cuttings in summer with bottom heat; difficult without controlled conditions.
Mature size
60 cm–1 m tall and wide as a pot plant.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Long-lived with correct care; several years.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring (April–June); cascading pendant panicles of pink-rose flowers.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; minimum 15 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A phosphorus-rich fertilizer every 2–3 weeks from late winter through summer promotes the spectacular bloom; reduce to once a month in autumn and stop in winter.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Flower buds drop without openingLow humidity or sudden temperature change.Maintain stable warm temperatures and high humidity; do not move plant when in bud.
Yellow leavesOverwatering or chlorosis from low humidity.Check watering; boost humidity; check for root rot.
Mealybugs in leaf axilsCommon on this species, especially in dry indoor air.Dab with alcohol-soaked cotton; treat with neem oil.
Availability: A specialist plant; found in select Tunisian plant shops, mainly in larger coastal cities.
