Bottle gourd

Lagenaria siceraria  ·  Family: Cucurbitaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Bottle gourd  ·  FR Calebasse  ·  AR قرعة حمراء  ·  Lagenaria siceraria
Origin: Africa and South Asia
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A fast-climbing cucurbit grown for its large edible gourds, widely cultivated across Tunisia from spring to autumn. Easy to grow in full sun with regular watering and a sturdy trellis.
Light
Full sun; needs at least 6–8 hours of direct sun daily.
Water
Regular and consistent; keep soil evenly moist, especially during fruit set.
Soil
Rich, well-drained soil with plenty of organic matter; sandy loam is ideal.
Temperature
Warm-season crop; best at 20–35 °C; sensitive to frost.
Humidity
Tolerates dry Mediterranean air; good airflow reduces fungal risk.
Feeding
Feed with a vegetable fertilizer every 2–3 weeks from flowering onwards.
Pruning & grooming
Pinch growing tips after 2–3 main laterals develop to encourage fruiting.
Propagation
Direct sow seeds after last frost, or start indoors 3–4 weeks earlier.
Mature size
Vines reach 3–5 m; fruit varies 20–100 cm depending on variety.
Growth rate
Fast — can cover a trellis in 4–6 weeks under warm conditions.
Lifespan
Annual.
Bloom / fruit season
White nocturnal flowers mainly in summer.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; plant only after temperatures stay above 15 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Edible when young; very bitter gourds (cucurbitacin) can cause gastric irritation — taste before eating large amounts.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced vegetable fertilizer (slightly higher in potassium during fruiting) every 2–3 weeks promotes vigorous vines and large gourds.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Powdery white coating on leavesPowdery mildew, common in warm dry spells.Improve airflow; spray with dilute baking-soda solution; remove badly affected leaves.
Wilting despite regular wateringRoot rot from waterlogging, or cucumber beetle damage.Improve drainage; check roots for rot; inspect stems for insect damage.
Fruit fails to setPoor pollination (not enough bees or hand-pollination needed).Hand-pollinate by transferring pollen with a small brush between male and female flowers at dawn.
Yellow mosaic pattern on leavesCucumber mosaic virus spread by aphids.Remove and destroy infected plants; control aphid populations.
Availability: Seeds widely available in Tunisian markets and garden centres; easy to find in season.
