Chinese silver grass

Miscanthus sinensis  ·  Family: Poaceae
Exterior · Ornamental grass
EN Chinese silver grass  ·  FR Herbe d'argent  ·  AR عشب الفضة  ·  Miscanthus sinensis
Origin: East Asia (China, Japan, Korea); introduced ornamental in Tunisia
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A tall, elegant ornamental grass with autumn plumes and fine arching foliage, best suited to the cooler, more humid northern and coastal Tunisian gardens.
Light
Full sun; at least 6 hours for good plumes and upright growth.
Water
Moderate; more than most ornamental grasses; water weekly in summer heat; drought-tolerant once established.
Soil
Moist, well-drained, moderately fertile loam; adaptable but does not like dry sandy soil in summer.
Temperature
Hardy; tolerates frost but loses foliage in winter; struggles in extreme Tunisian southern summers.
Humidity
Prefers moderate humidity; best in northern and coastal Tunisia.
Feeding
Balanced fertilizer once in spring; avoid excess nitrogen.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back to 15–30 cm in late winter before new growth begins.
Propagation
By division in spring; divisions establish well when kept moist.
Mature size
1.2–3 m tall depending on cultivar; elegant arching clumps.
Growth rate
Moderate to fast once established.
Lifespan
Long-lived perennial; 10+ years with periodic division.
Bloom / fruit season
Feathery silver-to-copper plumes from late summer through winter, very showy.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −20 °C; fully frost-tolerant for Tunisian winters.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced fertilizer applied once in spring is sufficient; excess nitrogen causes floppy growth and reduces the ornamental effect.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Floppy, leaning stemsToo much nitrogen, too much shade, or insufficient summer heat.Full sun location; balanced (not nitrogen-heavy) feed; stake if necessary.
Poor or late plume productionInsufficient summer heat accumulation; common in cooler Tunisian northern sites.Choose a warm, sheltered south-facing aspect; use early-flowering cultivars.
Miscanthus blight or leaf streakFungal diseases in hot humid conditions.Improve airflow; avoid overhead watering; cut back affected foliage.
Availability: Available in Tunisian garden centres, mainly in the north; a wide range of cultivars can be sourced from specialist growers.
