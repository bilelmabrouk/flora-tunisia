Corn poppy

Papaver rhoeas  ·  Family: Papaveraceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN Corn poppy  ·  FR Coquelicot  ·  AR شقائق النعمان  ·  Papaver rhoeas
Origin: Mediterranean basin and western Asia (naturalised across Tunisia)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short Tunisia's iconic wild poppy — its blazing scarlet petals are already a natural spectacle every spring across northern fields and roadsides, and it is effortless to grow from a pinch of self-gathered seed.
Light
Full sun — needs direct sun all day.
Water
Low: very drought-tolerant once established; avoid over-watering.
Soil
Poor to moderately fertile, well-drained soil; rich soil produces foliage at the expense of flowers.
Temperature
Cool-season annual; best at 10–22 °C; fades in Tunisian summer heat.
Humidity
Tolerates dry conditions well.
Feeding
Rarely needed; thin soil actually improves flowering.
Pruning & grooming
Deadhead to extend season; allow some capsules to ripen and self-seed.
Propagation
Direct sow on the surface in autumn; germinates in cool weather. Do not transplant.
Mature size
40–60 cm tall, 20–30 cm wide.
Growth rate
Fast in cool season.
Lifespan
Annual — completes cycle in spring; self-seeds prolifically.
Bloom / fruit season
March to June in Tunisia; peaks April–May.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy annual; tolerates frost well.
Toxicity & safety
All parts mildly toxic if ingested; latex can irritate skin.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Fertilizer is counterproductive — poppies flower best in lean soil; skip feeding entirely or at most apply a single very light balanced dose at sowing time.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Plants too leafy, few flowersSoil too rich or too much nitrogen.Do not feed; move to poorer soil next season.
Poor germinationSeeds buried too deep or soil too warm at sowing.Scatter seed on the surface and press lightly; sow in autumn when soil is cooler.
Downy mildew patches on leavesFungal disease favoured by damp, cool, poorly ventilated conditions.Thin out crowded seedlings; improve airflow; remove affected leaves promptly.
Availability: Sold as seed packets in Tunisian markets and garden shops; also self-seeds freely in fields and roadsides across the north.
