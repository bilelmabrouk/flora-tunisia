White wormwood

Artemisia herba-alba  ·  Family: Asteraceae
Exterior · Herb / aromatic
EN White wormwood  ·  FR Armoise blanche  ·  AR شيح أبيض  ·  Artemisia herba-alba
Origin: Mediterranean basin & Middle East
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short A native Tunisian steppe shrub with silvery-white aromatic foliage, extraordinarily drought- and heat-resistant. Widely used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent across North Africa.
Light
Full sun all day; native to open steppes and hillsides.
Water
Extremely drought-tolerant; water once every 2–3 weeks in summer; almost nothing in winter.
Soil
Poor, stony or sandy well-drained soil; naturally adapted to Tunisian arid soils.
Temperature
Tolerates extreme heat (45 °C) and moderate frost (−10 °C); thrives across all Tunisian climates.
Humidity
Thrives in low humidity; dislikes prolonged dampness.
Feeding
No fertilizer required; excess nutrients cause rank growth and reduce aromatic oils.
Pruning & grooming
Light trim after flowering to maintain shape; remove old woody stems in early spring.
Propagation
By seed or semi-ripe cuttings in late summer.
Mature size
30–70 cm tall and wide.
Growth rate
Slow to moderate.
Lifespan
Perennial; 5–10 years under good conditions.
Bloom / fruit season
Tiny yellowish flowers July–September; often removed for appearance.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −10 °C; one of the most resilient herbs for inland and southern Tunisia.
Toxicity & safety
Toxic in large internal doses; essential oil contains thujone. Do not ingest; keep from pets.

Loss of silver colour, green floppy leavesToo much shade or over-fertilization.Move to full sun; stop all fertilization.
Aphids on tipsCommon pest on aromatic plants in spring.Spray with diluted soap water or simply pinch off affected tips.
