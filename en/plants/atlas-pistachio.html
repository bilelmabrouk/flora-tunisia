Atlas pistachio (betoum)

Pistacia atlantica  ·  Family: Anacardiaceae
Trees · Olive / native
EN Atlas pistachio (betoum)  ·  FR Bétoum  ·  AR بطم أطلسي  ·  Pistacia atlantica
Origin: North Africa, the Canary Islands and western Asia; native across much of Tunisia
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A majestic long-lived native tree found from Tunisia's northern hills to its desert oases — exceptionally drought- and heat-tolerant, an important reforestation species and the wild rootstock for cultivated pistachio.
Light
Full sun; one of the most tolerant trees for hot, arid conditions.
Water
Very low; established trees survive on minimal rainfall; one of Tunisia's most drought-tolerant natives.
Soil
Any well-drained soil from rocky limestone to sandy plains; tolerates mild salinity.
Temperature
Wide range: −15 °C to 45 °C; fully adapted to all Tunisian regions.
Humidity
Very tolerant of low humidity and arid conditions.
Feeding
Not required; thrives in poor soils.
Pruning & grooming
Minimal; remove deadwood; tolerates some shaping.
Propagation
From fresh seed soaked 24 h; slow-growing seedlings.
Mature size
6–15 m with a broad spreading crown.
Growth rate
Slow.
Lifespan
Many centuries; venerable old trees are landmarks in Tunisian landscapes.
Bloom / fruit season
March–April; small clusters (dioecious); small dark berries ripen in autumn.
Hardiness (min temp)
Very hardy: −15 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Not toxic; berries edible though small.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) No fertilizer needed; over-feeding young trees can actually slow their development of drought-resistance.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Poor establishment after transplantingDeep taproot disturbed; very slow to recover from root damage.Transplant when very small or grow from seed in situ; water generously for first two seasons.
Galls or resinous growths on branchesGall-forming insects or fungal cankers — minor issue.Prune affected branches; no chemical treatment usually needed.
Sparse foliage in very wet wintersExcess soil moisture in poorly drained soils.Plant on a slope or in well-drained soil; avoid low-lying waterlogged areas.
Availability: Available from Tunisian nurseries specialising in native species; also used in reforestation programmes across the country.
