Tree aeonium

Aeonium arboreum  ·  Family: Crassulaceae
Exterior · Succulent / cactus
EN Tree aeonium  ·  FR Aéonium  ·  AR أيونيوم  ·  Aeonium arboreum
Origin: Canary Islands

Setting
Outdoor
In short A striking Canary Island succulent with glossy rosettes on branching stems — unlike most succulents it grows actively in Tunisia's cool, wet winters and rests in summer heat.
Light
Bright indirect light to full sun in cooler months; shade or shelter in peak summer.
Water
Moderate in autumn–spring (its growing season); very little in summer dormancy.
Soil
Well-drained, gritty compost mix; good drainage is essential.
Temperature
Best at 5–25 °C; dislikes intense heat above 30 °C; tolerates light frost briefly.
Humidity
Moderate; copes with coastal air.
Feeding
A balanced fertilizer once a month in autumn–spring; none in summer.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back leggy stems to encourage branching; remove faded flower heads.
Propagation
By stem cuttings or rosette offsets; leave cut end to dry before planting.
Mature size
60–90 cm tall; branching stems with rosettes at tips.
Growth rate
Moderate in growing season.
Lifespan
Perennial; individual rosettes die after flowering but the plant persists via branching.
Bloom / fruit season
Bright yellow cone-shaped flower clusters at stem tips in late winter–spring.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tolerates light frost down to about −3 °C; best in frost-free coastal areas.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets.

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Rosettes close up and plant looks driedNormal summer dormancy response to heat.Move to shade; reduce watering; the plant will revive in autumn.
Mushy stems at the baseOverwatering in summer or poor drainage.Cut off rotted stems; let dry; repot in fresh mix.
Leggy, etiolated stemsInsufficient light.Move to brighter position; cut back to encourage compact growth.
Availability: Found in garden centres along the northern coast; prized as an ornamental in Tunisian gardens.
