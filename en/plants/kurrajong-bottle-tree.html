Kurrajong / bottle tree

Brachychiton populneus  ·  Family: Malvaceae
Trees · Ornamental / shade
EN Kurrajong / bottle tree  ·  FR Brachychiton  ·  AR شجرة الزجاجة  ·  Brachychiton populneus
Origin: E and SE Australia (inland NSW, Victoria, Queensland)
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short An elegant, semi-evergreen Australian tree with a swollen water-storing trunk and attractive cream-and-red bell flowers in summer. Extremely drought-tolerant, it is ideally suited to Tunisia's drier inland and southern regions.
Light
Full sun; thrives in hot, open positions.
Water
Very low; stores water in the trunk; one of the most drought-tolerant ornamental trees available.
Soil
Well-drained, sandy to loamy; tolerates rocky and poor soils; intolerant of waterlogging.
Temperature
Tolerates heat above 45 °C; light frost down to −5 °C.
Humidity
Perfectly adapted to dry and semi-arid conditions.
Feeding
Seldom needed; a light balanced feed at planting helps establishment.
Pruning & grooming
Minimal; shape lightly in early spring if needed.
Propagation
From seed; soak seeds in hot water for 24 hours before sowing.
Mature size
10–20 m tall; broad spreading canopy.
Growth rate
Moderate — 30–60 cm per year.
Lifespan
Long-lived, centuries.
Bloom / fruit season
Cream-coloured bell flowers with red or spotted interior in summer (May–July).
Hardiness (min temp)
Tolerates light frost; well-suited to coastal and arid southern Tunisia.
Toxicity & safety
Seeds and roots edible; safe for humans and animals.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Kurrajong is very low-maintenance; a balanced fertilizer at planting on poor soil is all that is typically needed; established trees rarely need supplemental feeding.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellowing leaves, slow growth on heavy clayWaterlogging; this species is intolerant of wet roots.Improve drainage or plant on a raised mound; do not over-water.
Leaf drop during stress periodsSemi-deciduous response to drought or cold; tree conserves water.Normal behaviour; the tree will releaf when conditions improve.
Scale insects on branchesScale insect infestation, more common on stressed trees.Scrub with a soapy water solution; ensure adequate watering in heat.
Availability: Available from some Tunisian tree nurseries; growing in popularity in arid and semi-arid landscaping projects.
