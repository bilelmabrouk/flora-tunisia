Crown vetch

Coronilla varia  ·  Family: Fabaceae
Exterior · Ground-cover
EN Crown vetch  ·  FR Coronille  ·  AR إكليل التاج  ·  Coronilla varia
Origin: Europe and western Asia
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short A vigorous, nitrogen-fixing groundcover with clusters of pink pea flowers that quickly stabilises slopes and dry banks in northern and central Tunisia. Low-maintenance but spreads aggressively — best for large areas.
Light
Full sun to light shade; flowers best in full sun.
Water
Low to moderate; drought-tolerant once established; avoid waterlogging.
Soil
Adapts to poor, sandy, clay, or rocky soils; fixes its own nitrogen, so no rich soil needed.
Temperature
Hardy; tolerates frost below −20 °C and summer heat.
Humidity
Tolerates dry air and coastal conditions.
Feeding
No nitrogen fertilizer needed (fixes its own); a light phosphorus feed aids flowering.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back to the ground in late winter to keep tidy and encourage dense regrowth.
Propagation
By seed in spring (scarify first) or by division in spring.
Mature size
30–60 cm tall, spreading unlimited by rhizomes.
Growth rate
Fast and spreading; can be invasive — best contained.
Lifespan
Long-lived perennial; essentially indefinite via spreading rhizomes.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring to late summer (April–August), pink and white pea flowers.
Hardiness (min temp)
Extremely hardy; tolerates hard frost and drought.
Toxicity & safety
Toxic if eaten in quantity; keep away from pets and children.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) No nitrogen fertilizer is needed as this legume fixes atmospheric nitrogen; a light phosphorus-rich application in spring can support more abundant flowering.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Uncontrolled spreading into garden bedsAggressive rhizome spread — natural behaviour.Install a root barrier at planting; mow or cut back margins regularly.
Yellowing leavesPoor drainage or iron deficiency in alkaline soil.Improve drainage; apply chelated iron if soil is highly alkaline.
Aphids on new growthCommon spring pest on legumes.Blast off with water; apply insecticidal soap if severe.
Availability: Occasionally found in Tunisian nurseries mainly in the north; primarily used for erosion control on public slopes.
