Courgette / zucchini

Cucurbita pepo  ·  Family: Cucurbitaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Courgette / zucchini  ·  FR Courgette  ·  AR قرعة / كوسة  ·  Cucurbita pepo
Origin: Americas (Mexico / southern USA)
Light
Full sun
Water
High
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short One of Tunisia's most productive summer vegetables — courgettes grow explosively fast in the heat, needing plenty of water but rewarding with harvests every few days from early summer onward.
Light
Full sun — 6–8 hours minimum.
Water
High; needs consistent, deep watering — never allow the soil to dry out; mulch to retain moisture.
Soil
Rich, well-drained with plenty of organic matter; heavy feeding plant.
Temperature
Best at 18–30 °C; slows in intense heat above 35 °C; frost-tender.
Humidity
Moderate; avoid overhead watering to reduce powdery mildew risk.
Feeding
Heavy feeder; balanced fertilizer at sowing, then weekly liquid feeding during the growing season.
Pruning & grooming
Remove old or yellowing leaves to improve airflow; no heavy pruning needed.
Propagation
Direct sow in warm soil (15 °C+) or start indoors 3–4 weeks before transplanting.
Mature size
Bush varieties: 60–90 cm; trailing varieties can spread 1.5–2 m.
Growth rate
Very fast; first harvest within 45–60 days of sowing.
Lifespan
Annual.
Bloom / fruit season
Large yellow male and female flowers; fruit sets after pollination. Flowers also edible.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; sow in Tunisia from March (south) to April–May (north).
Toxicity & safety
Fully safe; both fruit and flowers are edible.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A heavy feeder — apply a balanced fertilizer at planting, then a weekly liquid vegetable feed throughout the growing season to sustain the rapid fruit production.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Powdery mildew on leavesFungal disease, common in humid or overcrowded conditions.Ensure good spacing; water at the base; spray with diluted sodium bicarbonate or neem oil.
Female flowers drop without setting fruitPoor pollination due to lack of insects or very high temperatures.Hand-pollinate in early morning using a brush; plant in early spring to avoid peak heat during flowering.
Cucumber mosaic virus — mottled, distorted leavesVirus transmitted by aphids.Control aphid populations; remove affected plants promptly; do not compost infected material.
Availability: Seeds and seedlings widely available in Tunisian markets and garden centres in spring; a very popular and widely grown summer vegetable.
