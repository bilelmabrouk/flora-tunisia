Holm oak

Quercus ilex  ·  Family: Fagaceae
Trees · Olive / native
EN Holm oak  ·  FR Chêne vert  ·  AR بلوط أخضر  ·  Quercus ilex
Origin: Mediterranean basin native, including northern Tunisia
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A majestic native evergreen oak that defines Tunisia's northern forests and hillsides — extremely drought-tolerant, fire-resilient, and virtually care-free once established.
Light
Full sun to light shade; very adaptable once established.
Water
Very low once established; survives on rainfall alone across most of its range.
Soil
Adaptable: limestone, clay, sandy or rocky soils; prefers well-drained ground.
Temperature
Tolerates −15 °C; also withstands hot summers; fully adapted to all Tunisian northern and central zones.
Humidity
Tolerates dry summers and moderate winter humidity.
Feeding
None required; a general fertilizer at planting helps young trees.
Pruning & grooming
Requires minimal pruning; remove deadwood; tolerates clipping into hedges.
Propagation
From fresh acorns sown immediately after collection; germination in 2–4 weeks.
Mature size
10–25 m in forest; often multi-stemmed shrub on dry hillsides.
Growth rate
Slow — 30–50 cm per year.
Lifespan
Hundreds of years; exceptional trees exceed 500 years.
Bloom / fruit season
April–May; inconspicuous catkins (wind-pollinated).
Hardiness (min temp)
Very hardy: tolerates −15 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Acorns mildly toxic to livestock in large quantities; safe for humans in small doses.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Established holm oaks need no fertilizer; only young transplants benefit from a slow-release general feed in the first two years.  Find fertilizer →
