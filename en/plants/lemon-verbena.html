Lemon verbena

Aloysia citriodora  ·  Family: Verbenaceae
Exterior · Herb / aromatic
EN Lemon verbena  ·  FR Verveine citronnée  ·  AR لويزة  ·  Aloysia citriodora
Origin: South America (Chile, Argentina, Peru)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short Lemon verbena — known in Tunisia as لويزة — is the quintessential Tunisian herbal tea shrub, its intensely citrus-scented leaves cherished for digestion and calm. A sun-loving, drought-tolerant shrub that thrives on benign neglect along the coast.
Light
Full sun — at least 6 hours; more sun intensifies the lemon scent.
Water
Low: drought-tolerant once established; water deeply but infrequently.
Soil
Well-drained, light to moderately fertile; sandy or loamy soils work well.
Temperature
Best at 15–30 °C; damaged by frost — protect or cut back in cold winters.
Humidity
Tolerates dry coastal air well; avoid waterlogging in high-humidity areas.
Feeding
Light balanced feed in spring; avoid heavy feeding which dilutes the aroma.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back hard in late winter/early spring before new growth — it responds well.
Propagation
Semi-hardwood cuttings in summer root easily; or from seed (slow).
Mature size
1–3 m tall; can be kept compact by annual hard pruning.
Growth rate
Moderate; becomes a substantial shrub in 2–3 years.
Lifespan
Long-lived if frost-protected; can persist for decades on the coast.
Bloom / fruit season
Small white to pale lilac flowers in small spikes, late summer (July–September).
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender: damaged below −5 °C; cut back but often reshoots from roots.
Toxicity & safety
Safe; widely used for herbal teas; avoid very large medicinal doses during pregnancy.
