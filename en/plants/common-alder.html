Common alder

Alnus glutinosa  ·  Family: Betulaceae
Trees · Olive / native
EN Common alder  ·  FR Aulne glutineux  ·  AR قطلب مائي  ·  Alnus glutinosa
Origin: Europe and North Africa, including northern Tunisia along streams and wetlands
Light
Full sun
Water
High
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A native nitrogen-fixing riparian tree that stabilises stream banks and enriches soil; best suited to the humid north around Jendouba, Béja and Bizerte.
Light
Full sun to light shade; grows best in the open.
Water
High; requires consistently moist to wet soil; naturally grows at water margins.
Soil
Heavy, waterlogged or at least permanently moist; tolerates clay and silt.
Temperature
Hardy; tolerates hard frost and mild Tunisian winters in the north.
Humidity
Requires high humidity; not suited to semi-arid or arid zones.
Feeding
Fixes its own nitrogen via root nodules; a light phosphorus boost in spring can help.
Pruning & grooming
Prune in late autumn or winter; tolerates coppicing to renew the canopy.
Propagation
From seed sown fresh in autumn or stratified and sown in spring; also by cuttings.
Mature size
15–25 m tall; narrow-oval canopy.
Growth rate
Moderate to fast near water.
Lifespan
60–150 years.
Bloom / fruit season
Catkins appear in late winter before the leaves; male catkins elongated, female small.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −25 °C; no issue in northern Tunisia.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and animals.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) This nitrogen-fixer rarely needs nitrogen fertilizer; a light phosphorus and potassium application in spring helps young trees establish.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellowing leaves with dark spotsPhytophthora root rot in waterlogged but poorly aerated soils.Ensure soil is waterlogged but flowing; avoid stagnant conditions; remove affected roots.
Leaf miner trails on foliageAlder leaf miner larvae (Fenusa dohrnii).Pick off mined leaves; healthy, well-watered trees usually cope without treatment.
Tree decline and branch dieback near dry sitesWater stress; alder is not drought-tolerant.Relocate or ensure constant moisture; not suitable for dry or arid Tunisian zones.
Availability: Mostly available through forestry nurseries in northern Tunisia; less common in general garden centres.
