Barbary thuja / sandarac

Tetraclinis articulata  ·  Family: Cupressaceae
Trees · Olive / native
EN Barbary thuja / sandarac  ·  FR Thuya de Barbarie  ·  AR عرعار  ·  Tetraclinis articulata
Origin: Maghreb endemic (NW Africa and SE Spain)
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A slow-growing, fragrant Maghrebi conifer valued for its beautiful burl wood used in Tunisian crafts. Drought-tough and long-lived, it thrives in stony soils across northern and central Tunisia.
Light
Full sun; thrives on dry, sunny rocky slopes.
Water
Very low; extremely drought-tolerant; water young trees only until established.
Soil
Poor, stony, calcareous, well-drained; intolerant of waterlogging.
Temperature
Hardy to −10 °C; handles both hot summers and cold winters.
Humidity
Prefers dry conditions; tolerates semi-arid environments.
Feeding
Rarely needed; light fertilizer only for young containerised plants.
Pruning & grooming
Minimal; tolerates light shaping; avoid heavy pruning into old wood.
Propagation
From seed in spring; germination slow and irregular.
Mature size
5–15 m; often shrubby in dry conditions.
Growth rate
Very slow.
Lifespan
Centuries; known specimens exceed 1000 years.
Bloom / fruit season
Small cones; not ornamental.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −10 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; aromatic resin used traditionally.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) This species thrives on neglect; no regular fertilizing needed. Young plants benefit from one light balanced feed in spring for the first 2 years.  Find fertilizer →
