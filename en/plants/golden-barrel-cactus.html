Golden barrel cactus ⇄ also: Interior

Echinocactus grusonii  ·  Family: Cactaceae
Exterior · Succulent / cactus
EN Golden barrel cactus  ·  FR Coussin de belle-mère  ·  AR صبّار البرميل الذهبي  ·  Echinocactus grusonii
Origin: Central Mexico
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Indoor & out
In short An iconic golden-spined ball cactus that thrives on Tunisian sun and neglect; near-indestructible outdoors in the centre and south, and a striking pot specimen.
Light
Full sun; the more the better.
Water
Very little; water deeply then let dry fully. Almost none in winter.
Soil
Gritty, very free-draining cactus mix.
Temperature
Loves heat; happy to 40 °C+.
Humidity
Prefers dry air.
Feeding
A dilute cactus feed once or twice in summer only.
Pruning & grooming
None needed.
Propagation
From seed (slow) or offsets; rarely offsets when young.
Mature size
To 40–80 cm across over many years.
Growth rate
Very slow.
Lifespan
Very long-lived — decades.
Bloom / fruit season
Yellow flowers only on old, large plants in summer.
Hardiness (min temp)
Keep above ~0–2 °C and dry; damaged by frost.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic, but the spines are sharp — site away from paths and children.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A dilute low-nitrogen cactus fertilizer once or twice in summer is ample.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Soft, brown baseOver-watering / cold-wet rot.Stop watering; keep warm and dry; cut to firm tissue if needed.
Corky brown patchesSunscald after sudden full sun, or age.Acclimatise to sun gradually; cosmetic only.
No growthNormal — it is naturally very slow.Be patient; ensure sun and summer warmth.
Availability: Commonly sold in Tunisian garden centres and cactus growers as a popular ornamental.
