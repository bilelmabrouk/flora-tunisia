Fenugreek

Trigonella foenum-graecum  ·  Family: Fabaceae
Exterior · Herb / aromatic
EN Fenugreek  ·  FR Fenugrec  ·  AR حلبة  ·  Trigonella foenum-graecum
Origin: Southern Europe & Western Asia (widely cultivated since antiquity)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A fast-growing annual herb prized in Tunisian kitchens for its aromatic seeds and edible leaves. Thrives in sunny spots with little fuss, fixing nitrogen in the soil as it grows.
Light
Full sun; requires at least 6 hours of direct sunlight daily.
Water
Moderate; water once or twice a week in summer; reduce in cooler weather. Dislikes waterlogging.
Soil
Well-drained, loamy to sandy soil; tolerates poor soils well. Slightly alkaline preferred.
Temperature
Best at 10–28 °C; a cool-season crop in Tunisia — sow autumn to early spring in most regions.
Humidity
Low to moderate; does not need humid conditions.
Feeding
Light nitrogen-fixing crop; little fertilizer needed — a balanced feed at planting is sufficient.
Pruning & grooming
Pinch tips to encourage bushiness; harvest leaves regularly for cooking.
Propagation
Direct-sow seed; germinates in 3–5 days. Sow in autumn or spring.
Mature size
30–60 cm tall.
Growth rate
Fast — harvest ready in 3–4 weeks for leaves, 4–5 months for seeds.
Lifespan
Annual — completes lifecycle in one season.
Bloom / fruit season
Small white flowers in spring (March–May); followed by narrow seed pods.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tolerates light frost; performs best between 10–25 °C. Avoid intense summer heat.
Toxicity & safety
Safe and edible; widely used as food and herbal medicine. Avoid very large quantities during pregnancy.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Being a legume, fenugreek fixes its own nitrogen; a small amount of phosphorus-rich fertilizer at sowing helps root development and seed set.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Powdery mildew (white coating)Warm humid conditions with poor air circulation.Space plants well; water at the base; remove affected leaves.
Yellowing leaves, slow growthOverwatering or iron deficiency in alkaline soil.Improve drainage; apply chelated iron if soil is very alkaline.
Aphids clustering on shootsSoft spring growth attracts aphids.Spray with diluted soap solution; ladybirds are natural predators.
Availability: Widely available as seeds in Tunisian spice markets and garden centres; very commonly grown in home gardens and on small farms.
