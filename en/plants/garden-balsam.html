Garden balsam

Impatiens balsamina  ·  Family: Balsaminaceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN Garden balsam  ·  FR Balsamine  ·  AR حنّة بنت  ·  Impatiens balsamina
Origin: South and Southeast Asia (India, Myanmar, China)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Moderate
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short An old-fashioned but charming summer annual known as حنّة بنت in Tunisia — produces camellia-like flowers nestled in the foliage from spring to autumn in shaded to semi-shaded spots.
Light
Bright partial shade to dappled sun; tolerates more sun than Impatiens walleriana with adequate watering.
Water
Moderate to high: keep soil evenly moist; wilts if allowed to dry out; daily watering in hot months.
Soil
Humus-rich, moisture-retaining but well-drained soil; mulch helps.
Temperature
Ideal 20–30 °C; withstands Tunisia's summer heat in shaded spots; frost kills it.
Humidity
Moderate to high; benefits from afternoon shade and mulched roots in summer.
Feeding
Balanced liquid fertilizer every 2–3 weeks from germination through flowering.
Pruning & grooming
Pinch seedlings at 10 cm for bushiness; generally self-branching.
Propagation
From seed sown indoors in spring or directly after last frost; self-seeds readily.
Mature size
30–70 cm tall, 30 cm wide.
Growth rate
Fast — flowers in 8–10 weeks from seed.
Lifespan
Annual; self-seeds and reappears year after year.
Bloom / fruit season
Late spring to early autumn.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; sow when temperatures consistently above 18 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans; parts may cause mild skin irritation.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced flowering-plant fertilizer every 2–3 weeks from first bud sustains the long bloom season; avoid excess nitrogen which elongates stems and reduces flower density.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Wilting in the afternoon heatToo much direct afternoon sun combined with insufficient water.Provide afternoon shade; water more frequently; mulch the soil surface.
Powdery white coating on leavesPowdery mildew in humid, poorly ventilated conditions.Improve airflow; thin out dense plantings; remove infected leaves.
Seed pods bursting and scattering seeds earlyNormal ripening behaviour — the plant's explosive seed dispersal mechanism.Collect ripe pods in a bag before they burst if you want to save seed; otherwise let it self-seed.
Availability: Sold as seed packets in Tunisian markets and garden shops, especially in traditional neighbourhoods; self-seeds readily once established.
