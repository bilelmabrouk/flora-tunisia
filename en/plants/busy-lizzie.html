Busy Lizzie ⇄ also: Interior

Impatiens walleriana  ·  Family: Balsaminaceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN Busy Lizzie  ·  FR Impatiente  ·  AR بلسمينة  ·  Impatiens walleriana
Origin: East Africa (Tanzania and Mozambique)
Light
Shade / indirect
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Indoor & out
In short The unrivalled choice for shaded spots in Tunisian gardens — continuous vivid flowers in pinks, reds, and whites from spring to autumn, asking only for regular watering and shade.
Light
Partial to full shade; tolerates bright indirect light; direct midday sun wilts and bleaches flowers.
Water
Moderate to high: keep soil consistently moist; wilts quickly when dry; water daily in hot weather.
Soil
Moist, humus-rich, well-drained; mix in compost for good moisture retention.
Temperature
Ideal 18–25 °C; struggles above 32 °C; frost kills it.
Humidity
Prefers moderate to high humidity; struggles in very dry conditions.
Feeding
Every 2 weeks with a balanced flowering-plant fertilizer in spring and summer.
Pruning & grooming
Pinch growing tips to encourage bushiness; no deadheading necessary (self-cleaning).
Propagation
From stem cuttings (5–7 cm) in water or moist soil; very easy.
Mature size
20–40 cm tall and wide.
Growth rate
Fast.
Lifespan
Tender perennial usually grown as annual; dies in frost.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring to autumn (can bloom almost year-round in mild coastal zones).
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; protect or replace below 5 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced liquid flowering-plant fertilizer every two weeks from spring to autumn keeps the continuous bloom cycle going — skip any formula heavy in nitrogen as it pushes foliage.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Sudden wilting despite moist soilImpatiens Downy Mildew (IDM) — a highly contagious oomycete disease.Remove and destroy infected plants immediately; do not replant impatiens in the same spot.
Spider mites: pale stippling on leavesSpider mite infestation, worse in hot, dry conditions.Raise humidity by misting; rinse leaves with water; use insecticidal soap if severe.
Leggy stems with sparse leavesInsufficient light or plant becoming leggy over the season.Pinch back stems to encourage bushiness; move to brighter indirect light.
Availability: One of the most widely sold bedding annuals in Tunisia, available as young seedlings in nurseries and market stalls every spring.
