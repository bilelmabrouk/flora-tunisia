ZZ plant

Zamioculcas zamiifolia  ·  Family: Araceae
Interior · Foliage
EN ZZ plant  ·  FR Zamioculcas  ·  AR زاميوكولكاس  ·  Zamioculcas zamiifolia
Origin: East Africa (Kenya, Tanzania, Zimbabwe)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Indoor
In short The near-indestructible houseplant — glossy dark-green pinnate leaves and fat underground rhizomes store water, making it perfect for forgetful waterers in any Tunisian home, even dim hallways.
Light
Tolerates low light; best in bright indirect light. Avoid direct harsh sun which scorches leaves.
Water
Extremely drought-tolerant: water every 2–4 weeks in summer, once a month in winter. The rhizomes store water.
Soil
Well-draining mix; a cactus/succulent blend suits well. Never leave in standing water.
Temperature
15–30 °C; tolerates down to 8 °C briefly.
Humidity
Tolerates dry air well — no misting needed.
Feeding
Once every 2 months in spring–summer with a balanced fertilizer; never in winter.
Pruning & grooming
Remove yellowing or damaged stems at the base; no other pruning needed.
Propagation
Leaf cuttings in water or moist soil (slow, several months); or divide rhizomes at repotting.
Mature size
60 cm–1 m tall.
Growth rate
Slow.
Lifespan
Decades — one of the most long-lived houseplants.
Bloom / fruit season
Inconspicuous spadix flowers occasionally; not decorative.
Hardiness (min temp)
Protect from frost; minimum 8 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Toxic if ingested (calcium oxalates); wear gloves when handling sap — keep away from children and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Feed sparingly — a balanced fertilizer at half dose every 2 months in the growing season; over-fertilizing causes salt damage and root burn.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellow leavesOverwatering — the most common mistake.Reduce watering drastically; let soil dry out completely between waterings.
Mushy stem or rhizomeRoot rot from standing water.Unpot, remove rotted parts, dry for a few days, repot in very free-draining soil.
Pale or light green new leavesToo little light or nutrient deficiency.Move to brighter indirect light; consider a light feeding.
Availability: Very widely available across Tunisia; found in plant shops, supermarkets, and online.
