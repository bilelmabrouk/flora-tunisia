Cat's-claw vine

Macfadyena unguis-cati  ·  Family: Bignoniaceae
Exterior · Climber
EN Cat's-claw vine  ·  FR Griffe de chat  ·  AR مخلب القط  ·  Macfadyena unguis-cati
Origin: Tropical America (Mexico to Argentina)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A powerfully self-clinging climber with bright-yellow trumpet flowers in spring; drought-tough and perfect for covering large walls in hot Tunisian coastal and Sahel gardens.
Light
Full sun; needs maximum light for good flowering.
Water
Low to moderate; very drought-tolerant once established.
Soil
Any well-drained soil; tolerates poor, stony ground.
Temperature
Best above 5 °C; frost-sensitive, suited to coastal and southern Tunisia.
Humidity
Tolerates dry and humid air; coastal conditions fine.
Feeding
Light annual feed in spring; extra feeding not needed.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back hard after flowering to limit spread; claw tendrils can damage wall surfaces.
Propagation
Semi-hardwood cuttings in summer; tuberous roots can be divided.
Mature size
10–15 m if unchecked; keep manageable by pruning.
Growth rate
Very fast.
Lifespan
Long-lived; many decades if not controlled.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring (March–May); spectacular yellow display.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-sensitive; avoid cold inland regions.
Toxicity & safety
Generally considered non-toxic to humans and animals.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A light balanced feed once in spring is sufficient; over-fertilizing encourages foliage and reduces flowering.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Peeling wall plasterTendrils grip masonry and eventually damage it.Grow on a purpose-built trellis; remove vine from surfaces not intended for climbing.
Bare stems at baseNatural as the plant ages; lower foliage drops.Underplant with low shrubs to hide the base.
Failure to flowerToo much shade or young plant not yet mature.Relocate to full sun; be patient — new plants may take 2–3 years to bloom.
Availability: Occasionally found in Tunisian garden centres; relatively easy to source.
