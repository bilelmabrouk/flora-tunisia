Horned violet

Viola cornuta  ·  Family: Violaceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN Horned violet  ·  FR Violette cornue  ·  AR بنفسج  ·  Viola cornuta
Origin: Pyrenees mountains (Spain/France)
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A more compact, longer-blooming cousin of the pansy — the horned violet covers beds with small, delicate flowers throughout Tunisia's cool season and tolerates brief frosts better than most.
Light
Full sun to light partial shade — needs at least 5 hours of sun.
Water
Moderate: keep evenly moist; do not let dry out between waterings.
Soil
Rich, moist, well-drained soil; benefits from added compost.
Temperature
Best at 5–18 °C; hardier than garden pansy; fades above 25 °C.
Humidity
Average; ensure good air movement to prevent fungal problems.
Feeding
Balanced fertilizer every 2–3 weeks during the cool growing season.
Pruning & grooming
Deadhead to maintain flowering; cut back hard after main flush to re-sprout.
Propagation
By seed in late summer or by division in autumn.
Mature size
15–25 cm tall, up to 30 cm wide.
Growth rate
Moderate; spreads gently as a short-lived perennial.
Lifespan
Short-lived perennial; often grown as an annual in Tunisia.
Bloom / fruit season
Autumn through spring; longer season than garden pansy.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to about −10 °C; more cold-tolerant than Viola × wittrockiana.
Toxicity & safety
Edible flowers; non-toxic.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced fertilizer applied every two to three weeks during the cool growing season maintains good flowering and healthy foliage.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Pale leaves, slow growthNutrient deficiency, particularly nitrogen.Apply a balanced liquid fertilizer immediately.
Flowers collapsing in heatTemperatures above 22–25 °C.Normal end-of-season decline; pull and compost; replant in September–October.
Grey mould on flowers and stemsBotrytis favoured by damp, poorly ventilated conditions.Remove affected parts; improve spacing and airflow; water at the base.
Availability: Sold as seedling trays and seed packets in Tunisian nurseries from late summer through autumn for the winter bedding season.
