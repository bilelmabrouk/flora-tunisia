Great bougainvillea ⇄ also: climber

Bougainvillea spectabilis  ·  Family: Nyctaginaceae
Exterior · Shrub
EN Great bougainvillea  ·  FR Bougainvillier  ·  AR بوقنفيلية  ·  Bougainvillea spectabilis
Origin: Brazil
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Moderate
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short The larger, more vigorous cousin of B. glabra, producing spectacular masses of magenta to red bracts. Ideal for covering large walls, pergolas and fences in warmer Tunisian regions.
Light
Full sun — minimum 6 hours; essential for flowering.
Water
Moderate; water deeply then allow to dry; deliberate dry spells trigger blooming.
Soil
Well-drained fertile loam; tolerates most soils; avoid waterlogging.
Temperature
Loves heat; best 20–38 °C; damaged below −2 °C. Suited to southern and coastal Tunisia.
Humidity
Average; copes with dry coastal air.
Feeding
Flowering-plant fertilizer every 3–4 weeks in spring and summer.
Pruning & grooming
Hard prune after main flowering flush to control size and encourage new blooming shoots.
Propagation
Semi-hardwood cuttings 15–20 cm in summer.
Mature size
Can reach 8–12 m as a climber; 3–4 m as a shrub.
Growth rate
Very fast.
Lifespan
Very long-lived — decades.
Bloom / fruit season
Peak spring (Mar–May) and autumn (Sep–Nov); more spectacular than B. glabra.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; protect below −2 °C. Best in regions 1, 3, 5, 6.
Toxicity & safety
Thorns cause injury; sap is mildly irritating to skin. Keep from children.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A high-potassium flowering fertilizer every 3–4 weeks in the growing season intensifies bract colour and extends the flowering period.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Failure to bloomOver-watering, too much nitrogen, or insufficient sun.Reduce watering, switch to high-potassium feed, ensure 6+ hours sun.
Severe leaf drop after watering changeStress response to sudden water change — common after transplanting.Maintain consistent watering; plant will recover in 2–4 weeks.
Caterpillars eating leavesMoth caterpillars active in warm season.Pick off by hand at night or use a biological caterpillar treatment.
Availability: Very widely available across Tunisian garden centres and plant markets; sold in a wide range of flower colours.
