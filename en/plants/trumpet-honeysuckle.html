Trumpet honeysuckle

Lonicera sempervirens  ·  Family: Caprifoliaceae
Exterior · Climber
EN Trumpet honeysuckle  ·  FR Chèvrefeuille de Virginie  ·  AR صريمة حمراء  ·  Lonicera sempervirens
Origin: Eastern North America
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Moderate
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short A showstopping semi-evergreen climber bearing long scarlet-orange trumpet flowers from spring to autumn; less invasive than Japanese honeysuckle and a favourite of hummingbird gardeners worldwide.
Light
Full sun to part shade; best flowering and colour in full sun.
Water
Moderate; water regularly in summer, tolerates some drought when established.
Soil
Well-drained, fertile soil; improves with compost.
Temperature
Hardy to −15 °C; perfectly adapted to Tunisia.
Humidity
Tolerates a wide range; adapts to coastal conditions.
Feeding
Balanced fertilizer in spring; supplemental flowering feed in early summer.
Pruning & grooming
Light pruning after each flush; harder cut in late winter to tidy.
Propagation
Semi-hardwood cuttings in summer.
Mature size
3–6 m.
Growth rate
Moderate to fast.
Lifespan
Long-lived perennial; 10–20 years or more.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring to autumn (April–September); repeat-flowering.
Hardiness (min temp)
Very frost-hardy; suitable for all Tunisian regions.
Toxicity & safety
Berries mildly toxic; not intended for consumption.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Apply a balanced fertilizer in spring and a phosphorus-rich flowering feed in early summer to extend the blooming season.  Find fertilizer →
