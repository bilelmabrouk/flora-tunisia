Gardenia ⇄ also: Interior

Gardenia jasminoides  ·  Family: Rubiaceae
Exterior · Shrub
EN Gardenia  ·  FR Gardénia  ·  AR غاردينيا  ·  Gardenia jasminoides
Origin: China, Japan, and Taiwan
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Moderate
Care
Demanding
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Indoor & out
In short The queen of fragrant summer flowers — waxy white blooms with an intoxicating scent from late spring through summer. Demanding but deeply rewarding in sheltered Tunisian coastal gardens or as a container plant in a bright, humid spot.
Light
Bright indirect light or morning sun; intense afternoon sun scorches leaves.
Water
Regular; keep soil consistently moist but not waterlogged; use rain or non-calcareous water.
Soil
Acidic, humus-rich, well-drained; pH 5.0–6.0 essential for good growth.
Temperature
Prefers 16–24 °C; sensitive to drops below 10 °C; avoid frost.
Humidity
High humidity essential; mist leaves regularly or place on a pebble tray with water.
Feeding
Acid fertiliser every 2 weeks from spring through summer; stop in autumn–winter.
Pruning & grooming
Light trim after flowering to keep shape; remove dead or crossing branches.
Propagation
From semi-ripe cuttings in early summer with bottom heat.
Mature size
0.6–1.5 m tall and wide in cultivation.
Growth rate
Slow to moderate.
Lifespan
Long-lived; 20+ years with good care.
Bloom / fruit season
Late spring to summer (May–August); large, intensely fragrant white flowers.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; best in coastal regions 1, 3, 5.
Toxicity & safety
Mildly toxic if ingested; can cause vomiting — keep away from children and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) An acid fertiliser every two weeks during the growing season is essential for healthy foliage and flowering; yellowing leaves in alkaline soil indicate iron deficiency — treat with chelated iron.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellow leaves with green veinsIron or magnesium deficiency in alkaline or hard-water soil.Apply chelated iron; use acidic water for irrigation.
Buds drop before openingLow humidity, temperature fluctuations, or moving the plant during bud set.Stabilise position; raise humidity; maintain 18–22 °C consistently.
Sooty mould and sticky leavesScale insects or mealybugs secreting honeydew.Wipe with soapy water; apply horticultural oil; treat the insect pest at its source.
White powdery coating on leavesPowdery mildew in warm dry conditions.Improve ventilation; reduce dry heat; apply a suitable fungicide.
