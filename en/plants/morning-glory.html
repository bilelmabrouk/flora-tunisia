Morning glory

Ipomoea purpurea  ·  Family: Convolvulaceae
Exterior · Climber
EN Morning glory  ·  FR Ipomée pourpre  ·  AR مجد الصباح  ·  Ipomoea purpurea
Origin: Mexico and Central America
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short A beloved annual climber showering Tunisian summer gardens with purple, pink, red, and white funnel flowers — incredibly easy from seed, fast, and spectacular on fences and trellises.
Light
Full sun; needs at least 6 hours direct sun daily.
Water
Low to moderate; drought-tolerant. Water young plants regularly until established.
Soil
Average to poor, well-drained soil; too much fertility reduces flowering.
Temperature
Annual; sow after last frost; grows best in warm conditions.
Humidity
Tolerates dry to moderately humid conditions.
Feeding
Light monthly feed; avoid excess nitrogen.
Pruning & grooming
Pinch tips when young to encourage branching; otherwise little pruning needed.
Propagation
Direct sow from seed in spring; soak seeds overnight before sowing.
Mature size
Climbs 2–4 m in a season.
Growth rate
Very fast.
Lifespan
Annual (warm-season); self-seeds in favourable conditions.
Bloom / fruit season
May–October; large funnel flowers in shades of purple, pink, red, blue, white.
Hardiness (min temp)
Annual; frost-tender. Sow fresh each spring.
Toxicity & safety
Seeds contain toxic alkaloids; do not eat.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A phosphorus-rich fertilizer applied lightly once a month in summer encourages prolific flowering; high nitrogen produces leafy vines with few flowers.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Germination failureHard seed coat prevents water absorption.Soak seeds in warm water for 24 hours; nick the coat with a file if needed.
Rust or leaf spotFungal disease in humid, overcrowded conditions.Improve spacing and air circulation; remove affected leaves.
Spider mites in hot dry spellsHot, dry summer conditions.Spray leaves with water; insecticidal soap for severe cases.
Availability: Seeds widely available in Tunisia; very popular as a quick seasonal climber for fences and pergolas.
