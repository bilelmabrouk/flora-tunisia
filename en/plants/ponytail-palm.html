Ponytail palm

Beaucarnea recurvata  ·  Family: Asparagaceae
Interior · Foliage
EN Ponytail palm  ·  FR Pied d'éléphant  ·  AR قدم الفيل  ·  Beaucarnea recurvata
Origin: Mexico (semi-arid regions)
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Indoor
In short A striking sculptural houseplant with a swollen water-storing trunk — almost indestructible, perfect for bright Tunisian rooms and forgetful waterers.
Light
Bright direct or indirect sun — the more light, the better.
Water
Very infrequent: every 2–3 weeks in summer, once a month or less in winter. The swollen trunk stores water.
Soil
Sandy, gritty, very well-draining cactus or succulent mix.
Temperature
Thrives at 18–30 °C; tolerates brief dips to 5 °C but prefers warmth.
Humidity
Loves dry air; no misting needed.
Feeding
Once in spring and once in summer with a balanced fertilizer — no more.
Pruning & grooming
Only remove dead leaf tips or brown lower leaves; never cut the growing tip.
Propagation
From offsets (pups) that form at the base, or from seed (very slow).
Mature size
0.5–1.5 m indoors over many years.
Growth rate
Very slow.
Lifespan
Decades — can outlive its owner.
Bloom / fruit season
Rarely flowers indoors; outdoors produces small cream flower clusters.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tolerates brief frost to about −5 °C outdoors; keep above 5 °C indoors.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Feed just twice a year — once in spring and once mid-summer with a balanced liquid fertilizer; this slow grower needs very little nutrition.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Soft or rotting base/trunkOver-watering; waterlogged roots.Stop watering immediately; repot into dry gritty mix removing all rotten material.
Brown leaf tipsNatural ageing or low humidity/dry air.Trim brown tips with clean scissors; no action needed if minor.
Pale or yellowing leavesInsufficient light.Move closer to a bright window or add supplementary light.
Availability: Commonly sold in Tunisian garden centres; popular as an indoor specimen plant.
