Tuberous begonia ⇄ also: Interior

Begonia tuberhybrida  ·  Family: Begoniaceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN Tuberous begonia  ·  FR Bégonia tubéreux  ·  AR بيغونيا درنية  ·  Begonia tuberhybrida
Origin: Andes (hybrid)

Setting
Indoor & out
In short A flamboyant summer-flowering tuberous begonia, best in cool-coastal Tunisia; its large rose-like blooms thrive in bright shade and need a dry winter rest.
Light
Bright indirect light or dappled shade; avoid harsh midday sun in Tunisian summers.
Water
Regular watering while in growth; reduce in autumn, stop completely when foliage dies back.
Soil
Rich, humus-rich, well-drained potting mix; slightly acidic preferred.
Temperature
Best 15–22 °C; sensitive to heat above 30 °C and frost.
Humidity
Moderate to high; mist surroundings but not flowers.
Feeding
Every 2 weeks in spring–summer with a flowering-plant fertilizer.
Pruning & grooming
Remove spent flowers and yellowing leaves; cut stems back after foliage dies.
Propagation
By tuber division in spring, or stem cuttings in summer.
Mature size
20–45 cm tall and wide depending on cultivar.
Growth rate
Moderate during growing season.
Lifespan
Tubers perennial; treat as annual in hot inland areas.
Bloom / fruit season
Late spring through summer (April–September in coastal Tunisia).
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; tubers must be stored dry indoors below 10 °C in winter.
Toxicity & safety
Mildly toxic if eaten (especially tubers); keep away from pets and children.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Apply a high-phosphorus flowering-plant fertilizer every two weeks during active growth to encourage abundant blooms.  Find fertilizer →

SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Stem rot at baseOverwatering or waterlogged soil.Reduce watering; repot with fresh well-drained mix; remove affected tissue.
Powdery mildew on leavesPoor air circulation and high humidity.Space plants for better airflow; avoid wetting foliage.
Flower buds drop before openingHeat stress, drought, or sudden temperature change.Move to a cooler spot; water consistently; avoid cold draughts.
Pale, washed-out leavesToo much direct sun or nutrient deficiency.Move to brighter shade; apply balanced fertilizer.
Availability: Tubers and seedlings sold seasonally in Tunisian garden centres; more common in northern coastal areas.
