Four o'clock flower

Mirabilis jalapa  ·  Family: Nyctaginaceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN Four o'clock flower  ·  FR Belle de nuit  ·  AR لولية الليل  ·  Mirabilis jalapa
Origin: Central and South America
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short A fragrant summer evening favourite, opening at 4 pm in pink, yellow, white or striped blooms that perfume the night air. Extremely tough, drought-tolerant, and thrives in Tunisia's hot summers across all regions.
Light
Full sun; thrives in strong summer sun.
Water
Low to moderate; drought-tolerant; deep tuberous roots store water.
Soil
Adaptable; average to poor, well-drained; tolerates poor urban soils.
Temperature
Loves heat; best 20–35 °C; dies back in frost, but tuberous roots survive mild winters.
Humidity
Low to average; very adaptable.
Feeding
Light feeding once a month in summer with a balanced fertilizer.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back hard in late autumn or winter; will regrow vigorously from the tuberous root.
Propagation
From seed (direct sow in spring) or by dividing tuberous roots.
Mature size
60–100 cm tall and wide; bushy habit.
Growth rate
Fast once established.
Lifespan
Perennial in warm zones (regions 3–6); annual in colder zones.
Bloom / fruit season
Late spring to late autumn (May–October in Tunisia); opens in late afternoon.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tuberous roots hardy to about −5 °C; top growth frost-tender.
Toxicity & safety
All parts toxic if eaten — seeds especially toxic; can cause skin irritation.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A light monthly balanced fertilizer in summer sustains continuous blooming; avoid heavy nitrogen feeding which promotes excess leaf growth.  Find fertilizer →
