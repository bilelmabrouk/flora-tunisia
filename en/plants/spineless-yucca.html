Spineless yucca ⇄ also: Exterior

Yucca elephantipes  ·  Family: Asparagaceae
Interior · Foliage
EN Spineless yucca  ·  FR Yucca  ·  AR يوكا  ·  Yucca elephantipes
Origin: Mexico and Central America
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Indoor & out
In short A drought-tolerant architectural plant with a woody trunk and sword-like leaves — thrives with neglect in bright Tunisian rooms or on a sunny patio. Ideal for busy households or beginners.
Light
Full sun to very bright indirect light; the more sun the better indoors.
Water
Very drought-tolerant: water every 2–3 weeks in summer, once a month in winter. Overwatering kills it.
Soil
Sandy, free-draining mix; a cactus blend works well. Excellent drainage is non-negotiable.
Temperature
Ideal 10–30 °C; tolerates brief cold to 5 °C but not frost.
Humidity
Thrives in dry conditions — perfectly suited to Tunisia's climate.
Feeding
Once in spring and once in summer with a diluted balanced fertilizer.
Pruning & grooming
Remove brown leaf tips and dead lower leaves; cut old flower spikes after blooming.
Propagation
Remove and pot up offsets (pups) from the base; or stem cuttings left to dry before planting.
Mature size
1–2 m indoors; can reach 4–8 m outdoors.
Growth rate
Slow.
Lifespan
Very long-lived — decades.
Bloom / fruit season
Tall spikes of white bell-flowers in summer on mature plants; rare indoors.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tolerates brief light frost; best kept above 5 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Toxic if ingested; sap can irritate skin and eyes — handle with care.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced fertilizer at half dose twice a year (spring and early summer) is all this slow-grower needs; excess nitrogen produces soft, weak leaves.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Soft, mushy stem base or rootsRoot rot from overwatering.Remove from pot, trim rotted tissue, dry thoroughly, replant in fresh dry mix.
Brown leaf tipsLow humidity or fluoride in tap water.Trim tips neatly; use collected rainwater or let tap water stand overnight.
Scale insects on leaves and trunkDry conditions, common on Yucca.Scrape off with a soft brush; treat with neem oil.
Availability: Widely available in Tunisian garden centres and plant shops; a staple of indoor plant collections.
