Green bean

Phaseolus vulgaris  ·  Family: Fabaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Green bean  ·  FR Haricot vert  ·  AR لوبيا خضراء  ·  Phaseolus vulgaris
Origin: Mesoamerica (Central and South America)
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short One of Tunisia's most popular summer vegetables, sown from April to August for a steady harvest of pods in 50–60 days. Bush or climbing varieties suit any garden size.
Light
Full sun; needs 6–8 hours of direct sun for good pod production.
Water
Consistent moisture; water at the base to avoid leaf wetness; critical at flowering.
Soil
Well-drained, fertile soil; prefers slightly acidic pH 6–6.8; avoid heavy clay.
Temperature
Warm-season; optimal 18–28 °C; frost-tender; suffers above 35 °C.
Humidity
Moderate; avoid wetting foliage to prevent fungal problems.
Feeding
Light balanced fertilizer at planting; avoid excess nitrogen which promotes foliage over pods.
Pruning & grooming
No pruning needed for bush types; pinch climbing varieties to encourage lateral branching.
Propagation
Direct sow after last frost, 3–4 cm deep, 10 cm apart; succession-sow every 3 weeks.
Mature size
Bush types 30–50 cm; climbing types 1.5–2.5 m.
Growth rate
Fast; harvest in 50–65 days from sowing.
Lifespan
Annual.
Bloom / fruit season
Small white, yellow, or pink flowers in spring through summer.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; do not plant until soil is consistently above 15 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Safe when cooked; raw beans contain lectins that may cause gastric upset.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A phosphorus-rich fertilizer at sowing and a light potassium top-dress at flowering supports pod development without excess leaf growth.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
White powdery coating on leavesPowdery mildew, worse in warm and dry conditions.Improve spacing for airflow; spray with dilute bicarbonate solution; remove affected leaves.
Rust-coloured pustules on leavesBean rust fungus (Uromyces appendiculatus).Remove affected leaves promptly; ensure good airflow; avoid wetting foliage.
Pods are stringy and toughHarvested too late; high temperatures during pod fill.Harvest when pods are young and pencil-thin; pick regularly to encourage new pods.
Availability: Seeds widely sold in Tunisian markets and garden centres; available year-round in major retailers.
