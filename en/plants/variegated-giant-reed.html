Variegated giant reed

Arundo donax 'Variegata'  ·  Family: Poaceae
Exterior · Ornamental grass
EN Variegated giant reed  ·  FR Canne de Provence panachée  ·  AR قصبة مخططة  ·  Arundo donax 'Variegata'
Origin: Garden cultivar of Arundo donax (Mediterranean species)
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short The cream-and-green striped form of the classic giant reed — less vigorous than the species but equally dramatic, making a bold focal point in sunny Tunisian gardens.
Light
Full sun; variegation is most vivid with maximum sun; part shade dulls the white stripes.
Water
Moderate; keep soil moist but not waterlogged; tolerates short dry spells.
Soil
Fertile, well-drained loam; tolerates most garden soils but dislikes waterlogging.
Temperature
Best at 15–35 °C; cold-hardy to about −5 °C, but white tissues may scorch after hard frost.
Humidity
Tolerates coastal humidity and dry inland air.
Feeding
Light balanced fertilizer once in spring; avoid excess nitrogen which promotes reversion to green.
Pruning & grooming
Cut all canes to ground level in late winter to refresh growth and preserve variegation.
Propagation
Division of clumps in spring; stem cuttings may revert — select from variegated shoots only.
Mature size
2–4 m tall (shorter than plain species); clumping habit.
Growth rate
Fast but slightly slower than plain Arundo donax.
Lifespan
Long-lived perennial.
Bloom / fruit season
Feathery plumes in late summer; less prominent than in the plain species.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −5 °C; protect young plants from severe frost.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A single application of balanced slow-release fertilizer in early spring is sufficient; excess nitrogen risks reverting shoots to plain green.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Shoots reverting to plain greenGenetic reversion, often triggered by high nitrogen or root competition.Remove green shoots at the base immediately; reduce nitrogen feeding.
Leaf scorch on white partsIntense midday sun in summer combined with water stress.Water deeply in summer; provide light afternoon shade during peak heat.
Slow growthPoor soil, waterlogged roots, or insufficient sun.Improve drainage, feed with a balanced fertilizer, and ensure a sunny site.
Availability: Available in Tunisian garden centres and from specialist nurseries; less common than plain Arundo donax.
