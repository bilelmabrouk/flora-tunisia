Wax begonia

Begonia × semperflorens  ·  Family: Begoniaceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN Wax begonia  ·  FR Bégonia  ·  AR بيغونيا  ·  Begonia × semperflorens
Origin: South America (hybrid of Brazilian Begonia species)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Moderate
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short The ultimate no-fuss bedding plant for shaded or semi-shaded Tunisian spots — wax begonia blooms continuously from spring to autumn with tiny red, pink or white flowers and thrives where few other bedding plants survive.
Light
Bright indirect light to light partial shade; also tolerates morning direct sun; avoid harsh midday sun.
Water
Moderate: keep soil evenly moist; allow the top 1–2 cm to dry before re-watering; avoid waterlogging.
Soil
Rich, well-drained, slightly acidic soil; quality pot mix is ideal.
Temperature
Best at 18–28 °C; frost-tender; happy in Tunisian coastal summers if sheltered from intense midday sun.
Humidity
Prefers moderate humidity; misting helps in dry conditions.
Feeding
Balanced flowering-plant fertilizer every 2 weeks during the growing season.
Pruning & grooming
Minimal — remove spent flowers; pinch young plants to encourage bushy growth.
Propagation
From seed (fine, dust-like) in spring, or from stem cuttings.
Mature size
15–35 cm tall, 20–30 cm wide.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Grown as an annual in Tunisia; perennial in frost-free indoor conditions.
Bloom / fruit season
Continuously from spring to autumn in Tunisia.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; protect from temperatures below 10 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Mildly toxic if ingested — the rhizome is the most toxic part; can cause irritation.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced flowering-plant fertilizer every two weeks during the growing season supports the near-continuous blooming habit; avoid high nitrogen which causes lush foliage at the expense of flowers.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Powdery mildew on leavesPoor air circulation in warm, humid conditions.Space plants for airflow; remove infected leaves; treat with diluted bicarbonate solution.
Leggy, elongated stems with few flowersInsufficient light or too much nitrogen.Move to brighter light; reduce nitrogen feeding; pinch back to encourage branching.
Root rot, sudden collapseOver-watering or waterlogged container.Remove from soggy soil; trim rotten roots; repot in fresh, well-drained mix; reduce watering.
Availability: Widely sold as seedling trays and potted plants in Tunisian garden centres from spring.
