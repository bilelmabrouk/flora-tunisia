Purple fountain grass

Pennisetum setaceum 'Rubrum'  ·  Family: Poaceae
Exterior · Ornamental grass
EN Purple fountain grass  ·  FR Pennisetum pourpre  ·  AR الثيوم الأحمر  ·  Pennisetum setaceum 'Rubrum'
Origin: Cultivar of the North African / Southwest Asian species P. setaceum
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A striking ornamental grass with deep burgundy-purple foliage and dark wine-red plumes, adding dramatic colour to Tunisian gardens from summer through autumn.
Light
Full sun essential; leaf colour fades in shade.
Water
Low; drought-tolerant once established; deep watering every 10–14 days in summer.
Soil
Well-drained, moderately fertile; tolerates poor and sandy soils.
Temperature
Warm-season; performs best at 22–38 °C; foliage browns below ~5 °C.
Humidity
Tolerates dry air well; thrives in all Tunisian coastal and inland gardens.
Feeding
Light balanced fertilizer once in spring; high nitrogen reduces the purple colouration.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back to ~15 cm in late winter; remove dead stems for a tidy appearance.
Propagation
By division in spring; sterile cultivar — does not seed true to type.
Mature size
60–90 cm tall; dark arching clump with plumes to 120 cm.
Growth rate
Fast in warm weather.
Lifespan
Short-lived perennial; divide every 3–4 years to keep vigorous.
Bloom / fruit season
Dark burgundy-pink plumes from mid-summer to autumn.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-sensitive; protect or replant if temperatures drop below −3 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; sharp awns may irritate sensitive skin.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Use a balanced fertilizer (not nitrogen-heavy) once in spring; excess nitrogen greens up the foliage and dulls the purple effect.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Foliage turns green instead of purpleToo much shade or too much nitrogen fertilizer.Move to full sun; stop high-nitrogen feeds; use balanced fertilizer only.
Clump dies out in the centreAging clump; natural after 3–5 years.Divide in spring: discard the dead centre, replant the vigorous outer sections.
Crown rot in cold wet wintersWaterlogging combined with cold; this cultivar is slightly more frost-sensitive than the species.Plant in well-drained spot; mulch crown in winter; reduce watering to near zero in cold months.
Availability: Available in well-stocked Tunisian garden centres; often sold as summer seasonal plants.
