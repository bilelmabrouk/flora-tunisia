Avocado

Persea americana  ·  Family: Lauraceae
Trees · Fruit / nut
EN Avocado  ·  FR Avocatier  ·  AR أفوكا  ·  Persea americana
Origin: Central America and Mexico
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Demanding
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short An increasingly cultivated subtropical fruit tree on Tunisia's northern and southern coasts. The avocado rewards careful drainage management and frost protection with creamy, nutritious fruit.
Light
Full sun; at least 6 hours daily; sheltered from cold winds.
Water
Moderate but consistent; deep watering with drying between; very sensitive to waterlogging.
Soil
Light, very well-drained, slightly acidic (pH 5.5–7); add organic matter; avoid clay-heavy soils.
Temperature
Hardy A-grade (Hass) to about −2 °C; needs warmth above 12 °C to grow well.
Humidity
Moderate; low humidity during flowering helps pollination; dislikes very high humidity.
Feeding
Regular feeding with a tropical/citrus fertilizer (with micronutrients) during the growing season.
Pruning & grooming
Light shaping after fruiting; avoid heavy pruning; remove crossing branches.
Propagation
By grafting onto avocado seedling rootstock; seed-grown trees take 7–10+ years to fruit.
Mature size
5–12 m; can be kept at 3–5 m with annual pruning.
Growth rate
Moderate; grafted trees fruit from year 3–5.
Lifespan
Long-lived — 50–100+ years.
Bloom / fruit season
Small panicles of pale yellow flowers in winter–spring; fruit develops over 9–18 months.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender below −2 °C; suitable for frost-free coastal regions 1, 3, 5, 6.
Toxicity & safety
Fruit safe for humans; leaves, bark and pit are toxic to dogs, cats, birds and horses.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Feed with a citrus or tropical fruit fertilizer that includes nitrogen, potassium and micronutrients (especially zinc and boron) every 2–3 months during the growing season.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Wilting despite adequate watering (root rot)Phytophthora cinnamomi root rot from waterlogged or poorly drained soil.Improve drainage immediately; avoid over-watering; use raised beds if soil is heavy.
Brown corky patches on fruit skinSun scorch or anthracnose fungus.Provide afternoon shade cloth in extreme heat; apply copper spray preventatively in spring.
Chlorotic, pale young leavesIron or zinc deficiency from alkaline or calcareous soils — common in Tunisia.Apply chelated iron and zinc as foliar spray; slightly acidify soil if persistent.
Availability: Available in specialist nurseries mainly in northern and southern coastal Tunisia; demand is growing.
