Bunny-ears cactus

Opuntia microdasys  ·  Family: Cactaceae
Exterior · Succulent / cactus
EN Bunny-ears cactus  ·  FR Oponce oreilles de lapin  ·  AR صبار الأرنب  ·  Opuntia microdasys
Origin: Mexico
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A compact, ornamental cactus with paired velvety-looking pads that fool the eye — the golden glochids are painfully irritating, but the plant is irresistibly decorative in a sunny Tunisian rockery.
Light
Full sun; at least 6 hours of direct sun daily.
Water
Very low: water once every 2–4 weeks in summer; almost none in winter.
Soil
Sandy, extremely well-drained cactus mix; zero tolerance for soggy soil.
Temperature
Best at 18–35 °C; sensitive to frost — protect below 5 °C.
Humidity
Very low humidity preferred; dislikes damp air.
Feeding
A dilute cactus fertilizer once in spring; none in summer or winter.
Pruning & grooming
No pruning; remove damaged pads with tongs to avoid skin contact with glochids.
Propagation
By pads detached and dried for 1–2 weeks, then planted in dry sandy soil.
Mature size
60–90 cm tall and wide.
Growth rate
Slow to moderate.
Lifespan
Decades in the right spot.
Bloom / fruit season
Small yellow flowers in late spring; small red fruit may follow.
Hardiness (min temp)
Not frost-hardy; protect if temps drop below 5 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Glochids (tiny spines) cause serious skin and eye irritation — always use gloves and eye protection.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) One dilute application of cactus fertilizer in spring is sufficient; feeding in summer can cause soft, unstable growth.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Glochid splinters in skinAccidental contact with pads.Use tape or tweezers to remove; apply soothing lotion; do not rub.
Yellowing, limp padsOverwatering or root rot.Stop watering; let soil dry completely; check and trim roots.
Shrivelling in full sunSevere water stress.Water deeply once; the plant usually recovers within days.
Availability: Available in garden centres as an ornamental cactus; less common than prickly pear but widely grown.
