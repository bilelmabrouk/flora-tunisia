European fan palm

Chamaerops humilis  ·  Family: Arecaceae
Trees · Palm / statement
EN European fan palm  ·  FR Palmier nain / doum  ·  AR دوم  ·  Chamaerops humilis
Origin: Western Mediterranean (native to North Africa including Tunisia)
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short Tunisia's only native palm, the dwarf fan palm is an extremely tough, multi-stemmed shrub-palm that colonises rocky hillsides and coastal cliffs. Virtually maintenance-free, drought-proof, and frost-hardy — perfect for a naturalistic Tunisian garden.
Light
Full sun to light shade; extremely sun-tolerant.
Water
Very low: deeply drought-tolerant; water occasionally in extreme heat; virtually no watering once established.
Soil
Thrives in poor, rocky, or sandy soils; excellent drainage essential; tolerates some salinity.
Temperature
Very hardy — tolerates frost to −12 °C; withstands intense heat.
Humidity
Tolerates both dry and coastal humid air.
Feeding
Occasional light feed with a slow-release balanced fertilizer; not required in decent soil.
Pruning & grooming
Remove only dead or damaged fronds; avoid removing live fronds.
Propagation
By seed (slow germination) or carefully dividing basal suckers.
Mature size
1–5 m tall; clumping spread up to 3–4 m wide.
Growth rate
Very slow.
Lifespan
Very long — centuries in the wild.
Bloom / fruit season
Dioecious; small yellow flowers in spring; female plants produce small yellow-brown inedible fruits.
Hardiness (min temp)
Extremely hardy — one of the most cold-tolerant palms in the Mediterranean.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; not edible.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A light annual application of slow-release balanced fertilizer is sufficient; this palm is naturally adapted to poor soils and excess feeding encourages rank growth.  Find fertilizer →

Leaves browning at tipsOccasional natural tip burn; can worsen with salty spray or very dry air.Normal for this species; no treatment needed — simply trim the brown tips.
Fronds yellowing overall on established plantsWaterlogging or root rot from poorly drained soil.Improve drainage immediately; let soil dry out; move container plants to better position.
Availability: Widely available in Tunisian nurseries; also found growing wild on rocky hillsides throughout northern and coastal Tunisia.
