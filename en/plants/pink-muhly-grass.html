Pink muhly grass

Muhlenbergia capillaris  ·  Family: Poaceae
Exterior · Ornamental grass
EN Pink muhly grass  ·  FR Muhlenbergie  ·  AR مولنبرجيا  ·  Muhlenbergia capillaris
Origin: Eastern North America
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A spectacular ornamental grass that erupts into a cloud of rosy-pink plumes each autumn. Drought-tough and very low-maintenance, it is best suited to coastal Tunisia's mild winters.
Light
Full sun; foliage thins and bloom diminishes in shade.
Water
Low once established; water occasionally in prolonged dry spells.
Soil
Well-drained, even poor sandy or rocky soil; dislikes waterlogged ground.
Temperature
Tolerates heat well; hardy to about -10 C but fares best where winters are mild.
Humidity
Handles coastal humidity and dry interior air equally well.
Feeding
Light balanced feed once in spring is enough; over-fertilising reduces bloom.
Pruning & grooming
Cut clumps back to about 10 cm in late winter before new growth appears.
Propagation
By division in spring, or from seed sown under cover.
Mature size
60-90 cm foliage, flower plumes reach 1.2 m.
Growth rate
Moderate; fills out nicely in 2-3 seasons.
Lifespan
Perennial; lasts many years with annual cutting back.
Bloom / fruit season
Showy pink-purple plumes in autumn (Sep-Nov).
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to around -10 C; tender foliage may die back in hard frosts.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to people and animals.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A single light application of a balanced granular fertilizer in early spring is sufficient; avoid high-nitrogen feeds that promote lush foliage at the expense of the autumn blooms.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Clump dies out in the centreNatural ageing of an old clump.Divide in spring, replant the vigorous outer sections.
Poor or no autumn bloomToo much shade or excessive nitrogen fertiliser.Move to a sunnier spot; reduce feeding.
Rust-coloured spots on leavesLeaf rust fungus, favoured by wet conditions.Improve air circulation; cut back affected foliage; avoid overhead watering.
Availability: Occasionally found in Tunisian garden centres, especially those carrying ornamental grasses; may need to be sourced from specialist nurseries.
