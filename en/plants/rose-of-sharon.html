Rose of Sharon

Hibiscus syriacus  ·  Family: Malvaceae
Exterior · Shrub
EN Rose of Sharon  ·  FR Althéa  ·  AR خطمية  ·  Hibiscus syriacus
Origin: East Asia (China, Korea, and India)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A deciduous shrub that puts on a long late-summer flower show in white, pink, purple, or blue — much more cold-hardy than tropical hibiscus and a reliable flowering shrub for gardens across northern and central Tunisia.
Light
Full sun; tolerates light shade but flowers less.
Water
Moderate; water regularly when establishing; drought-tolerant once mature.
Soil
Well-drained, fertile; adapts to a wide range of soils.
Temperature
Very hardy to about −20 °C; suits all Tunisian zones except the hottest desert south.
Humidity
Adaptable; tolerates coastal and continental conditions.
Feeding
Balanced fertiliser in spring; a second application in early summer promotes longer flowering.
Pruning & grooming
Prune hard in late winter to maintain compact habit and maximise flowering on new wood.
Propagation
From semi-ripe cuttings in summer or hardwood cuttings in winter.
Mature size
2–3 m tall, 1–1.5 m wide; upright habit.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Long-lived; 30+ years.
Bloom / fruit season
Mid-summer to autumn (July–October); flowers on new wood — pruning promotes more blooms.
Hardiness (min temp)
Very frost-hardy; one of the most cold-tolerant flowering shrubs for Tunisia.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; flowers and young leaves are edible in many cultures.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced fertiliser in spring, with a potassium-rich supplement in early summer, extends the flowering season into autumn.  Find fertilizer →
