Bougainvillea (climbing)

Bougainvillea × buttiana  ·  Family: Nyctaginaceae
Exterior · Climber
EN Bougainvillea (climbing)  ·  FR Bougainvillier grimpant  ·  AR بوقنفيلية متسلقة  ·  Bougainvillea × buttiana
Origin: Garden hybrid (B. glabra × B. peruviana), South America
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Moderate
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short The classic Tunisian climbing bougainvillea, covering walls and pergolas in blazing magenta bracts for months — tough, drought-tolerant, and a true icon of coastal Tunisian gardens.
Light
Full sun, minimum 6 hours daily — more sun means more colour.
Water
Low to moderate; allow soil to dry between waterings. Drought stress actually triggers blooming.
Soil
Well-drained, slightly acidic to neutral; does not tolerate waterlogging.
Temperature
Frost-tender; best above 5 °C. Coastal and southern Tunisia ideal.
Humidity
Tolerates dry conditions; dislikes very high humidity.
Feeding
Every 3–4 weeks in growing season with a high-potassium fertilizer; reduce in winter.
Pruning & grooming
Prune after each bloom flush; wear gloves (sharp thorns). Hard prune in late winter.
Propagation
Hardwood or semi-ripe cuttings; rooting powder helps.
Mature size
Climbs 4–12 m if allowed.
Growth rate
Fast.
Lifespan
Very long-lived; can survive for decades.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring to autumn with multiple flushes; peak in summer.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; protect below 3–5 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Sap and thorns can cause skin irritation and dermatitis; wear gloves.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A high-potassium, low-nitrogen fertilizer applied every 3–4 weeks in the growing season maximises bract colour and bloom duration.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
No bloomingOver-watering, over-feeding with nitrogen, or insufficient sun.Reduce watering; stop nitrogen; ensure 6+ hours sun; stress slightly before bloom season.
Leaf drop after move or cold snapStress from environmental change.Keep in stable conditions; avoid moving during winter. Recovery is normal.
Bougainvillea looper caterpillarsCaterpillars of specialist moths that target bougainvillea.Hand-pick or apply Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) spray.
Availability: Very widely sold and grown throughout Tunisia; one of the most popular climbers on the market.
