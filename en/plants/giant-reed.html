Giant reed

Arundo donax  ·  Family: Poaceae
Exterior · Ornamental grass
EN Giant reed  ·  FR Canne de Provence  ·  AR قصبة  ·  Arundo donax
Origin: Mediterranean basin and western Asia
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short The iconic tall reed of Tunisian riverbeds and windbreaks — grows explosively fast, tolerates heat, drought and saline soils, and provides year-round structure in large gardens.
Light
Full sun; thrives in the intense Tunisian summer sun.
Water
Moderate; drought-tolerant once established, but fastest growth with regular deep watering.
Soil
Adaptable to most soils including clay, sand, and brackish conditions; good drainage preferred.
Temperature
Very heat-tolerant; survives light frost down to about −5 °C.
Humidity
Tolerates dry air; grows naturally along watercourses.
Feeding
Rarely necessary; a nitrogen-rich feed in spring boosts early growth if desired.
Pruning & grooming
Cut old canes to ground level in late winter to encourage fresh, straight growth.
Propagation
By division of rhizome sections or from stem cuttings placed in moist soil.
Mature size
3–6 m tall; spreads via rhizomes.
Growth rate
Very fast — can add 1–2 m per season.
Lifespan
Long-lived perennial; effectively permanent once established.
Bloom / fruit season
Feathery silvery-purple plumes appear in late summer to autumn.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to about −5 °C; fully suited to all Tunisian climates.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; traditionally used for flute-making and baskets in Tunisia.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A nitrogen-rich fertilizer applied once in early spring gives the canes a strong start; established plants rarely need additional feeding.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Rapid, uncontrolled spreadRhizomes spread underground beyond the planting area.Install a root barrier 50–60 cm deep around the planting; divide and remove excess rhizomes each spring.
Yellowing older canesNatural ageing; old canes die after 2–3 years.Cut yellowed canes at ground level; young growth replaces them quickly.
Scale insects on canesDry, stressed plants attract scale infestations.Scrub off with soapy water; ensure adequate moisture; remove heavily infested canes.
Availability: Widely available in Tunisian garden centres and nurseries; also naturalised along watercourses throughout the country.
