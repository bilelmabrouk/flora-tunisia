African lily

Agapanthus africanus  ·  Family: Amaryllidaceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN African lily  ·  FR Agapanthe  ·  AR زهرة النيل  ·  Agapanthus africanus
Origin: South Africa (Cape Province)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short Bold, globe-shaped heads of violet-blue flowers on tall stems from May to August — a classic of Tunisian coastal gardens. Very tough, drought-tolerant, and long-lived once established.
Light
Full sun; thrives in strong Tunisian summer sun.
Water
Moderate; drought-tolerant but benefits from regular watering in summer; reduce in winter.
Soil
Well-drained, fertile to average soil; tolerates sandy coastal soils.
Temperature
Best 15–30 °C; frost-sensitive (damaged below −5 °C); fine in coastal Tunisia year-round.
Humidity
Low to average; very adaptable.
Feeding
Slow-release balanced fertilizer in spring and a liquid feed monthly in summer.
Pruning & grooming
Remove spent flower heads; cut old foliage in late winter.
Propagation
By clump division every 4–5 years in spring; allow divided pieces time to re-establish.
Mature size
60–100 cm tall in flower; evergreen clumps 40–60 cm wide.
Growth rate
Slow to moderate; long-lived clumps.
Lifespan
Long-lived evergreen perennial (decades).
Bloom / fruit season
May to August in Tunisia.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; protect below −5 °C; fine in regions 1, 3, 5, 6.
Toxicity & safety
Toxic if eaten — causes vomiting and diarrhoea; keep away from pets.

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
No flowering despite healthy leavesClump too crowded, too much shade, or recently divided.Divide clumps every 4–5 years; move to full sun; allow 1–2 years after division.
Brown leaf tipsUnder-watering in summer heat or salt accumulation in pot.Water more regularly in summer; flush pot with extra water occasionally.
Root mealybug on pot-grown plantsWarm, dry conditions in containers.Remove plant, wash roots, repot in fresh compost.
Availability: Commonly available in Tunisian garden centres; very popular on the coast and in urban gardens.
