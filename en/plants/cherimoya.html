Cherimoya

Annona cherimola  ·  Family: Annonaceae
Trees · Fruit / nut
EN Cherimoya  ·  FR Chérimolier  ·  AR شيريمويا  ·  Annona cherimola
Origin: Andean highlands of South America (Ecuador, Peru, Bolivia)
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Demanding
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A subtropical fruit tree bearing large, creamy-sweet 'custard apple' fruits prized for their exceptional flavour. Suited to coastal and northern Tunisia's mild climate, but demands hand-pollination and attentive care to fruit well.
Light
Full sun; minimum 6 hours daily; avoids flowering in deep shade.
Water
Moderate to high; regular deep watering during growth and fruit development; reduce after harvest.
Soil
Deep, well-drained, fertile loam; tolerates slightly alkaline soils; mulch heavily.
Temperature
Best 15–28 °C; sensitive to frost below −2 °C and to heat above 38 °C during flowering.
Humidity
Prefers moderate humidity; very low humidity drops flowers and reduces fruit set.
Feeding
Regular balanced fruit-tree fertilizer during growing season; higher potassium near harvest.
Pruning & grooming
Annual light pruning after harvest to maintain open shape and encourage new fruiting wood.
Propagation
By seed (slow, 3–5 years to fruit) or grafting onto rootstock (flowers in 2–3 years).
Mature size
5–9 m unpruned; keep to 3–5 m with pruning for easy harvest.
Growth rate
Moderate; establishes over 3–4 years.
Lifespan
20–40 years in good conditions.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring (March–May); flowers are perfect but require hand-pollination for good fruit set.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tender; frost damage below −2 °C; protect young trees in northern Tunisia winters.
Toxicity & safety
Fruit is edible; seeds are toxic if crushed and consumed in large amounts — do not eat seeds.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Apply a balanced fruit-tree fertilizer three times per year: early spring, post-flowering, and mid-summer; increase potassium in the final month before harvest to improve fruit flavour and size.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Flowers falling without setting fruitLack of pollination — cherimoya needs hand-pollination as natural pollinators are scarce.Hand-pollinate: collect pollen from open male flowers with a fine brush and transfer to receptive female flowers in the afternoon.
Yellowing leaves mid-seasonIron or magnesium deficiency in alkaline soil, or overwatering.Apply chelated iron or magnesium sulfate; check drainage and reduce watering if roots are waterlogged.
Mealybugs on young shoots and fruitCommon sap-sucking pest on Annona species in warm conditions.Spray with insecticidal soap or neem oil; monitor regularly as mealybugs reduce fruit quality.
Fruit splitting on the treeIrregular watering — dry spells followed by heavy watering during fruit development.Maintain consistent, regular watering throughout the fruiting period; mulch heavily to retain soil moisture.
Availability: Rare in Tunisia; occasionally found in specialist subtropical fruit nurseries; more common in coastal areas with mild winters.
