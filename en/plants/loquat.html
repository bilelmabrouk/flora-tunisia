Loquat

Eriobotrya japonica  ·  Family: Rosaceae
Trees · Fruit / nut
EN Loquat  ·  FR Néflier du Japon  ·  AR بشملة / مزّاح  ·  Eriobotrya japonica
Origin: Central China and Japan

Full sun

Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A hardy, evergreen fruit tree that flowers in winter and ripens in spring — one of the earliest fruits of the Tunisian garden. Low-maintenance and beautiful year-round, the loquat is a staple in coastal Tunisia.
Light
Full sun for best fruiting; tolerates partial shade but fewer fruits.
Water
Moderate; water regularly when young; drought-tolerant once established.
Soil
Well-drained, fertile; adaptable to most Tunisian soils including calcareous.
Temperature
Hardy to about −10 °C; flowers and young fruit can be damaged by frost below −3 °C.
Humidity
Tolerates coastal humidity and dry inland air alike.
Feeding
Light annual feed with balanced fertilizer in autumn before flowering.
Pruning & grooming
Thin fruit clusters to 3–4 per cluster for larger fruit; remove dead or crossing branches after harvest.
Propagation
By seed (easy but slow, variable); by air layering or grafting for true-to-type.
Mature size
3–8 m; can be pruned to any size.
Growth rate
Moderate to fast; fruits from year 3–4 when grafted.
Lifespan
Long-lived — decades with little maintenance.
Bloom / fruit season
White fragrant flowers in November–January; golden-orange fruit in April–May.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy in coastal Tunisia (regions 1, 3, 5, 6); frost kills flower buds in cold highlands.
Toxicity & safety
Fruit and flesh are safe; seeds contain amygdalin — do not eat large amounts.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) An annual application of balanced fruit-tree fertilizer in early autumn (before flowers open) supports flowering and the following spring's crop.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Flower clusters browning and dyingFrost damage during winter flowering (below −3 °C).Cover with horticultural fleece during frost events; choose a sheltered south-facing position.
Scabby, brown lesions on fruit skinLoquat scab fungus (Fusicladium eriobotryae), favoured by wet spring.Apply copper fungicide at flowering; ensure good air flow within the canopy.
Leaves with large brown blotches in summerEntomosporium leaf spot or sunburn in extreme heat.Improve air circulation; avoid wetting leaves when irrigating; shade during extreme heat.
Availability: Very commonly sold in Tunisian nurseries and markets, especially in spring.
