Snapdragon

Antirrhinum majus  ·  Family: Plantaginaceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN Snapdragon  ·  FR Muflier  ·  AR فم الأسد  ·  Antirrhinum majus
Origin: Mediterranean region (SW Europe and NW Africa)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A cool-season Mediterranean native that shines as a Tunisian winter bedding plant — tall spikes of dragon-mouth flowers in every colour from November through May, thriving in the northern coastal climate.
Light
Full sun — minimum 6 hours daily; partial shade reduces flowering and can cause rust disease.
Water
Moderate: water when the top 2–3 cm dry out; avoid waterlogging; good drainage prevents crown rot.
Soil
Well-drained, fertile garden soil or potting mix; slightly acidic to neutral pH.
Temperature
Cool-season plant; ideal 10–20 °C; tolerates light frost; goes into decline above 27 °C.
Humidity
Average; good airflow is important — poor circulation encourages rust and mildew.
Feeding
A balanced flowering-plant fertilizer every 2–3 weeks during flowering; avoid excess nitrogen.
Pruning & grooming
Deadhead spent spikes by cutting to the nearest lateral bud; pinch young plants to promote branching.
Propagation
From seed sown in September–October in Tunisia for winter blooms; plant out seedlings after 6 weeks.
Mature size
20–90 cm tall depending on variety; dwarf, intermediate, and tall types available.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Short-lived perennial usually grown as a cool-season annual.
Bloom / fruit season
Winter to late spring (November–May in northern Tunisia).
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to about −5 °C; adapts well to Tunisia's mild winters.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A phosphorus- and potassium-rich flowering-plant fertilizer every 2–3 weeks during the blooming season promotes dense flower spikes; reduce or stop in summer when the plant goes dormant in the heat.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Orange-brown powdery pustules on leaf undersidesSnapdragon rust (Puccinia antirrhini), common in warm humid periods.Remove infected leaves; improve airflow; avoid wetting foliage; replace severely affected plants.
Powdery white spots on leavesPowdery mildew in conditions with poor air circulation.Improve spacing; remove affected leaves; avoid overhead watering.
No new flowering spikes, plant looks exhaustedPlant not cut back after first flush, or summer heat decline.Cut back hard after the first flush to encourage reblooming; expect decline in peak summer heat and replant in autumn.
Clusters of small green insects on stems and budsAphid infestation, especially on tender new growth.Spray off with a strong jet of water; use insecticidal soap if the infestation persists.
Availability: Widely available as seedlings in Tunisian nurseries in early autumn for winter planting, and as seed packets year-round.
