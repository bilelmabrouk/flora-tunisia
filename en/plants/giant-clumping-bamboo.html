Giant clumping bamboo

Bambusa sp.  ·  Family: Poaceae

Setting
Outdoor
In short Tall, clumping tropical bamboos of the Bambusa genus create instant dramatic screens and windbreaks in warm Tunisian coastal gardens without the invasive spread of running bamboos.
Light
Full sun; most Bambusa species thrive with at least 6 hours of direct sun.
Water
Moderate to high; deep watering weekly in summer; drought-tolerant once established.
Soil
Rich, well-drained loam; tolerates clay if not waterlogged.
Temperature
Tropical; grows best above 15 °C; frost-sensitive below 0–2 °C depending on species.
Humidity
Prefers moderate humidity; coastal Tunisia is ideal.
Feeding
Nitrogen-rich fertilizer in spring promotes dense, tall culm growth.
Pruning & grooming
Remove old, dead, or crowded culms at the base annually to maintain density and airflow.
Propagation
Division of established clumps in spring; replant promptly with good watering.
Mature size
Varies by species: 5–20 m; clumping — spread is slow and manageable.
Growth rate
Fast in warm months.
Lifespan
Very long-lived perennial clumps.
Bloom / fruit season
Bamboos rarely flower; gregarious flowering is infrequent and may weaken the clump.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; best in regions 1, 3, 5, 6 in Tunisia.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; young shoots edible when properly cooked.

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellowing leavesNitrogen deficiency or waterlogged roots.Feed with nitrogen; improve drainage; check irrigation frequency.
Culm die-back after floweringGregarious (mass) flowering depletes plant energy.Remove dead culms; the clump may regenerate from the roots — allow time.
Scale or mealybug on young shootsWater stress or dense interior reducing air circulation.Thin crowded culms; treat with neem-oil solution; water adequately.
Availability: Available from bamboo specialist nurseries in Tunisia and larger garden centres, particularly in northern and coastal regions.
