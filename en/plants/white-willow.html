White willow

Salix alba  ·  Family: Salicaceae
Trees · Olive / native
EN White willow  ·  FR Saule blanc  ·  AR صفصاف  ·  Salix alba
Origin: Europe and West Asia, naturalised in North Africa including Tunisia
Light
Full sun
Water
High
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A graceful, fast-growing water-loving tree with silvery leaves, thriving along Tunisian rivers, wadis and irrigation channels; classic shade and windbreak tree.
Light
Full sun; needs at least 6 hours of direct sun daily.
Water
High; thrives with constant moisture; ideal near water; water deeply and regularly in summer.
Soil
Moist to wet, heavy or loamy soil; tolerates clay and periodic waterlogging.
Temperature
Hardy; handles Tunisian frost in the north and heat in summer near water.
Humidity
Prefers humid sites; less suitable for the dry south.
Feeding
Light balanced feed in spring for young trees; mature trees are self-sufficient near water.
Pruning & grooming
Coppice or pollard every few years in late winter for structural wood or to control size.
Propagation
Very easy from hardwood cuttings stuck directly into moist soil; natural layer also work.
Mature size
15–25 m tall; wide, weeping canopy.
Growth rate
Very fast — among the fastest willows.
Lifespan
30–80 years; shorter if water-stressed.
Bloom / fruit season
Narrow yellow catkins in early spring before leaves.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to around −25 °C; no frost problems in Tunisia.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; bark has medicinal salicin content but safe in normal exposure.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A light balanced fertilizer in early spring benefits young trees; avoid excess nitrogen, which causes weak, disease-prone growth.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Willow scab: dark spots and shoot diebackVenturia saliciperda fungus, worst in wet springs.Remove affected shoots; improve air circulation; avoid overhead watering.
Leaf rust (yellow-orange pustules)Melampsora species; common after humid weather.Rake fallen leaves; thin the canopy for light and airflow.
Roots invading pipes or pavementAggressive root growth seeking water; improper siting.Plant well away from infrastructure (at least 10 m); use root barriers.
Availability: Readily available in nurseries near rivers and in public forestry programmes across northern Tunisia.
