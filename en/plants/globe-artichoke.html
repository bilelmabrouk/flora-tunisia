Globe artichoke

Cynara cardunculus var. scolymus  ·  Family: Asteraceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Globe artichoke  ·  FR Artichaut  ·  AR قنّارية  ·  Cynara cardunculus var. scolymus
Origin: North Africa & Mediterranean (domesticated from the wild cardoon in North Africa or the Canary Islands)
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A staple of Tunisian vegetable gardens and cuisine — a large architectural perennial grown for its edible flower buds. Drought-tolerant once established and productive for many years.
Light
Full sun (minimum 6 hours).
Water
Moderate; water deeply and regularly during bud development; reduce in summer dormancy.
Soil
Deep, fertile, well-drained soil; rich in organic matter; pH 6–7.
Temperature
Best at 15–24 °C; tolerates light frost; goes dormant in extreme heat.
Humidity
Tolerates coastal humidity well.
Feeding
Rich compost or balanced fertilizer in autumn and early spring.
Pruning & grooming
Cut stems down after harvest; divide every 3–5 years to maintain vigour.
Propagation
By offshoots (suckers) in spring; or from seed (slower).
Mature size
1–1.5 m tall and 1 m wide.
Growth rate
Moderate — first buds in year 1–2; peak production from year 2.
Lifespan
Perennial — productive for 5–10 years.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring–early summer (April–June); harvest buds before they open.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tolerates light frost; protect roots with mulch in cold areas.
Toxicity & safety
Safe; edible and widely consumed.

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Black-brown rot at the base of budsBotrytis grey mould in humid conditions.Improve air circulation; remove affected buds; avoid overhead watering.
Aphid colonies in bud scalesArtichoke aphids.Wash off with strong water jet; encourage ladybirds.
Powdery white coating on leavesPowdery mildew.Improve air circulation; remove affected leaves.
Availability: Commonly grown / sold in Tunisia; widely cultivated along the northern coast and Sahel.
