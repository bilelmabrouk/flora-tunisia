Burro's tail

Sedum morganianum  ·  Family: Crassulaceae
Interior · Succulent / cactus
EN Burro's tail  ·  FR Sédum queue d'âne  ·  AR ذيل الحمار  ·  Sedum morganianum
Origin: Mexico and Honduras
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Indoor
In short A striking trailing succulent with plump blue-green 'tails' — beautiful in a hanging basket by a bright Tunisian window, demanding only bright light and restrained watering.
Light
Bright direct sun; south or west window — 4–6 hours daily prevents stem elongation.
Water
Very low: water thoroughly when the soil is bone-dry; roughly every 2–3 weeks in summer, monthly in winter.
Soil
Sharply drained cactus mix; add 30–40% perlite or coarse grit.
Temperature
Ideal 18–25 °C; tolerates to ~7 °C but frost-sensitive.
Humidity
Low; avoid bathrooms and humid kitchens — dry air is preferred.
Feeding
Monthly in spring–summer with a dilute low-nitrogen fertilizer; withhold in autumn–winter.
Pruning & grooming
Handle as little as possible — leaves fall off easily; trim stray stems to keep shape.
Propagation
From stem cuttings or individual leaves; let cut ends callous 2–3 days before placing on dry soil.
Mature size
Trailing stems to 60–90 cm long.
Growth rate
Moderate once established.
Lifespan
Several years in a stable spot.
Bloom / fruit season
Rare indoors; small pink-red flowers at stem tips in late spring–summer.
Hardiness (min temp)
Keep above 7 °C; frost kills it.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A cactus fertilizer diluted to half dose, once a month in spring and summer, supports healthy plump leaves without promoting weak elongation.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Shrivelled, wrinkled leavesUnderwatering or root damage.Water thoroughly and allow to drain; check roots for rot.
Leaves dropping at the slightest touchNormal characteristic; also worsened by overwatering or temperature shock.Handle minimally; avoid moving the pot frequently or brushing against the trailing stems.
Soft, mushy, translucent stemsRoot or stem rot from overwatering.Cut away rotten sections, let cuttings dry a few days, and repot in fresh dry cactus mix.
White cottony clusters between the leavesMealybug infestation.Dab with rubbing alcohol on a cotton swab; repeat weekly until gone.
Availability: Popular hanging succulent found in Tunisian nurseries and plant shops, especially for balconies and terraces.
