Toothed lavender ⇄ also: Interior

Lavandula dentata  ·  Family: Lamiaceae
Exterior · Herb / aromatic
EN Toothed lavender  ·  FR Lavande dentée  ·  AR خزامى مسننة  ·  Lavandula dentata
Origin: Mediterranean (North Africa, Spain, Canary Islands)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Indoor & out
In short Toothed lavender is the most heat-tolerant and long-blooming lavender, native to North Africa and ideal for Tunisia's warm coastal, central, and southern regions. Its finely toothed grey-green leaves and nearly year-round flowering make it the easiest lavender to grow across most of the country.
Light
Full sun — at least 6 hours; handles more heat than other lavenders.
Water
Low: drought-tolerant; water deeply and infrequently; roots must never stay wet.
Soil
Well-drained, lean to moderate fertility, alkaline-tolerant; sandy or stony soils preferred.
Temperature
Wide range: 5–40 °C; more heat-tolerant than angustifolia; damaged by hard frost below −5 °C.
Humidity
Tolerates moderate coastal humidity if drainage is excellent.
Feeding
One light balanced application in early spring only.
Pruning & grooming
Trim spent flower spikes regularly to encourage re-flowering; reshape lightly in early spring.
Propagation
Semi-hardwood cuttings in summer root very easily.
Mature size
40–90 cm tall.
Growth rate
Moderate; fills out in 1–2 seasons.
Lifespan
8–15 years; fairly long-lived for lavender.
Bloom / fruit season
Long-blooming: spring through summer, with an autumn repeat (March–June and September–November in Tunisia).
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −5 °C; suitable for all Tunisian zones except the coldest NW highlands.
Toxicity & safety
Safe; used in aromatherapy, cooking, and as a garden ornamental.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) One light spring feed is all toothed lavender needs; in lean, well-drained soil it flowers more freely and is longer-lived.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Root rot (yellowing, wilting despite watering)Overwatering or heavy soil — main killer.Stop watering; check roots; improve drainage with grit or sand; replant in well-drained soil.
Grey mould (botrytis) on stemsHumid, poorly-ventilated conditions in cool weather.Improve airflow by pruning; remove affected stems; avoid wetting foliage.
No repeat bloom in autumnSpent summer flower heads not removed.Trim all spent spikes after the summer flush to trigger autumn re-flowering.
Pale, stunted growthSoil too nutrient-poor or pH too low (acidic).Add a light balanced feed; if soil is acidic add lime to raise pH.
Availability: Available in Tunisian nurseries and garden centres, especially in coastal areas; sometimes sold as a potted ornamental.
