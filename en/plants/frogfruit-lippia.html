Frogfruit / lippia

Phyla nodiflora  ·  Family: Verbenaceae
Exterior · Ground-cover
EN Frogfruit / lippia  ·  FR Lippia  ·  AR ليبيا  ·  Phyla nodiflora
Origin: Pantropical (native also in North Africa and Mediterranean)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A tough, drought-tolerant creeping groundcover used as a water-saving lawn alternative in warm Tunisian gardens. It carpets the ground with tiny pink-white flowers all summer and withstands foot traffic.
Light
Full sun for best density and flowering; tolerates very light shade.
Water
Low to moderate; tolerates drought once established but looks best with occasional watering.
Soil
Adapts to sandy, clay, loam, and even slightly saline soils; very versatile.
Temperature
Best above 10 °C; frost-tender; damaged by frost but regrows from roots.
Humidity
Tolerates coastal humidity and salt; also survives dry inland conditions.
Feeding
Light balanced feed once in spring; does not need much feeding.
Pruning & grooming
Mow to 3–5 cm to keep dense and neat; tolerates mowing very well.
Propagation
By seed, cuttings, or division; establishes very quickly from transplanted sections.
Mature size
2–8 cm tall, spreads indefinitely.
Growth rate
Fast; covers bare ground rapidly in warm conditions.
Lifespan
Perennial in warm zones; annual in cold areas.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring to autumn (April–October), small pink to white flower heads all season.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; regrows from roots after mild frost.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; leaves used in traditional remedies in some cultures.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) One light balanced feed in early spring is enough; this plant is naturally adapted to poor soils and over-feeding causes excessive spread and weak, floppy growth.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Brown or bare patches in the carpetHeavy foot traffic, drought stress, or frost damage.Water more; replant small divisions or cuttings into gaps; it fills back quickly.
Spider mites in hot dry spellsLow humidity and heat favour mites.Spray water on foliage; apply diluted neem oil in severe cases.
Invasion of nearby flower bedsNatural creeping spread — aggressive in ideal conditions.Install edging barriers; trim margins every 3–4 weeks during the growing season.
Availability: Widely available in Tunisian nurseries and garden centres; used extensively in public landscaping across the country.
