Golden bamboo

Phyllostachys aurea  ·  Family: Poaceae
Exterior · Bamboo
EN Golden bamboo  ·  FR Bambou doré  ·  AR خيزران ذهبي  ·  Phyllostachys aurea
Origin: Southern China

Sun / part shade

Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A running bamboo with golden-yellow canes that thrives in northern Tunisian gardens — elegant and fast-growing but must be contained with a root barrier to prevent spread.
Light
Full sun to part shade; golden colouring is best in full sun.
Water
Moderate; keep moist, especially in summer; drought-tolerant once established.
Soil
Fertile, well-drained, slightly acidic to neutral; tolerates a range of soils.
Temperature
Cold-hardy to about −15 °C; suits cooler Tunisian regions including the north-west.
Humidity
Tolerates dry and humid conditions; adaptable.
Feeding
Nitrogen-rich fertilizer in early spring supports vigorous culm production.
Pruning & grooming
Thin old culms annually; use a root barrier 60 cm deep to prevent invasive spread.
Propagation
By division; be careful — runners spread aggressively if uncontained.
Mature size
3–5 m tall in Tunisia; rhizomes spread widely.
Growth rate
Fast; runners can spread several metres per year without containment.
Lifespan
Effectively permanent once established.
Bloom / fruit season
Very rarely flowers; mast seeding can weaken or kill the colony.
Hardiness (min temp)
One of the hardier bamboos; tolerates frost well.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A nitrogen-rich fertilizer in early spring fuels the season's new culm growth; a second application in early summer sustains density.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Invasive spread beyond garden boundariesRunning rhizomes spreading underground without barrier.Install a 60 cm deep HDPE root barrier around the planting; cut back runners annually in spring.
Yellowing or pale leaves in summerNitrogen deficiency or insufficient watering.Apply nitrogen fertilizer and increase watering during dry spells.
Culm die-back after rare floweringMass (mast) seeding exhausts the plant's resources.Cut back dead culms; mulch heavily; the colony often regenerates from roots over 1–2 years.
Availability: Available in bamboo specialist nurseries and some garden centres in northern Tunisia; less common in the south.
