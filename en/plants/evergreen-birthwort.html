Evergreen birthwort

Aristolochia sempervirens  ·  Family: Aristolochiaceae
Exterior · Climber
EN Evergreen birthwort  ·  FR Aristoloche  ·  AR أرسطولوخيا  ·  Aristolochia sempervirens
Origin: Eastern Mediterranean (native — including Tunisia)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Very toxic
Setting
Outdoor
In short A native evergreen twiner of Tunisian maquis bearing curious pipe-shaped flowers in spring — low-maintenance and good for shaded walls, but highly toxic so handle with care.
Light
Part shade to full sun; adapts well to shaded walls.
Water
Low; drought-tolerant once established. Water young plants regularly.
Soil
Well-drained, rocky or average soil; tolerates poor conditions.
Temperature
Hardy to about −5 °C; suited to northern and coastal Tunisia.
Humidity
Average; tolerates coastal conditions.
Feeding
No regular feeding needed; a light spring top-dress suffices.
Pruning & grooming
Trim to restrict spread; remove dead stems in spring.
Propagation
By seed in autumn or cuttings in spring.
Mature size
Climbs 2–4 m; can spread wider on walls.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Long-lived perennial climber.
Bloom / fruit season
March–May; curious yellowish-brown pipe-shaped flowers.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tolerates light frost to −5 °C.
Toxicity & safety
All parts highly toxic (aristolochic acid); do not ingest; wear gloves when pruning.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Needs very little feeding; a light balanced fertilizer once in spring is ample. Over-feeding encourages soft growth vulnerable to pests.  Find fertilizer →
