Black bamboo

Phyllostachys nigra  ·  Family: Poaceae
Exterior · Bamboo
EN Black bamboo  ·  FR Bambou noir  ·  AR خيزران أسود  ·  Phyllostachys nigra
Origin: Southern China

Setting
Outdoor
In short The most dramatic bamboo for Tunisian gardens — slender canes that turn jet-black in their second year, creating a stunning contrast with lush green foliage in cool northern sites.
Light
Full sun to part shade; maximum sun intensity develops the blackest culms.
Water
Moderate; keep consistently moist; tolerates short dry spells once established.
Soil
Fertile, moisture-retentive, well-drained; slightly acidic preferred.
Temperature
Hardy to about −15 °C; suits northern and north-western Tunisia.
Humidity
Adaptable; tolerates coastal humidity and drier inland air.
Feeding
Balanced fertilizer in spring; avoid excess nitrogen which can delay blackening.
Pruning & grooming
Remove first-year green canes if space is limited; thin interior for airflow.
Propagation
Division of established clumps; install root barriers — a running bamboo.
Mature size
3–5 m tall; slender, elegant canes.
Growth rate
Moderate to fast.
Lifespan
Long-lived; permanent with management.
Bloom / fruit season
Extremely rare; mast seeding may kill the stand.
Hardiness (min temp)
One of the hardiest ornamental bamboos; fully suitable for northern Tunisia.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced fertilizer in spring is sufficient; avoid high-nitrogen feeds which delay the characteristic blackening of the culms.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Culms remain green — no blackeningInsufficient sun, or first-year canes — they only blacken in year two.Move to a sunnier position; be patient — blackening develops gradually in the second growing season.
Invasive spread into neighbouring areasRunning rhizomes without a containment barrier.Install a 60 cm deep root barrier; prune escaping rhizomes each spring.
Pale, sparse growthPoor soil or nutrient deficiency.Improve soil with organic matter; apply balanced fertilizer in spring.
Availability: Available in specialist bamboo nurseries in Tunisia; more commonly found in the northern regions.
