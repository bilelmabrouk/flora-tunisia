Greater periwinkle

Vinca major  ·  Family: Apocynaceae
Exterior · Ground-cover
EN Greater periwinkle  ·  FR Grande pervenche  ·  AR وينكا كبيرة  ·  Vinca major
Origin: Mediterranean Europe and North Africa (native across the region)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short A robust, fast-spreading evergreen groundcover ideal for shaded and semi-shaded slopes in northern Tunisian gardens, producing blue-violet flowers in late winter and spring. Extremely easy and nearly indestructible.
Light
Deep shade to full sun; one of the best performers under dense trees.
Water
Moderate; water regularly until established, then tolerates some dryness in shade.
Soil
Adapts to almost any soil; prefers moist, well-drained loam; tolerates clay.
Temperature
Hardy to −15 °C; very adaptable to Tunisian winters.
Humidity
Adapts to coastal and inland humidity.
Feeding
Light balanced feed in early spring; minimal feeding needed.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back hard (to 10–15 cm) every 2–3 years in late winter to renew vigour.
Propagation
Very easy from cuttings or division year-round; roots almost anywhere.
Mature size
30–50 cm tall, spreads indefinitely by long trailing stems.
Growth rate
Fast; covers banks rapidly.
Lifespan
Essentially permanent; extremely long-lived.
Bloom / fruit season
Late winter to spring (Feb–May), blue-violet five-petalled flowers.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-hardy to −15 °C; one of the most cold-tolerant groundcovers.
Toxicity & safety
All parts toxic if eaten; contains alkaloids — keep away from children and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A light balanced spring fertilizer supports healthy growth; over-feeding encourages excessive spread that is difficult to control.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Pale, washed-out leaf colourToo much sun causing bleaching, or nitrogen deficiency.Move to shadier position; apply a light feed.
Stems dying back in patchesStem blight fungus, worse in warm humid conditions.Remove and dispose of affected stems; improve air circulation; avoid overhead watering.
Excessive spread colonising other plantsNatural aggressive trailing growth.Cut back margins regularly; install root barriers if adjacent to delicate plants.
Availability: Very commonly sold across Tunisia; one of the most widely used groundcovers in both public and private northern gardens.
