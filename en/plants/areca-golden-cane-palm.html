Areca / golden cane palm ⇄ also: Exterior

Dypsis lutescens  ·  Family: Arecaceae

Setting
Indoor & out
In short The most popular indoor palm in Tunisia — its feathery golden-green fronds brighten any space, and it doubles as a natural air humidifier in dry coastal apartments.
Light
Bright indirect light indoors; full sun outdoors in coastal areas; direct harsh sun indoors causes scorching.
Water
Moderate: water when top 2–3 cm dry; keep soil consistently moist but never soggy; sensitive to drought.
Soil
Well-drained loam or palm potting mix; avoid compacting or heavy clay.
Temperature
Ideal 20–35 °C; minimum 15 °C; frost-tender.
Humidity
Prefers high humidity; mist fronds or place on a pebble tray — especially important in Tunisia's dry summers.
Feeding
Balanced palm fertilizer every 2–3 weeks in spring–summer; monthly in winter.
Pruning & grooming
Remove only fully brown, dead fronds at the stem base; do not cut yellowing fronds prematurely.
Propagation
By seed (fresh, slow — 4–6 weeks); or division of clumped base stems.
Mature size
Up to 6–8 m outdoors; typically 2–3 m indoors.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Long-lived in suitable conditions.
Bloom / fruit season
Panicles of small yellow flowers in spring–summer, followed by small yellow to orange fruits.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; only suitable in warmest Tunisian coastal zones outdoors.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A palm fertilizer with added magnesium and iron every 2–3 weeks in the growing season prevents the yellowing fronds common in nutrient-poor soils.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellow fronds starting from lower leavesOverwatering, poor drainage, or nutrient deficiency.Improve drainage; apply a palm fertilizer.
Brown frond tipsLow humidity, fluoride or salt buildup, or underwatering.Mist daily; flush soil occasionally to remove salt buildup; use low-fluoride water.
Fine speckling and webbing on frondsSpider mites, especially in dry indoor conditions.Rinse fronds thoroughly, raise humidity, and treat with insecticidal soap if the infestation persists.
Multiple thin trunks leaning or crowdingNatural clumping habit; overcrowding in a small pot can weaken individual stems.Repot into a larger container every few years and thin out the weakest stems if overcrowded.
Availability: One of the most commonly sold indoor palms in Tunisia, valued for its graceful golden-yellow stems in garden centres and shopping malls alike.
