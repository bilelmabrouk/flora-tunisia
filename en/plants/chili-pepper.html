Chili pepper

Capsicum annuum (hot)  ·  Family: Solanaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Chili pepper  ·  FR Piment  ·  AR فلفل حار  ·  Capsicum annuum (hot)
Origin: Central and South America
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short The fiery backbone of Tunisian harissa — hot peppers thrive in Tunisia's summer heat, producing abundant small to medium pods that dry beautifully for year-round spice use.
Light
Full sun — 6–8+ hours per day; more sun increases capsaicin (heat) levels.
Water
Moderate; slightly drier conditions than sweet peppers intensify the heat. Do not let soil dry completely.
Soil
Well-drained, fertile; tolerates slightly sandy soils better than bell peppers.
Temperature
Loves heat; grows best at 20–32 °C; frost-tender.
Humidity
Moderate; tolerates the dry heat of Tunisian summers well.
Feeding
Balanced feed at planting; moderate potassium during fruiting to support pod development.
Pruning & grooming
Minimal; remove damaged branches; some growers remove early flowers to increase overall yield.
Propagation
From seed started indoors 8–10 weeks before transplanting.
Mature size
30–80 cm depending on variety.
Growth rate
Moderate to fast in warm weather.
Lifespan
Annual; can survive multiple years in frost-free conditions.
Bloom / fruit season
Small white flowers in summer; pods ripen from green to red/orange/yellow over 60–90 days.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; plant after last cold spell in Tunisia.
Toxicity & safety
Safe to eat; capsaicin causes burning sensation — wear gloves when handling hot varieties.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced vegetable fertilizer at planting, then a moderate potassium-rich feed during fruiting promotes pod development; avoid excess nitrogen which increases foliage at the expense of fruit.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Fruit failing to ripen to redInsufficient sun or the season ending before ripening completes.Ensure maximum sun; harvest green and ripen indoors if season ends.
Aphids on stems and undersides of leavesCommon sap-sucking pest.Spray with diluted soap water; remove by hand if light infestation.
Wilting despite adequate waterRoot rot from waterlogged soil or Verticillium wilt.Improve drainage; rotate crops; avoid replanting in the same spot each year.
Availability: Seeds and seedlings widely available in spring throughout Tunisia; chili varieties specific to harissa production (such as 'baklouti') are a local specialty.
