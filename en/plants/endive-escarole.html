Endive / escarole

Cichorium endivia  ·  Family: Asteraceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Endive / escarole  ·  FR Chicorée frisée  ·  AR هندباء  ·  Cichorium endivia
Origin: Mediterranean (E Mediterranean & Near East)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A slightly bitter cool-season salad leaf, less common than lettuce but grown in Tunisian northern and coastal gardens in autumn–winter. Blanching the heart reduces bitterness.
Light
Full sun to partial shade; shade reduces bitterness.
Water
Regular moisture; do not allow soil to dry completely.
Soil
Fertile, well-drained, moisture-retentive loam; pH 6–7.
Temperature
Best at 10–20 °C; tolerates light frost; bolts in heat.
Humidity
Average; good air circulation reduces mildew.
Feeding
Light balanced fertilizer every 3 weeks.
Pruning & grooming
Blanch the heart 2–3 weeks before harvest by tying outer leaves.
Propagation
Direct sow or transplant; sow late summer for autumn harvest.
Mature size
30–50 cm diameter rosette.
Growth rate
Moderate — ready in 85–100 days.
Lifespan
Annual.
Bloom / fruit season
Bolts and flowers in heat; harvest before.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tolerates light frost to −3 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Safe; slightly bitter taste due to natural compounds.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A light balanced vegetable fertilizer every 3 weeks maintains healthy growth without excessive bitterness.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Excessive bitternessHeat or lack of blanching.Blanch hearts 2–3 weeks before harvest; grow in cooler months.
Bolting to flowerHigh temperature or prolonged heat.Plant in autumn; avoid warm-season planting.
Brown or slimy crown rotOverwatering or soil contact after blanching.Reduce watering; ensure good drainage; tie leaves carefully.
Availability: Commonly grown / sold in Tunisia, mainly in the cooler north and coastal regions.
