Madagascar jasmine ⇄ also: Interior

Stephanotis floribunda  ·  Family: Apocynaceae
Exterior · Climber
EN Madagascar jasmine  ·  FR Stéphanotis  ·  AR ستيفانوتيس  ·  Stephanotis floribunda
Origin: Madagascar

Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short Prized for its waxy, intensely fragrant white flowers used in wedding bouquets; a demanding climber that rewards careful watering and stable warmth in Tunisian coastal gardens.
Light
Bright indirect light or morning sun; avoid harsh afternoon sun.
Water
Regular but careful; moist but never waterlogged; use soft or filtered water.
Soil
Rich, well-drained, slightly acidic mix; good drainage is critical.
Temperature
Best at 18–25 °C; avoid temperature fluctuations; minimum 12 °C.
Humidity
Likes moderate to high humidity; mist in dry spells.
Feeding
High-potassium flowering fertilizer every 2 weeks in spring–summer.
Pruning & grooming
Minimal pruning; remove dead stems only; avoid disturbing when in bud.
Propagation
Semi-hardwood cuttings with bottom heat in spring; challenging.
Mature size
3–5 m on support.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Long-lived in right conditions; 10–20+ years.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring to early autumn (April–September); heaviest in late spring.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; keep above 12 °C; not suited to inland or cold regions.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; flowers safe to handle.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A high-potassium flowering fertilizer every two weeks in spring and summer encourages flower production; any imbalance quickly shows as bud drop.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Bud drop before openingTemperature fluctuation, overwatering, or moving the plant when in bud.Keep in a stable, warm spot; do not move once buds form; check watering.
Yellow leavesOverwatering, poor drainage, or iron deficiency.Improve drainage; reduce watering; apply chelated iron if soil is too alkaline.
Mealybugs on stemsCommon pest; worsened by dry indoor conditions.Remove with alcohol-dipped cotton; spray insecticidal soap.
Availability: Available in specialist nurseries; less common in mass-market Tunisian garden centres.
