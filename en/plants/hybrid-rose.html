Hybrid rose

Rosa × hybrida  ·  Family: Rosaceae
Exterior · Shrub
EN Hybrid rose  ·  FR Rosier  ·  AR وردة  ·  Rosa × hybrida
Origin: Complex multi-species hybrid developed in Europe and Asia over centuries
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Demanding
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short The classic garden rose — Tunisia's most beloved flowering shrub for private gardens, producing fragrant blooms from spring to autumn with dedicated but rewarding care.
Light
Minimum 6 hours of full sun daily; essential for healthy flowering and disease resistance.
Water
Moderate to high: water deeply 2–3 times per week in summer at the base; avoid wetting foliage.
Soil
Rich, fertile, well-drained loam with good organic matter; pH 6–6.5 ideal.
Temperature
Best at 15–25 °C; slows in extreme summer heat above 35 °C; dormant in cold winters.
Humidity
Moderate; humid air promotes fungal disease — good spacing and air flow are essential.
Feeding
Dedicated rose fertilizer every 2–3 weeks during the growing season from early spring to autumn.
Pruning & grooming
Hard prune in late winter (February in Tunisia) to 3–5 strong buds; deadhead throughout the season.
Propagation
From hardwood cuttings in autumn or budding onto rootstock.
Mature size
Hybrid teas/floribundas: 0.6–1.5 m; shrub roses can reach 2 m.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Long-lived with good care; 20–30+ years.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring to autumn; typically 2–4 main flowering flushes.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to about −15 °C depending on variety; most hybrid teas need mild winters.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; thorns cause mechanical injury only.

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Black spot on leaves (round black spots with yellow margin)Diplocarpon rosae fungal disease; common in humid conditions with overhead watering.Water only at the base; remove and dispose of infected leaves; improve airflow; apply diluted neem solution preventively.
Powdery mildew (white dusty coating on new growth)Fungal disease in dry warm days and cool humid nights.Ensure good air circulation; water at soil level; remove infected shoots.
Aphid clusters on buds and new growthVery common in spring flush of growth.Blast off with water jet; apply neem oil or insecticidal soap; attract ladybirds.
Wilting despite wateringRoot damage from over-watering, pests, or summer heat stress.Check drainage and reduce watering; mulch around the base to cool roots in summer.
Availability: Widely sold in Tunisian garden centres; one of the most popular garden plants in the country with many varieties available.
