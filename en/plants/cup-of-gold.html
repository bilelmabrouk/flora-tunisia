Cup of gold

Solandra maxima  ·  Family: Solanaceae
Exterior · Climber
EN Cup of gold  ·  FR Solandra  ·  AR سولاندرا  ·  Solandra maxima
Origin: Mexico and Central America
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Very toxic
Setting
Outdoor
In short A dramatic large-leaved climber with enormous golden-yellow fragrant cups up to 25 cm across, blooming mainly in winter and spring; best for warm coastal and southern Tunisian gardens.
Light
Full sun; needs strong sun for flowering.
Water
Moderate; regular watering in summer, reduced in winter.
Soil
Rich, well-drained soil; benefits from organic matter.
Temperature
Frost-tender; best above 7 °C; not suited to cold mountain areas.
Humidity
Tolerates coastal humidity; prefers moderate humidity.
Feeding
Balanced fertilizer every 3–4 weeks in spring and summer.
Pruning & grooming
Prune after flowering to control size; tolerates hard pruning.
Propagation
Semi-hardwood cuttings in summer.
Mature size
Up to 15 m; massive climber needing strong support.
Growth rate
Fast.
Lifespan
Long-lived in frost-free zones; decades.
Bloom / fruit season
Mainly winter and spring (October–April); sporadic at other times.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; protect below 5 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Highly toxic — all parts, especially seeds and flowers; keep away from children and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced fertilizer every 3–4 weeks during active growth supports the large flowers; reduce or stop in winter.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
No flowers on established plantInsufficient sun or over-watering.Move to full sun; let soil dry slightly between waterings.
Frost-burnt growing tipsCold spell below 5 °C.Prune damaged tips back to healthy tissue; protect in future cold spells.
Mealybugs in leaf axilsWarm, slightly dry conditions favour mealybugs.Dab with a cotton swab dipped in alcohol; spray with insecticidal soap.
Availability: Rarely available; found occasionally in specialist nurseries in coastal Tunisia.
