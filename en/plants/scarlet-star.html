Scarlet star (bromeliad)

Guzmania lingulata  ·  Family: Bromeliaceae
Interior · Flowering
EN Scarlet star (bromeliad)  ·  FR Guzmania  ·  AR غوزمانيا  ·  Guzmania lingulata
Origin: Tropical America (Ecuador, Colombia, Costa Rica)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Low
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Indoor
In short A tropical bromeliad grown for its vivid, long-lasting central star-shaped bract in red, orange, or yellow — adds exotic colour to Tunisian interiors for months. Water into the central cup and enjoy minimal care.
Light
Bright indirect light; no direct sun. Tolerates lower light but the bract colour is less vivid.
Water
Keep the central 'tank' (cup) filled with water at all times; water the soil only moderately (every 2–3 weeks). Change cup water monthly to prevent stagnation.
Soil
Fast-draining, bark-based or bromeliad-specific mix; the plant is epiphytic in nature.
Temperature
18–28 °C; do not expose to below 15 °C.
Humidity
Prefers high humidity; mist the rosette (not the cup) in dry periods.
Feeding
Dilute fertilizer in the cup water once a month in the growing season; half dose.
Pruning & grooming
Remove the central bract/spike once it fades; the mother plant then produces offsets (pups).
Propagation
Separate pups once they are about one-third the size of the mother plant and have roots.
Mature size
30–50 cm tall.
Growth rate
Slow for the mother rosette; blooms once then dies, replaced by pups.
Lifespan
The mother plant blooms once (colour lasting 3–6 months), then produces pups that repeat the cycle.
Bloom / fruit season
The coloured bract is long-lasting; actual tiny white flowers emerge from the centre.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; minimum 15 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets.

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Fading or browning central bractNatural end of flowering cycle; the mother plant is completing its life.Remove the spent bract and wait for pups to grow at the base.
Brown leaf tipsLow humidity or salt build-up in the cup.Flush the cup with plain water monthly; increase humidity.
No pups appearing after floweringInsufficient light or low temperature slowing down the plant's development.Move to a brighter, warmer spot; pups usually appear within a few months.
Availability: Available in Tunisian plant shops and florists; popular as a gift or decorative plant.
