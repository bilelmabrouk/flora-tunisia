Spinach

Spinacia oleracea  ·  Family: Amaranthaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Spinach  ·  FR Épinard  ·  AR سبناخ  ·  Spinacia oleracea
Origin: Central & South-West Asia (Persia/Iran)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Moderate
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A fast cool-season leaf vegetable beloved in Tunisian cooking. Sow in autumn or early spring; it thrives in the northern and central regions where winters are mild.
Light
Full sun in cool months; tolerates partial shade.
Water
Keep soil consistently moist; avoid waterlogging.
Soil
Fertile, well-drained, slightly alkaline soil with organic matter.
Temperature
Best at 10–18 °C; bolts quickly above 24 °C.
Humidity
Moderate; adequate air circulation reduces downy mildew.
Feeding
Nitrogen-rich feed every 2–3 weeks encourages leafy growth.
Pruning & grooming
Harvest outer leaves progressively; cut entire plant before bolting.
Propagation
Direct sow; succession sow every 2–3 weeks for continuous harvest.
Mature size
15–30 cm tall rosette.
Growth rate
Fast — harvestable in 35–50 days.
Lifespan
Annual cool-season crop.
Bloom / fruit season
Bolts and flowers in heat or long days; avoid.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tolerant to −5 °C; prefers cool winters.
Toxicity & safety
Safe; high oxalate content — moderate intake for kidney-stone sufferers.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A nitrogen-rich vegetable fertilizer applied every 2–3 weeks produces large, dark-green leaves.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Premature boltingHigh temperatures or long day length.Sow in autumn; choose bolt-resistant varieties; provide afternoon shade.
Yellow or pale leavesNitrogen deficiency or overwatering.Apply nitrogen feed; reduce watering frequency; check drainage.
White powdery patches on leavesDowny mildew fungal disease.Improve air circulation; remove affected leaves; avoid wetting foliage.
Holes or chewed leavesCaterpillars, slugs or leaf miners.Hand-pick; use row covers; keep beds clean of debris.
Availability: Commonly grown / sold in Tunisia; seeds readily available in autumn.
