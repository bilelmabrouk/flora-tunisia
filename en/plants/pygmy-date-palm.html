Pygmy date palm

Phoenix roebelenii  ·  Family: Arecaceae
Interior · Palm / statement
EN Pygmy date palm  ·  FR Palmier nain dattier  ·  AR نخيل التمر القزم  ·  Phoenix roebelenii
Origin: Laos and Yunnan (SW China), Southeast Asia
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Indoor
In short A graceful miniature date palm that brings the look of a tropical landscape into a bright Tunisian room or sheltered terrace, with elegant arching fronds and modest size.
Light
Full sun to bright indirect light; outdoors in full sun in warm coastal areas.
Water
Moderate: water when the top few cm dry; avoid waterlogging; tolerates short dry spells once established.
Soil
Sandy loam or palm mix with excellent drainage; slightly acidic preferred.
Temperature
Ideal 18–35 °C; tolerates brief dips to 5 °C but frost damage is likely below 0 °C.
Humidity
Moderate; appreciates occasional misting in dry Tunisian summers.
Feeding
Balanced palm fertilizer monthly in spring–summer; stop in autumn–winter.
Pruning & grooming
Remove fully browned fronds only; cutting green fronds stresses the plant.
Propagation
From fresh seed; germinates in 1–3 months at warm temperatures.
Mature size
2–3 m tall indoors; rarely exceeds 3 m.
Growth rate
Slow.
Lifespan
Long-lived — can persist for decades.
Bloom / fruit season
Creamy white flower clusters in spring; small dark fruits follow, not edible.
Hardiness (min temp)
Mildly frost-tolerant; protect below 0 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets; sharp spines are a physical hazard.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A slow-release palm fertilizer rich in potassium and with added micronutrients, applied in spring and midsummer, supports frond colour and prevents deficiency chlorosis.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellowing of older frondsMagnesium or potassium deficiency.Apply a palm fertilizer with micronutrients; check soil pH.
Spear leaf fails to open, turns brownManganese deficiency or crown rot.Apply foliar manganese sulfate spray; if crown rot is suspected, cut away affected tissue and treat with a copper fungicide.
Fine webbing and tiny dots under the leafletsSpider mites, common in hot dry conditions.Rinse foliage with water, increase humidity, and apply insecticidal soap if needed.
Numerous sharp spines injuring hands during careNatural defensive spines along the leaf petioles.Wear thick gloves and long sleeves when pruning or repotting; place away from walkways.
Availability: Popular as an ornamental palm for Tunisian gardens, terraces, and hotel landscaping, available at specialised nurseries.
