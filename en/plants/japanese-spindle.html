Japanese spindle

Euonymus japonicus  ·  Family: Celastraceae
Exterior · Shrub
EN Japanese spindle  ·  FR Fusain du Japon  ·  AR فوزان ياباني  ·  Euonymus japonicus
Origin: Japan, Korea, and China
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short A stalwart evergreen hedging shrub with tough, glossy leaves — adaptable from full shade to full sun, wind and salt-resistant, and one of the most widely used hedge plants across Tunisian gardens north to south.
Light
Full sun to full shade; variegated forms retain better colour in bright light.
Water
Moderate; water regularly until established; fairly drought-tolerant afterwards.
Soil
Adaptable; prefers well-drained, fertile soil but tolerates most conditions.
Temperature
Hardy to about −15 °C; suits all Tunisian zones.
Humidity
Good salt and wind tolerance; excellent for coastal planting.
Feeding
Light balanced fertiliser in spring; not essential.
Pruning & grooming
Clip hedges 1–2 times per year; tolerates hard renovation pruning.
Propagation
From semi-ripe cuttings in summer.
Mature size
2–4 m unpruned; kept easily at 0.5–1.5 m as a hedge.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Long-lived; 30+ years.
Bloom / fruit season
Early summer (May–June); inconspicuous greenish flowers; followed by small pink-orange fruit capsules.
Hardiness (min temp)
Very frost-hardy; suitable for all Tunisian regions including the Tell.
Toxicity & safety
Mildly toxic: berries and leaves can cause stomach upset — keep away from children and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A light balanced fertiliser in spring keeps the foliage dense and healthy; no autumn feeding needed.  Find fertilizer →
