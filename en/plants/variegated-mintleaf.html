Variegated mintleaf (Swedish ivy) ⇄ also: Interior

Plectranthus madagascariensis  ·  Family: Lamiaceae
Exterior · Ground-cover
EN Variegated mintleaf (Swedish ivy)  ·  FR Plectranthe de Madagascar panaché  ·  AR النعناع المزيّن (بلكترانتس)  ·  Plectranthus madagascariensis
Origin: Southern Africa & Madagascar
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Moderate
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Indoor & out
In short A tough, mint-scented trailer with cream-edged leaves; superb spilling from pots and hanging baskets or as a shaded ground-cover, and very easy to root.
Light
Part shade to bright indirect light; tolerates morning sun.
Water
Water when the top few cm dry; drought-tolerant once established.
Soil
Any free-draining potting or garden soil.
Temperature
Best 15–28 °C; frost-tender.
Humidity
Undemanding; tolerates dry coastal air.
Feeding
Light monthly feed in spring–summer.
Pruning & grooming
Trim regularly to keep dense; use the trimmings as cuttings.
Propagation
Extremely easy from stem cuttings, in soil or water.
Mature size
Trails 30–60 cm; spreads as ground-cover.
Growth rate
Fast.
Lifespan
Long-lived perennial in mild areas.
Bloom / fruit season
Small white–mauve spikes in autumn.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tender; protect below ~5 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Considered non-toxic and pet-safe.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A light general feed in the growing season is plenty; it thrives on little.  Find fertilizer →

Mushy stemsOver-watering.Let dry out; improve drainage.
