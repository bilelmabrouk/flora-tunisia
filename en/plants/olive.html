Olive

Olea europaea  ·  Family: Oleaceae
Trees · Olive / native
EN Olive  ·  FR Olivier  ·  AR زيتونة  ·  Olea europaea
Origin: Mediterranean basin native
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short Tunisia's most iconic tree — the olive has shaped the landscape and economy for millennia. Supremely drought-tolerant, long-lived, and productive, it thrives across all six Tunisian regions.
Light
Full sun; needs at least 6–8 hours daily for good fruit production.
Water
Very low once established; deep irrigation every 2–4 weeks in summer, almost none in winter.
Soil
Well-drained, even poor or calcareous soil; hates waterlogging.
Temperature
Tolerates −10 °C when mature; needs warm summers to ripen fruit.
Humidity
Prefers dry air; tolerates coastal salt spray.
Feeding
Light balanced fertilizer in late winter/early spring; avoid excess nitrogen.
Pruning & grooming
Annual thinning in winter to open canopy, improve airflow and fruit access.
Propagation
From semi-hardwood cuttings in summer, or grafting.
Mature size
5–15 m depending on variety and pruning; often kept at 3–6 m in production orchards.
Growth rate
Slow to moderate.
Lifespan
Can live for centuries to millennia.
Bloom / fruit season
Small white fragrant flowers in spring (April–June).
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −10 °C when established; young trees need frost protection.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; fruit is edible (requires curing before eating).
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Apply a balanced fertilizer (NPK) in late winter before new growth; a potassium-rich feed in late summer helps harden wood and improve fruit quality.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Olive fruit fly damage (tunnelled fruit)Bactrocera oleae larvae feeding inside the fruit.Use kaolin clay sprays or yellow sticky traps; harvest promptly when ripe.
Peacock spot (dark circular leaf lesions)Fungal disease Spilocaea oleagina, favoured by wet winters.Apply copper-based fungicide in autumn; improve airflow by pruning.
Yellowing and leaf dropOver-watering or waterlogged roots.Reduce irrigation; check drainage; avoid mulching against the trunk.
Sooty mould on leavesBlack scale insect honeydew.Treat scale with horticultural oil spray in spring; wipe mould off.
Availability: Ubiquitous throughout Tunisia; widely sold as young trees in nurseries and markets.
