French marigold

Tagetes patula  ·  Family: Asteraceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN French marigold  ·  FR Œillet d'Inde  ·  AR قطيفة الهند  ·  Tagetes patula
Origin: Mexico
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A compact, heat-tough summer annual beloved in Tunisian gardens for its vivid orange and yellow flowers, natural pest-repelling scent, and cheerful, reliable blooming.
Light
Full sun — 6+ hours daily; tolerates some afternoon shade in very hot southern regions.
Water
Moderate: water at the base when soil surface dries; drought-tolerant once established.
Soil
Well-drained average garden soil; too rich a soil promotes leaves over flowers.
Temperature
Ideal 18–30 °C; handles Tunisia's summer heat well; frost kills it.
Humidity
Average to low; wet foliage encourages botrytis — always water at the base.
Feeding
Monthly with a low-nitrogen flowering-plant fertilizer; excess nitrogen reduces blooming.
Pruning & grooming
Deadhead spent flowers consistently to promote continuous blooming.
Propagation
From seed sown indoors 6 weeks before last frost or directly in warm soil.
Mature size
20–40 cm tall and wide.
Growth rate
Fast — flowers in 6–8 weeks from seed.
Lifespan
Annual; blooms from spring to frost.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring through late autumn (April–October in Tunisia).
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; dies below 0 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; the flowers are edible.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A phosphorus-rich flowering-plant fertilizer at low dose monthly keeps blooming strong; avoid high-nitrogen formulas that push leafy growth at the expense of flowers.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Grey mould on flowers and stemsBotrytis blight from poor airflow and wet conditions.Remove affected parts; improve spacing and ventilation; water at the base only.
Spider mite webbing, pale stipplingSpider mites in hot dry weather.Mist undersides of leaves; keep well watered; use neem oil spray.
Sticky residue and clusters of small insects on budsAphid infestation, common on tender new growth.Wash off with a water jet; use insecticidal soap for persistent colonies.
Few flowers and mostly leafy growthExcess nitrogen fertilizer or insufficient sunlight.Reduce nitrogen, switch to a phosphorus-rich bloom fertilizer, and ensure full sun exposure.
Availability: A classic and inexpensive Tunisian bedding annual, widely sold as seedlings in nurseries, souks, and supermarket garden sections.
