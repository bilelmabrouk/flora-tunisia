Sage ⇄ also: Interior

Salvia officinalis  ·  Family: Lamiaceae
Exterior · Herb / aromatic

Origin: Mediterranean

Setting
Indoor & out
In short A silver-grey Mediterranean herb deeply rooted in Tunisian herbal tradition — used medicinally and in cooking. Drought-hardy and sun-loving, it thrives in virtually any Tunisian garden with minimal fuss.
Light
Full sun; at least 6 hours — essential for flavour and compact growth.
Water
Low: drought-tolerant; water only when the top few cm of soil are dry.
Soil
Well-drained, moderately fertile, slightly alkaline; sandy-loam is ideal.
Temperature
Best at 15–30 °C; tolerates light frost to −5 °C; survives brief summer heat.
Humidity
Prefers low to moderate humidity; high coastal humidity can cause stem rot.
Feeding
Light spring feed with a balanced fertilizer; excessive feeding reduces aroma.
Pruning & grooming
Trim by one-third after flowering; reshape in early spring to prevent woodiness.
Propagation
From cuttings (most reliable), division, or seed.
Mature size
40–80 cm tall; bushy mound.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Short-lived perennial: 4–6 years; replace from cuttings.
Bloom / fruit season
Violet-blue spikes spring to early summer (April–June).
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −5 °C; tolerates all but the coldest Tunisian highlands.
Toxicity & safety
Safe in normal culinary use; large medicinal doses (especially essential oil) should be avoided — do not use during pregnancy.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A single application of balanced fertilizer in early spring is enough; sage in lean soil has stronger flavour and aroma.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Wilting and blackened stemsRoot or stem rot from overwatering.Remove affected parts; improve drainage; reduce watering sharply.
Leggy, open growthInsufficient sun or no annual pruning.Move to full sun; cut back by one-third in early spring.
White powdery spots on leavesPowdery mildew in humid, poorly ventilated spots.Improve air circulation; prune congested growth; avoid overhead watering.
Leaves pale and smallNutrient-depleted soil.Top-dress with compost in spring; apply balanced fertilizer.
Availability: Commonly available in Tunisian nurseries and herb markets; popular in both gardens and pots.
