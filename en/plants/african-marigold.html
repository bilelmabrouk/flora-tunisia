African marigold

Tagetes erecta  ·  Family: Asteraceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN African marigold  ·  FR Tagète dressé  ·  AR قطيفة أفريقية  ·  Tagetes erecta
Origin: Mexico (despite the common name 'African')
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A tall, bold summer annual with large, globe-shaped flowers in deep orange and yellow — a Tunisian garden staple and a natural pest deterrent used around vegetables.
Light
Full sun — 6–8 hours minimum; fewer hours means fewer and smaller flowers.
Water
Moderate: water at the base when the top 4–5 cm dry out; avoid wetting flowers and foliage.
Soil
Well-drained garden soil; tolerates poor to average soil; excess fertility promotes foliage.
Temperature
Ideal 20–32 °C; handles heat well; frost kills it.
Humidity
Low to moderate; wet flowers rot — always water at the base.
Feeding
A phosphorus-rich fertilizer every 3–4 weeks during the growing season.
Pruning & grooming
Deadhead regularly; pinch seedlings at 15 cm to encourage branching.
Propagation
From seed sown indoors 6–8 weeks before transplanting or directly in warm soil.
Mature size
60–90 cm tall, 30–45 cm wide.
Growth rate
Fast — flowers in 8–10 weeks from seed.
Lifespan
Annual; grows from spring to autumn.
Bloom / fruit season
Summer through autumn (May–October in Tunisia).
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; plant after last frost.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; flowers edible.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced flowering-plant fertilizer with elevated phosphorus every 3–4 weeks from planting supports large, long-lasting flower heads without excessive vegetative growth.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Flowers rotting on the plantWet flowers from overhead watering or prolonged rain.Deadhead promptly; switch to base watering; improve airflow.
Aphid clusters on tender shootsAphid infestation, common in warm weather.Remove by hand or strong water jet; encourage beneficial insects like ladybirds.
White powdery patches on leavesPowdery mildew, favoured by warm days and cool humid nights.Improve air circulation, avoid wetting foliage, and remove badly affected leaves.
Leggy, sparse plants with few bloomsInsufficient sunlight or overcrowding.Thin out crowded plants and ensure at least 6 hours of direct sun daily.
Availability: A staple bedding annual across Tunisia, sold cheaply as seedlings every spring in nurseries, souks, and supermarket garden aisles.
