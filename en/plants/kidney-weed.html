Kidney weed

Dichondra micrantha  ·  Family: Convolvulaceae
Exterior · Ground-cover
EN Kidney weed  ·  FR Dichondra  ·  AR ديكوندرا  ·  Dichondra micrantha
Origin: East Asia and eastern Australia
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A creeping, kidney-shaped-leafed groundcover used as a low-maintenance lawn alternative in shaded or semi-shaded northern Tunisian gardens. Stays green year-round and forms a dense, weed-suppressing carpet.
Light
Bright shade to full sun; performs best in semi-shade in hot Tunisian summers.
Water
Moderate; keep moist, especially in summer; not as drought-tolerant as succulents.
Soil
Moist, well-drained loam or sandy loam; tolerates most garden soils with some compost.
Temperature
Best 15–30 °C; frost-tender below −2 °C; can yellow in cool winters.
Humidity
Tolerates coastal humidity; prefers moderate moisture.
Feeding
Monthly balanced feed in spring–summer to keep the carpet dense and green.
Pruning & grooming
Mow or trim to 3–5 cm to keep dense; tolerates regular cutting.
Propagation
Easy from seed sown in spring, or by spreading stem sections with nodes.
Mature size
2–5 cm tall, spreads indefinitely.
Growth rate
Moderate to fast in warm moist conditions.
Lifespan
Perennial in frost-free zones; may die back in cold winters.
Bloom / fruit season
Flowers negligible and hidden; grown for foliage only.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tender; not suited to heavy frost areas (region 2 risky).
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Apply a balanced lawn-type fertilizer monthly during the growing season (spring to early autumn) to keep the mat lush and green; reduce to every 6–8 weeks in mild winters.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellowing and thinning of the carpetInsufficient light, underwatering in heat, or nitrogen deficiency.Move to brighter spot or increase water and fertilizer in summer.
Patches dying in summer heatDrought stress or direct reflected heat from hard surfaces.Water more frequently in summer; shade with a light net if exposed to afternoon sun reflection.
Snails eating through the carpetMoist, dense groundcover is ideal snail habitat.Set traps or use iron phosphate-based bait; remove debris where snails hide.
Availability: Found in garden centres in northern and coastal Tunisia; sold as seed or plugs for lawn alternatives.
