Cauliflower

Brassica oleracea var. botrytis  ·  Family: Brassicaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Cauliflower  ·  FR Chou-fleur  ·  AR قرنبيط / شفلور  ·  Brassica oleracea var. botrytis
Origin: Mediterranean (likely Asia Minor, developed in Cyprus & Italy)
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A cool-season vegetable grown widely in Tunisian autumn–spring cycles for its dense white curds. Needs consistent moisture, fertile soil and mild temperatures for a quality head.
Light
Full sun (6+ hours); head quality suffers in shade.
Water
Even moisture essential; drought causes small, loose or bitter curds.
Soil
Rich, well-drained, moisture-retentive loam; pH 6.5–7.5.
Temperature
Best at 15–20 °C; poor curd development below 5 °C or above 25 °C.
Humidity
Moderate; avoid overhead watering to prevent rot.
Feeding
Nitrogen-rich feed early; switch to balanced feed as curd develops.
Pruning & grooming
Tie outer leaves over the curd to protect whiteness (blanching).
Propagation
Sow in seed trays; transplant 4–5 weeks later.
Mature size
Head 15–30 cm; plant 50–60 cm wide.
Growth rate
Moderate — curd-ready in 70–100 days from transplant.
Lifespan
Annual; one harvest per plant.
Bloom / fruit season
Curd opens and flowers if left; harvest before this.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tolerates light frost; not suitable for harsh frost regions without protection.
Toxicity & safety
Fully safe to eat.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A nitrogen-rich fertilizer at transplanting and a balanced feed once the head begins forming gives large, firm curds.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Small, loose or discoloured curdHeat stress, irregular watering or nutrient shortage.Plant in cooler months; keep soil moist; feed regularly.
Caterpillar damage on outer leavesCabbage white butterfly caterpillars.Hand-pick; use fine-mesh covers over young plants.
Brown spots on curd surfaceBoron deficiency.Apply a micronutrient fertilizer containing boron; avoid liming too heavily.
Availability: Commonly grown / sold in Tunisia; a staple winter vegetable in markets.
