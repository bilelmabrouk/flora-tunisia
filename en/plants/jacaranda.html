Jacaranda ⇄ also: avenue

Jacaranda mimosifolia  ·  Family: Bignoniaceae
Trees · Ornamental / shade
EN Jacaranda  ·  FR Jacaranda  ·  AR جاكاراندا  ·  Jacaranda mimosifolia
Origin: Argentina and Bolivia (Andean foothills)

Full sun
Water

Setting
Outdoor
In short A spectacular avenue tree smothering itself in violet-blue blossom each spring — a Tunisian coastal-city landmark. Needs full sun and warmth; too frost-tender for northern inland zones.
Light
Full sun — at least 6 hours daily; flowers poorly in shade.
Water
Low to moderate once established; water young trees regularly, cut back in summer drought.
Soil
Well-drained sandy loam or light clay; avoids waterlogging.
Temperature
Thrives at 15–30 °C; damaged below −3 °C; unsuitable for frost-prone inland areas.
Humidity
Tolerates coastal humidity and dry air equally well.
Feeding
Light balanced feed in early spring; avoid excess nitrogen which promotes foliage over flowers.
Pruning & grooming
Prune lightly after flowering to shape; remove crossing branches; never cut back hard.
Propagation
From semi-hardwood cuttings in summer or from seed (slow — 3–7 years to flower).
Mature size
10–15 m tall, spreading canopy 8–10 m wide.
Growth rate
Moderate to fast once established.
Lifespan
50–100 years in suitable conditions.
Bloom / fruit season
April to June, carpeting the ground with violet petals.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender: tolerates light frost to about −3 °C for short periods only.
Toxicity & safety
Mildly toxic if ingested (leaves and flowers); keep away from children and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A low-nitrogen, phosphorus-rich fertilizer applied once in early spring encourages flowering rather than leafy growth.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
No flowersInsufficient sun, excess nitrogen, or tree too young.Move to full sun; switch to low-nitrogen feed; be patient with young specimens.
Yellowing leavesIron deficiency (chlorosis) in alkaline or waterlogged soil.Apply chelated iron; improve drainage; avoid over-watering.
Leaf scorch on edgesHot dry wind or salt spray.Shield young trees from drying winds; deep-water during summer heat.
Powdery mildew on leavesHigh humidity with poor airflow.Thin the canopy for airflow; avoid wetting foliage when watering.
Availability: Commonly grown in Tunisian coastal cities; available in garden centres, especially in spring.
