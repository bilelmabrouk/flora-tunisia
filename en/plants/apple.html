Apple

Malus domestica  ·  Family: Rosaceae
Trees · Fruit / nut
EN Apple  ·  FR Pommier  ·  AR تفّاح  ·  Malus domestica
Origin: Central Asia (Kazakhstan / Tian Shan mountains region)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short The classic orchard fruit, grown mainly in the cooler highlands and north of Tunisia. Apples need chilling hours for good fruit set and repay careful pruning with reliable annual harvests.
Light
Full sun for at least 6–8 hours; shaded trees produce less and poorer fruit.
Water
Moderate; regular deep watering especially in spring and summer; reduce in autumn.
Soil
Deep, fertile, well-drained loam, slightly acidic to neutral (pH 6–7); avoid heavy clay.
Temperature
Hardy to −20 °C; needs 800–1500 chilling hours below 7 °C depending on variety. Low-chill varieties work for Tunisian conditions.
Humidity
Moderate; high humidity combined with poor air flow encourages fungal diseases.
Feeding
Balanced fruit-tree fertilizer in early spring and a lighter potassium feed in early summer.
Pruning & grooming
Annual winter pruning essential to maintain open crown, remove dead wood and control size.
Propagation
By grafting onto appropriate rootstock; seedlings will not come true.
Mature size
3–8 m on standard rootstock; 1.5–3 m on dwarfing rootstock.
Growth rate
Moderate; grafted trees fruit from year 3–5.
Lifespan
50–100 years with good management.
Bloom / fruit season
Beautiful white-pink flowers March–May; fruit ripens July–October depending on variety.
Hardiness (min temp)
Very frost-hardy; highland Tunisia (regions 1, 2, 4) most suitable.
Toxicity & safety
Fruit safe and nutritious; apple seeds contain amygdalin — avoid eating large quantities.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced fruit-tree fertilizer in early spring supports blossom and fruit set; reduce nitrogen in mid-season to encourage fruit development over leafy growth.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Velvety dark spots on fruit skin (scab)Apple scab fungus (Venturia inaequalis), common in wet spring weather.Use scab-resistant varieties; apply copper or sulphur fungicide at bud break and early bloom.
Powdery white coating on young shootsPowdery mildew (Podosphaera leucotricha).Prune out affected shoots; improve air circulation; apply sulphur or potassium bicarbonate.
Tunnelled flesh in fruit — small exit holesCodling moth (Cydia pomonella) larvae.Use pheromone traps to monitor; apply targeted insecticide at egg hatching; remove fallen fruit.
Shoots wilt rapidly and turn brown, hooked tipFire blight (Erwinia amylovora) bacterial infection.Cut 30 cm below infection; sterilise tools; apply copper spray preventatively in spring.
Availability: Grafted apple trees available in Tunisian nurseries; low-chill varieties recommended for most of the country.
