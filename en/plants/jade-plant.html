Jade plant ⇄ also: Exterior

Crassula ovata  ·  Family: Crassulaceae
Interior · Succulent / cactus
EN Jade plant  ·  FR Arbre de jade  ·  AR شجرة اليشم  ·  Crassula ovata
Origin: South Africa (Eastern Cape, KwaZulu-Natal)
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Indoor & out
In short A classic long-lived succulent that develops into a miniature tree — glossy green oval leaves on woody branches. Almost indestructible in bright sunny Tunisian conditions, drought-tolerant, and a symbol of good fortune.
Light
Full sun to very bright indirect light; 4+ hours of direct sun daily gives the best compact, robust growth.
Water
Very drought-tolerant: water every 2–3 weeks in summer, once a month in winter. Always allow soil to dry out completely between waterings.
Soil
Sandy, fast-draining succulent or cactus mix; do not use regular potting soil alone.
Temperature
Ideal 15–25 °C; tolerates up to 35 °C with good drainage; protect below 5 °C.
Humidity
Thrives in dry conditions — well adapted to Tunisia's low-humidity climate.
Feeding
Once in spring and once in summer with a diluted succulent fertilizer; never in winter.
Pruning & grooming
Prune lightly after any leggy growth to maintain the tree shape; large plants can be cut back hard.
Propagation
Stem or leaf cuttings left to callus for 2–3 days before planting in dry mix; very easy.
Mature size
30 cm–1.2 m tall as a pot plant; can reach 2–3 m in the ground in warm climates.
Growth rate
Slow.
Lifespan
Decades — among the longest-lived houseplants.
Bloom / fruit season
Clusters of tiny star-shaped white or pale pink flowers in winter on mature plants.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tolerates brief light frost; best kept above 5 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Toxic if ingested — causes vomiting and incoordination in cats and dogs; keep away from pets and children.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A diluted succulent fertilizer (low nitrogen, higher phosphorus and potassium) twice a year in spring and early summer is all this slow-grower needs; excess feeding produces weak, leggy growth.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Soft, mushy stem or dropping leavesRoot rot from overwatering.Remove from pot, trim rotted roots, dry for several days, replant in fresh dry cactus mix.
Wrinkled leaves despite moist soilRoot rot — damaged roots cannot absorb water.Unpot and inspect roots; trim rotted parts; repot and reduce watering.
Leggy, stretched stems reaching for lightInsufficient light.Move to the sunniest spot available; gradually introduce to outdoor sun.
Mealy bugs at stem jointsDry indoor conditions; common on succulents.Dab with rubbing alcohol on a cotton bud; treat with diluted neem oil.
Availability: Widely available in Tunisian plant shops and markets; a popular gift plant and home décor succulent.
