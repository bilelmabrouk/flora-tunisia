Papyrus

Cyperus papyrus  ·  Family: Cyperaceae
Exterior · Ornamental grass
EN Papyrus  ·  FR Papyrus  ·  AR بردي  ·  Cyperus papyrus
Origin: Tropical Africa and the Nile valley
Light
Full sun
Water
Keep wet
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short The iconic ancient-Egyptian papyrus, with tall green stems topped by feathery umbrella heads. Superb for pond edges and water features across coastal and southern Tunisia where winters are mild.
Light
Full sun; needs maximum light for strong upright stems.
Water
Permanently wet; grow in shallow water (up to 30 cm deep) or keep soil saturated.
Soil
Heavy, fertile, moisture-retentive soil; thrives in pond mud.
Temperature
Frost-tender; best above 10 C; dies back below 0 C, may re-sprout from rhizomes.
Humidity
Loves high humidity; does well along the Tunisian coast.
Feeding
Balanced slow-release fertilizer pushed into the soil or water in spring and summer.
Pruning & grooming
Remove brown or bent stems at the base; cut back frost-damaged tops in spring.
Propagation
By division of rhizomes in spring; stems can be rooted upside-down in water.
Mature size
1.5-4 m tall in good conditions.
Growth rate
Fast in warm, wet conditions.
Lifespan
Perennial; long-lived with reliable water.
Bloom / fruit season
Small greenish flower clusters at stem tips in summer.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; protect or bring inside if temperatures drop below 5 C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to people and animals.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Push a balanced slow-release tablet or granule into the soil or pond substrate once in spring and once in summer to fuel the fast growth without polluting the water.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Stems flop or topple overInsufficient light or root-bound in a small container.Move to full sun; repot or transplant to a larger container or pond.
Yellowing stemsNutrient deficiency or plant running out of root space.Add slow-release fertiliser; divide and replant in fresh substrate.
Browning tips on the umbrella raysLow humidity or water level dropping too low.Maintain water level; mist the heads in dry weather.
Availability: Available in Tunisian water-garden specialists and larger general nurseries along the coast.
