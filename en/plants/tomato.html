Tomato

Solanum lycopersicum  ·  Family: Solanaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Tomato  ·  FR Tomate  ·  AR طماطم  ·  Solanum lycopersicum
Origin: Andes (western South America)
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short Tunisia's most popular kitchen vegetable — sown from seed in late winter and harvested through summer and autumn; heat-loving and rewarding when given water, sun, and regular feeding.
Light
Full sun — minimum 6–8 hours direct sun per day for good fruit set.
Water
Moderate to high: water deeply and consistently; irregular watering causes blossom end rot and cracking.
Soil
Rich, well-drained loam with compost; slightly acidic (pH 6–6.8) is ideal.
Temperature
Best fruit set at 18–27 °C; fruit drops if night temperatures exceed 30 °C for long.
Humidity
Moderate; high humidity promotes fungal diseases — ensure good air circulation.
Feeding
Start with balanced feed; switch to high-potassium fertilizer once flowering begins.
Pruning & grooming
Pinch out sideshoots (suckers) on cordon varieties; stake or cage indeterminate types.
Propagation
From seed indoors 6–8 weeks before last frost; transplant when 15–20 cm tall.
Mature size
50 cm–2 m+ depending on variety; train on stakes or cages.
Growth rate
Fast in warm weather.
Lifespan
Annual (or short-lived perennial in frost-free zones).
Bloom / fruit season
Yellow flowers spring through summer; fruit follows 45–80 days after setting.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; sow after last cold spell in Tunisia (Feb–March in the south, March–April elsewhere).
Toxicity & safety
Fruit is safe to eat; leaves and stems contain mildly toxic alkaloids — not edible.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Start with a balanced vegetable fertilizer at planting; once first flowers open, switch to a high-potassium formula to promote fruit size and flavour.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Blossom end rot (dark sunken patch on fruit base)Calcium deficiency due to irregular watering.Water consistently and deeply; mulch to retain moisture; ensure soil calcium is adequate.
Leaf curl in summer heatHeat stress or physiological response to strong sun.Ensure deep, regular watering; provide light afternoon shade during peak Tunisian summer heat.
Whitefly infestationCommon pest in warm Tunisian summers.Use yellow sticky traps; spray with dilute soap solution in the early morning.
Early blight (dark spots with yellow halo on lower leaves)Alternaria fungus, aggravated by wet foliage.Water at the base only; remove affected leaves; improve air circulation; rotate crops annually.
Availability: Seedlings and seeds very widely available throughout Tunisia in spring; grown in virtually every Tunisian vegetable garden.
