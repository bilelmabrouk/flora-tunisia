Kale

Brassica oleracea var. sabellica  ·  Family: Brassicaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Kale  ·  FR Chou frisé  ·  AR كرنب أجعد  ·  Brassica oleracea var. sabellica
Origin: Western Europe (Atlantic coasts; one of the oldest cultivated brassicas)
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A cold-hardy, highly nutritious leafy brassica increasingly grown in Tunisian cool-season gardens. Best in the cooler north and north-west; frost actually sweetens the leaves.
Light
Full sun; tolerates partial shade in hot periods.
Water
Regular moisture; does not like to dry out completely.
Soil
Fertile, well-drained, slightly alkaline soil; pH 6.5–7.5.
Temperature
Best at 10–18 °C; frost-hardy; flavour improves after frost.
Humidity
Average; good air circulation reduces disease.
Feeding
Nitrogen-rich fertilizer every 3–4 weeks promotes leaf growth.
Pruning & grooming
Harvest outer leaves from the base, leaving the central growing point.
Propagation
Direct sow or transplant; sow Aug–Sep for winter harvest.
Mature size
60–90 cm tall.
Growth rate
Moderate — harvestable in 55–75 days.
Lifespan
Annual or biennial; can produce over winter and into spring.
Bloom / fruit season
Flowers in spring of second year; remove to extend harvest.
Hardiness (min temp)
Very frost-hardy to −10 °C or colder.
Toxicity & safety
Safe and highly nutritious.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A nitrogen-rich vegetable fertilizer every 3–4 weeks produces large, dark, tender leaves throughout the season.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Caterpillar holes in leavesCabbage white butterfly or other caterpillars.Hand-pick; use fine netting covers.
Yellow lower leavesNormal ageing or nitrogen deficiency.Remove yellowed leaves; apply nitrogen fertilizer.
White powdery coating on leavesPowdery mildew fungal disease.Improve air circulation; remove affected leaves.
Availability: Increasingly available in Tunisia; found in garden centres and speciality markets, especially in autumn.
