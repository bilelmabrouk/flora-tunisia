Scarlet sage

Salvia splendens  ·  Family: Lamiaceae
Exterior · Flowering / bedding
EN Scarlet sage  ·  FR Sauge rouge  ·  AR مريمية حمراء  ·  Salvia splendens
Origin: Brazil
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short Vivid scarlet spikes that light up Tunisian summer borders from spring to autumn — scarlet sage loves heat, tolerates drought once established, and makes a striking contrast against white walls and pale foliage.
Light
Full sun to light partial shade — deeper red colour in full sun.
Water
Moderate: keep soil evenly moist but not waterlogged; tolerates brief drought.
Soil
Fertile, well-drained soil with added compost.
Temperature
Best at 18–32 °C; frost-tender; thrives in Tunisian summer heat.
Humidity
Tolerates moderate to high humidity; ensure good airflow to prevent botrytis.
Feeding
Balanced fertilizer every 2–3 weeks from spring through autumn.
Pruning & grooming
Deadhead spent spikes to encourage more flowers; pinch young plants to branch.
Propagation
From seed in spring or from tip cuttings.
Mature size
20–40 cm tall, 15–25 cm wide.
Growth rate
Fast — flowers within 10–12 weeks of sowing.
Lifespan
Annual in Tunisia; grown as a perennial in frost-free coastal zones.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring through autumn; virtually non-stop in warm conditions.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; minimum 10 °C for healthy growth outdoors.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets.

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Wilting despite adequate wateringRoot rot from waterlogging or fungal wilt.Improve drainage; check roots; remove severely affected plants.
Grey mould on flower spikesBotrytis in damp, poorly ventilated conditions.Remove infected spikes; improve air circulation; avoid wetting flowers.
Pale leaves, slow growthNutrient deficiency or cold soil early in the season.Apply a balanced liquid fertilizer; ensure soil has warmed to at least 15 °C.
Availability: Widely sold as seedling trays in Tunisian garden centres and markets from spring.
