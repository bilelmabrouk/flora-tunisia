Peppermint

Mentha piperita  ·  Family: Lamiaceae
Exterior · Herb / aromatic
EN Peppermint  ·  FR Menthe poivrée  ·  AR نعناع فلفلي  ·  Mentha piperita
Origin: Natural hybrid (M. aquatica × M. spicata), widespread in Europe and Mediterranean

Sun / part shade

Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short The stronger, more medicinal cousin of spearmint — with dark leaves and a sharp menthol scent. Great in a Tunisian herb garden for teas, digestion remedies, and cooling summer drinks.
Light
Full sun to partial shade; morning sun with afternoon shade is ideal in summer.
Water
Moderate: keep soil evenly moist; water every 1–2 days in hot dry weather.
Soil
Rich, moist, well-drained; add compost for best flavour; tolerates slightly acidic soil.
Temperature
Best at 13–22 °C; roots survive frost to −15 °C; top growth dies back and regrows.
Humidity
Moderate to high; benefits from mulch to retain moisture in summer.
Feeding
A nitrogen-rich herb fertilizer monthly in the growing season; avoid in winter.
Pruning & grooming
Harvest frequently; cut back flower stems before they open to maximise leaf production.
Propagation
Stem cuttings root easily in water or moist soil; also by root division.
Mature size
30–60 cm tall; spreads by underground runners.
Growth rate
Fast; can be invasive — best in a pot or with a root barrier.
Lifespan
Perennial; regrows annually from roots.
Bloom / fruit season
Lavender-pink flower spikes in mid-to-late summer; pinch back to encourage leafy growth.
Hardiness (min temp)
Roots cold-hardy to −15 °C; stems die in frost; very adaptable.
Toxicity & safety
Safe for culinary use in normal quantities; large medicinal doses can be harmful — do not give to infants.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A nitrogen-rich herb fertilizer monthly during the growing season promotes vigorous leafy growth; too much nitrogen at flowering time reduces menthol intensity.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Wilting and yellowingRoot rot from overwatering or poorly drained soil.Improve drainage; let soil dry slightly; remove rotten roots and replant.
Mint rust (orange spots)Puccinia menthae fungal disease in humid conditions.Remove infected leaves; improve airflow; avoid wetting foliage.
Rapid spread overtaking gardenVigorous underground stolons.Plant in a submerged pot or contained bed; trim runners at the edges every few weeks.
Weak, small leaves with poor scentOver-harvesting, poor light, or nutrient deficiency.Allow partial regrowth before the next harvest; feed monthly; move to better light.
Availability: Readily available in markets and garden centres throughout Tunisia; less universal than spearmint but still widely grown.
