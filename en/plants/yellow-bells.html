Yellow bells (yellow trumpetbush)

Tecoma stans  ·  Family: Bignoniaceae
Exterior · Shrub
EN Yellow bells (yellow trumpetbush)  ·  FR Tecoma jaune (bignone jaune)  ·  AR الجرس الأصفر (تيكوما)  ·  Tecoma stans
Origin: Tropical Americas
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A tough, fast shrub smothered in golden trumpet flowers all summer; loves heat and shrugs off drought once rooted.
Light
Full sun for the heaviest flowering.
Water
Low once established; deep soak every 10–14 days in summer.
Soil
Any well-drained soil; tolerates poor and rocky ground.
Temperature
Loves heat 20–35 °C; tender below 0 °C.
Humidity
Undemanding; dry Tunisian air suits it.
Feeding
Light feed in spring; too much nitrogen means leaves not flowers.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back hard in late winter to keep it dense and flowering.
Propagation
From seed or softwood cuttings in warmth.
Mature size
2–4 m tall and wide as a shrub.
Growth rate
Fast.
Lifespan
Long-lived perennial shrub.
Bloom / fruit season
Late spring through autumn.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to about −3 °C; may die back then reshoot.
Toxicity & safety
Not considered toxic to pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced flowering-plant fertilizer in spring, low on nitrogen, keeps blooms coming.  Find fertilizer →
