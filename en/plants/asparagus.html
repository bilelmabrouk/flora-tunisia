Asparagus

Asparagus officinalis  ·  Family: Asparagaceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Asparagus  ·  FR Asperge  ·  AR هليون  ·  Asparagus officinalis
Origin: Mediterranean basin and Eurasia
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Demanding
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A long-term perennial vegetable that rewards patience: the first harvest comes only in year three. Once established, an asparagus bed produces spears every spring for 15-20 years in Tunisia's north.
Light
Full sun; at least 8 hours daily for strong spear production.
Water
Low to moderate; water regularly to establish; drought-tolerant once mature.
Soil
Deep (40+ cm), very well-drained, sandy loam; slightly alkaline (pH 6.5-7.5); add grit to clay.
Temperature
Needs a cool winter dormancy; suited to Tunisia's north and north-west.
Humidity
Average; good air circulation reduces fungal disease.
Feeding
Heavy feeder: apply compost and balanced fertilizer in autumn and again after harvest.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back ferns to the ground after they yellow in autumn; do not cut before yellowing.
Propagation
From one-year-old crowns planted in spring, or from seed (takes longer).
Mature size
Ferns grow 1-1.5 m tall in summer; spears harvested at 15-25 cm.
Growth rate
Slow establishment (2-3 years before first harvest); very long-lived thereafter.
Lifespan
Perennial; productive for 15-25 years with proper care.
Bloom / fruit season
Small yellow-green bell flowers on fern foliage in summer, followed by red berries.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to -15 C or below when dormant.
Toxicity & safety
Spears and ferns are non-toxic to humans; red berries are mildly toxic to pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Asparagus is a heavy feeder: apply well-rotted compost and a balanced fertilizer each autumn to the bed, and give another balanced feed immediately after the harvest period ends each spring.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Fern foliage turns yellow and wilts in summerFusarium crown rot or asparagus rust.Ensure excellent drainage; remove affected plants to prevent spread.
Thin spindly spearsBed too young; over-harvesting; or nutrient depletion.Do not harvest in years 1-2; limit harvest in year 3; feed heavily each autumn.
Orange-brown pustules on fern stemsAsparagus rust (Puccinia asparagi).Remove and destroy infected fern material; improve spacing and air flow.
Availability: Less commonly grown in Tunisia; crowns and seeds available from specialist nurseries, mainly in the north.
