Peruvian pepper tree

Schinus molle  ·  Family: Anacardiaceae
Trees · Ornamental / shade
EN Peruvian pepper tree  ·  FR Faux-poivrier  ·  AR فلفل بيروفي  ·  Schinus molle
Origin: South America, mainly Peru and the Andes
Light
Full sun
Water
Very low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Outdoor
In short An elegant weeping evergreen with aromatic feathery foliage and clusters of rosy-pink berries — extremely drought-tolerant and a classic shade tree in Tunisia's drier regions.
Light
Full sun; very tolerant of heat and exposure.
Water
Very low; established trees survive on rainfall alone in most of Tunisia.
Soil
Any well-drained soil; excellent in rocky, poor or alkaline ground.
Temperature
Best above 0 °C; mature trees survive short frost to about −5 °C.
Humidity
Very tolerant of low humidity; does well in arid zones.
Feeding
Minimal; slow-release fertilizer at planting is sufficient for the tree's lifetime.
Pruning & grooming
Light pruning to remove deadwood or raise the crown; tolerates cutting back.
Propagation
From seed sown fresh; semi-hardwood cuttings in summer.
Mature size
8–15 m tall; wide weeping crown.
Growth rate
Moderate to fast.
Lifespan
Very long-lived — several centuries possible.
Bloom / fruit season
Spring (April–June); tiny creamy-white flowers in long drooping panicles.
Hardiness (min temp)
Mature trees tolerate short frost to −5 °C; young plants need protection.
Toxicity & safety
Mildly toxic: the pink berries can cause stomach upset in children and pets; resin may irritate skin.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Needs very little feeding; a slow-release general fertilizer at planting time is enough for years.  Find fertilizer →

Availability: Common in Tunisian nurseries and roadside plantings; easy to grow from seed.
