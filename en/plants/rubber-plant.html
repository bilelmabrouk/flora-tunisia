Rubber plant

Ficus elastica  ·  Family: Moraceae
Interior · Foliage
EN Rubber plant  ·  FR Ficus caoutchouc  ·  AR فيكوس المطاط  ·  Ficus elastica
Origin: South and Southeast Asia (India to Malaysia)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Indoor
In short A bold, architectural indoor tree with large shiny leaves — tolerant and easy to grow, a reliable statement plant for any bright Tunisian interior.
Light
Bright indirect to some direct morning sun; low light causes slow growth and small leaves.
Water
Water when top 2–3 cm dry; wipe dusty leaves occasionally. Reduce in winter.
Soil
Rich, well-draining mix; avoid compacted or waterlogged soil.
Temperature
Thrives at 15–27 °C; dislikes cold below 12 °C.
Humidity
Adaptable; wipe leaves to keep them clean and glossy.
Feeding
Balanced liquid fertilizer every 3–4 weeks spring to early autumn.
Pruning & grooming
Prune to control height or shape in spring; wear gloves — latex sap irritates.
Propagation
Stem cuttings or air layering; let cut callus before potting.
Mature size
1–3 m indoors; can reach more without pruning.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Very long-lived — decades.
Bloom / fruit season
Does not produce ornamental flowers indoors.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; keep above 12 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Mildly toxic — latex causes skin and eye irritation; harmful if eaten.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced liquid fertilizer every 3–4 weeks from spring to early autumn encourages steady growth and large glossy leaves; stop feeding in winter.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellowing and dropping leavesOver-watering, cold draught, or sudden move.Find a stable, bright, warm spot; correct watering; avoid draughts.
Dull, dusty leavesDust accumulation reducing photosynthesis.Wipe each leaf gently with a damp cloth.
Brown edges or spotsUnder-watering, low humidity, or cold.Water more consistently; keep away from cold windows in winter.
