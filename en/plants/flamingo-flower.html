Flamingo flower

Anthurium andraeanum  ·  Family: Araceae
Interior · Flowering
EN Flamingo flower  ·  FR Anthurium  ·  AR أنثوريوم  ·  Anthurium andraeanum
Origin: Colombia and Ecuador (tropical rainforest)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Indoor
In short A long-blooming tropical houseplant with waxy red, pink, or white spathes that can last months — adds vivid colour to any Tunisian interior. Prefers humidity but adapts to brighter, well-lit rooms.
Light
Bright indirect light; avoid direct sun which bleaches and burns the spathes.
Water
Keep soil moist but never soggy; water every 5–7 days, less in winter. Allow the top 2 cm to dry.
Soil
Chunky, very well-aerated mix (orchid bark + perlite + potting mix); excellent drainage essential.
Temperature
Ideal 18–28 °C; do not let it drop below 15 °C.
Humidity
High humidity preferred (60%+); use a pebble tray or humidifier — critical in dry Tunisian summer.
Feeding
Every 6–8 weeks with a phosphorus-rich flower fertilizer during the growing season.
Pruning & grooming
Remove spent spathes and yellowing leaves at the base.
Propagation
Stem cuttings with at least one node and aerial root; or divide at repotting.
Mature size
40–60 cm tall.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Many years with proper care.
Bloom / fruit season
Nearly year-round; each spathe lasts 6–8 weeks.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; minimum 15 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Toxic if ingested (calcium oxalates, very irritant to mouth and throat); keep strictly away from children and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A phosphorus-rich fertilizer (like a bloom formula) every 6–8 weeks encourages continuous flowering; avoid high nitrogen which promotes leaves over flowers.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellow leavesOverwatering or poor drainage.Improve drainage; water only when top 2 cm dry out.
Brown or pale spathesDirect sun or too little humidity.Move away from direct sun; increase humidity with a pebble tray.
No new flowers despite healthy leavesInsufficient light or lack of phosphorus.Move to brighter indirect light; feed with a phosphorus-rich fertilizer.
Mealybugs on leaf axilsWarm dry indoor conditions.Dab with cotton soaked in alcohol; spray with neem oil solution.
Availability: Widely sold in Tunisian plant shops; popular as a gift plant year-round.
