Cardoon

Cynara cardunculus var. altilis  ·  Family: Asteraceae
Exterior · Vegetable
EN Cardoon  ·  FR Cardon  ·  AR خرشف  ·  Cynara cardunculus var. altilis
Origin: Mediterranean (North Africa & Southern Europe — wild ancestor of the artichoke)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A large, dramatic Mediterranean perennial related to the artichoke, grown for its blanched leafstalks and ribbed stems. Native to the Tunisian landscape, it is drought-tolerant and ornamentally striking.
Light
Full sun.
Water
Low to moderate once established; drought-tolerant.
Soil
Deep, well-drained soil; tolerates poor and stony soils.
Temperature
Tolerates heat and mild frost; best at 15–28 °C.
Humidity
Adaptable; withstands coastal and semi-arid conditions.
Feeding
Compost at planting; balanced fertilizer once in spring.
Pruning & grooming
Blanch stems by tying and wrapping to reduce bitterness; cut back after harvest.
Propagation
From seed in spring or by root division.
Mature size
1.5–2.5 m tall; very architectural.
Growth rate
Moderate; reaches full size in year 2.
Lifespan
Perennial — long-lived with good care.
Bloom / fruit season
Large purple thistle-like flowers in summer; ornamental.
Hardiness (min temp)
Tolerates light frost; established plants to −5 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Safe; edible when blanched.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Enrich the planting hole with compost; a single balanced spring fertilizer sustains growth without encouraging lax stems.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Bitter or tough stalksInsufficient blanching or overmature stalks.Blanch stems 3–4 weeks before harvest by tying and wrapping in darkness.
Aphid infestation on new growthAphids attracted to tender new leaves.Spray with water jet; encourage beneficial insects.
Plant collapsing or rotting at baseRoot rot from waterlogged soil.Improve drainage; plant in raised beds if soil is heavy.
Availability: Moderately available in Tunisia; found in some garden centres and traditional markets.
