Blue gum

Eucalyptus globulus  ·  Family: Myrtaceae
Trees · Ornamental / shade
EN Blue gum  ·  FR Eucalyptus bleu  ·  AR كاليتوس  ·  Eucalyptus globulus
Origin: SE Australia (Tasmania and Victoria)
Light
Full sun
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short The classic 'blue gum' with silvery juvenile leaves is widely planted in Tunisia's wetter north and highlands for timber and windbreaks. It grows extremely fast and its aromatic foliage repels insects.
Light
Full sun only.
Water
Low to moderate; highly drought-tolerant once established.
Soil
Wide soil tolerance; well-drained loam to clay; tolerates mildly acidic to alkaline.
Temperature
Hardier than E. camaldulensis; tolerates −6 to −8 °C; suited to northern highlands.
Humidity
Tolerates a range; performs best in Tunisia's wetter northern zones.
Feeding
Rarely needed on reasonable soils; light balanced feed at planting only.
Pruning & grooming
Remove hazardous branches; coppice if desired; avoid pruning in very hot weather.
Propagation
From seed; very fast germination.
Mature size
30–55 m in the wild; 20–35 m in Tunisia.
Growth rate
Extremely fast — 1.5–2+ m per year.
Lifespan
Long-lived, 150+ years.
Bloom / fruit season
Large creamy-white flowers in winter–spring; attractive to bees.
Hardiness (min temp)
More cold-tolerant than most eucalypts; handles light frost in regions 1, 2, and 4.
Toxicity & safety
Leaves toxic to pets if ingested; essential oil is toxic in concentrated form; keep children and pets away.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Blue gum rarely needs fertilizer; a light balanced application at planting on poor soils helps establishment; thereafter the tree is largely self-sufficient.  Find fertilizer →
