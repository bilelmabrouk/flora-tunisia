Japanese quince

Chaenomeles japonica  ·  Family: Rosaceae
Exterior · Shrub
EN Japanese quince  ·  FR Cognassier du Japon  ·  AR سفرجل ياباني  ·  Chaenomeles japonica
Origin: Japan
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A hardy, thorny deciduous shrub that blooms with vivid orange-red flowers in late winter to early spring before the leaves emerge — one of the earliest and most cheerful signs of spring in Tunisian gardens.
Light
Full sun to partial shade; best flowering in full sun.
Water
Moderate; water when the top 3–4 cm dry; drought-tolerant once established.
Soil
Well-drained loam; adaptable to most soils except waterlogged; slight acidity preferred.
Temperature
Very hardy; tolerates −20 °C and heat to 38 °C; suits all Tunisian regions.
Humidity
Average; not fussy about humidity.
Feeding
Balanced fertilizer in early spring to support flower and fruit development.
Pruning & grooming
Prune after flowering (spring) to maintain shape; thin out crowded stems in winter.
Propagation
Suckers (rooted side-shoots), semi-ripe cuttings, or layering.
Mature size
0.8–1.5 m tall; spreads up to 2 m wide.
Growth rate
Slow to moderate.
Lifespan
Long-lived — 20–40 years.
Bloom / fruit season
Vivid orange-red (or pink/white) flowers February–April before leaves; fragrant apple-like fruits in autumn.
Hardiness (min temp)
Exceptionally hardy to −20 °C; suits all Tunisian climates including cold north-western winters.
Toxicity & safety
Seeds contain trace amounts of cyanogenic compounds — do not eat raw seeds. Fruit flesh is safe and used for jams.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A balanced fertilizer once in early spring, just as flower buds swell, supports the flowering and the development of ornamental autumn fruits.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Brown spots on leavesLeaf spot fungal disease or scab in wet conditions.Remove and dispose of fallen leaves; improve air circulation; avoid overhead watering.
Failure to flower in a mild winterInsufficient winter chill hours to break dormancy.This is more likely in southern Tunisia; choose a location with cooler winter exposure or select a lower-chill cultivar.
Aphids clustering on new shoots in springTypical spring pest on soft new growth.Spray with diluted soap water; encourage natural predators.
Fireblight — wilted brown shoots looking scorchedBacterial disease (Erwinia amylovora) — a risk for rosaceous plants.Cut out affected branches 30 cm below visible infection; disinfect tools between cuts.
Availability: Not always common in Tunisian garden centres; more likely found in northern regions or specialist nurseries.
