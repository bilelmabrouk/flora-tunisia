Dragon tree

Dracaena marginata  ·  Family: Asparagaceae
Interior · Foliage
EN Dragon tree  ·  FR Dracéna marginé  ·  AR شجرة التنين  ·  Dracaena marginata
Origin: Madagascar
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Toxic if eaten
Setting
Indoor
In short A slender, architectural indoor tree with red-edged leaves on bare sculptural trunks — tough, drought-tolerant, and a favourite in Tunisian homes and offices.
Light
Bright indirect light to light shade; tolerates low light but loses colour.
Water
Allow soil to dry out well between waterings — one of the most drought-tolerant dracaenas. Use fluoride-free water.
Soil
Well-draining sandy universal mix.
Temperature
Best at 18–27 °C; cold below 13 °C causes damage.
Humidity
Tolerates dry air; benefits from occasional misting.
Feeding
Balanced liquid fertilizer every 6–8 weeks in spring and summer only.
Pruning & grooming
Cut canes to desired height to branch; remove browning leaf tips.
Propagation
Cane cuttings in moist mix; or tip cuttings.
Mature size
1–2.5 m indoors over years; multi-trunk specimens are striking.
Growth rate
Slow.
Lifespan
Decades — very long-lived.
Bloom / fruit season
Rarely blooms indoors.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; keep above 13 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Toxic to cats and dogs; mildly toxic to humans.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A light balanced feed every 6–8 weeks in spring and summer is enough; this slow grower needs minimal nutrition and is sensitive to salt build-up.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Brown tips on narrow leavesFluoride in water or low humidity.Use fluoride-free water; mist occasionally.
Yellow or pale leavesOver-watering or insufficient light.Check soil moisture and light conditions; adjust accordingly.
Wilting despite moist soilRoot rot from chronic over-watering.Repot; trim rotten roots; improve drainage.
