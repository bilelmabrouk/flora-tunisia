Queen palm

Syagrus romanzoffiana  ·  Family: Arecaceae
Trees · Palm / statement
EN Queen palm  ·  FR Palmier reine  ·  AR نخيل الملكة  ·  Syagrus romanzoffiana
Origin: South America (Brazil, Argentina, Paraguay, Uruguay)

Setting
Outdoor
In short The queen palm is an elegant feather-leaved palm with graceful arching fronds and a smooth grey trunk. Popular in Tunisian coastal gardens for its tropical appearance, though it needs regular feeding to thrive in local soils.
Light
Full sun — grows poorly in shade.
Water
Moderate: water regularly; tolerates short dry spells once established but looks best with consistent moisture.
Soil
Well-drained, slightly acidic sandy loam; very susceptible to iron and manganese chlorosis in alkaline soils.
Temperature
Best at 15–35 °C; frost-tender below −4 °C; young trees need protection.
Humidity
Tolerates coastal humidity well.
Feeding
Requires regular palm fertilizer — every 3–4 months — to prevent nutrient deficiency, especially in Tunisian alkaline soils.
Pruning & grooming
Remove dead or completely brown fronds; never remove fronds that are still partially green.
Propagation
From seed; germinates in 3–6 months.
Mature size
10–15 m tall; crown 5–8 m wide.
Growth rate
Moderate to fast — up to 60–90 cm per year.
Lifespan
Moderate to long; 50–80 years.
Bloom / fruit season
Long cream inflorescences in summer; orange-red date-like fruits ripen in autumn.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; protect young trees below 0 °C; established palms may survive brief frost to −4 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; small fruits edible but barely palatable.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) Queen palms are heavy feeders — a slow-release palm fertilizer with nitrogen, potassium, magnesium, and chelated iron every 3–4 months is essential, especially in Tunisia's alkaline coastal soils where deficiencies are rapid.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellow-orange fronds (chlorosis)Manganese or iron deficiency — very common in alkaline Tunisian soils.Apply chelated manganese and iron as foliar spray or soil drench; acidify soil slightly.
Frizzle top — small, narrow, stunted new frondsSevere manganese deficiency.Apply manganese sulfate or chelated manganese immediately to soil and as foliar spray.
Crown rot — new spear not emergingPhytophthora fungal infection, often after excessive watering.No cure if severe; reduce watering; improve drainage; remove and replace affected palm.
Availability: Available in Tunisian coastal nurseries and garden centres; moderately common.
