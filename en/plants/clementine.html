Clementine

Citrus × clementina  ·  Family: Rutaceae
Trees · Citrus
EN Clementine  ·  FR Clémentinier  ·  AR كليمنتين  ·  Citrus × clementina
Origin: Mediterranean (hybrid — mandarin × bitter orange; first documented in Algeria/Tunisia)

Full sun

Setting
Outdoor
In short The clementine is a seedless, easy-peel citrus prized across Tunisia for its sweetness and winter harvest. A natural hybrid with strong Mediterranean roots, it thrives in the same coastal and mild inland zones as the sweet orange.
Light
Full sun — needs good light for sweet, well-coloured fruit.
Water
Moderate and regular; avoid prolonged dryness or waterlogging.
Soil
Fertile, well-drained loam or sandy loam; pH 6–7; no standing water.
Temperature
Best at 15–30 °C; frost-sensitive below −2 to −3 °C.
Humidity
Moderate; tolerates coastal humidity.
Feeding
Monthly citrus fertilizer with NPK and micronutrients from spring through summer.
Pruning & grooming
Light annual pruning to remove dead wood and improve canopy airflow.
Propagation
Grafted on rootstock; seeds produce seedless fruits only if pollinated by another variety.
Mature size
2–4 m; compact and suitable for large containers.
Growth rate
Moderate — first crop in 2–4 years.
Lifespan
Long-lived — productive for 30+ years.
Bloom / fruit season
March–April; fragrant white blossoms.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-sensitive — protect below −2 to −3 °C; best in milder coastal zones.
Toxicity & safety
Completely safe; no known toxicity.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A citrus-specific fertilizer applied monthly in spring and summer, with emphasis on potassium as fruit develops, gives the sweetest clementines; avoid nitrogen-heavy feeds in late summer.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Fruit with many seeds (despite being a seedless variety)Cross-pollination from bees carrying pollen from another citrus variety planted nearby.Plant away from other citrus varieties, or choose a more isolated site; not a disease.
Fruit remains small and acidicInsufficient sunlight, water stress during development, or nutrient deficiency.Improve sun exposure; water consistently in summer; feed with a citrus fertilizer including potassium.
Ants running up and down the trunkAnts farming honeydew-producing scale insects or aphids on the tree.Apply a sticky tree band around the trunk to block ants; then treat the insects directly.
Availability: Commonly grown and sold throughout coastal Tunisia; a popular choice in home gardens and orchards.
