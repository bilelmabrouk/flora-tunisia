Japanese blood grass

Imperata cylindrica 'Red Baron'  ·  Family: Poaceae
Exterior · Ornamental grass
EN Japanese blood grass  ·  FR Impérate rouge  ·  AR حشيشة الدم  ·  Imperata cylindrica 'Red Baron'
Origin: Cultivar selected from East Asian Imperata cylindrica; the species is also native to Tunisia (as a weed)
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short A striking ornamental grass with blood-red tipped foliage that intensifies in full sun, best suited to cooler and more humid northern Tunisian gardens rather than the hot dry south.
Light
Full sun to light shade; red colour deepens in full sun; too much shade turns leaves green.
Water
Moderate; keep soil consistently moist; does not tolerate drought well; water regularly in summer.
Soil
Moist, well-drained, moderately fertile loam; tolerates clay if drainage is adequate.
Temperature
Performs best in mild to warm conditions; the red colour is strongest in cool autumn; frost-tolerant to about −15 °C but foliage dies back.
Humidity
Prefers moderate humidity; well-suited to northern and coastal Tunisia.
Feeding
Light balanced fertilizer in spring; avoid excess nitrogen which causes green-ening.
Pruning & grooming
Cut back to 5–10 cm in late winter to encourage fresh, vibrant red new growth.
Propagation
By careful division in spring; warn: species Imperata cylindrica is highly invasive — only grow the sterile 'Red Baron' cultivar.
Mature size
30–50 cm tall; slowly spreading clump.
Growth rate
Slow to moderate.
Lifespan
Perennial; long-lived with annual pruning.
Bloom / fruit season
Rarely flowers in cultivation; the foliage is the sole ornamental feature.
Hardiness (min temp)
Hardy to −15 °C; foliage dies in frost but regrows from roots in spring.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A light balanced fertilizer in spring encourages vigorous red growth; high nitrogen applications will turn the foliage green — avoid them.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Leaves turn green throughoutToo much shade, too much nitrogen, or genetic reversion to the wild form.Move to full sun; stop nitrogen; remove and discard any fully green-reverted shoots immediately.
Plant spreads aggressively beyond its areaImperata is by nature extremely invasive via rhizomes.Install a deep root barrier; inspect and remove escaping rhizomes each spring.
Poor colour in hot dry summerHeat and drought stress washes out the red pigmentation.Water regularly and mulch to keep root zone cool; avoid the hottest southern Tunisian sites.
Availability: Available in specialist Tunisian garden centres; not always widely stocked due to limited demand.
