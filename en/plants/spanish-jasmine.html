Spanish jasmine

Jasminum grandiflorum  ·  Family: Oleaceae
Exterior · Climber
EN Spanish jasmine  ·  FR Jasmin à grandes fleurs  ·  AR ياسمين بلدي  ·  Jasminum grandiflorum
Origin: South Asia and Arabian Peninsula; long cultivated in Tunisia
Light
Full sun
Water
Moderate
Care
Moderate
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Outdoor
In short The beloved 'ياسمين بلدي' of Tunisian culture, filling summer evenings with its intoxicating fragrance — an icon of Tunisian gardens, terraces, and the perfume industry of Nabeul.
Light
Full sun; at least 6 hours for generous flowering.
Water
Moderate; keep soil evenly moist in summer. Reduce in winter.
Soil
Well-drained, fertile soil with compost; tolerates most garden soils.
Temperature
Frost-tender; best above 5 °C. Suited to coastal and southern Tunisia.
Humidity
Appreciates moderate humidity; tolerates coastal conditions.
Feeding
Every 2–3 weeks spring–autumn with a flowering-plant fertilizer.
Pruning & grooming
Trim after each flowering flush; cut back moderately in late winter.
Propagation
Semi-ripe cuttings in summer or layering.
Mature size
Climbs or scrambles 3–5 m.
Growth rate
Moderate to fast.
Lifespan
Long-lived; decades with good care.
Bloom / fruit season
June–October; white star-shaped flowers, intensely fragrant especially at night.
Hardiness (min temp)
Frost-tender; protect below 5 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic; flowers edible and used in perfumery and cooking.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A flowering-plant fertilizer applied every 2–3 weeks throughout the flowering season sustains the long bloom and intensifies fragrance.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
No flowersInsufficient sun or excess nitrogen.Move to full sun; switch to a high-phosphorus fertilizer.
Leaves yellowingOverwatering, poor drainage, or iron deficiency in alkaline soil.Improve drainage; apply iron chelate if soil is alkaline.
Spider mites in summer heatHot, dry conditions in mid-summer.Spray with water jet; insecticidal soap for heavy infestations.
Stem dieback after cold winterFrost damage on exposed stems.Cut dead stems back to healthy wood in spring; new growth emerges quickly.
Availability: Very widely grown and sold throughout Tunisia; especially abundant in the Nabeul area where it is cultivated for perfume.
