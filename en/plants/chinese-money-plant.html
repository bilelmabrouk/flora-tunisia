Chinese money plant

Pilea peperomioides  ·  Family: Urticaceae
Interior · Foliage
EN Chinese money plant  ·  FR Plante monnaie chinoise  ·  AR نبتة الفلوس  ·  Pilea peperomioides
Origin: South-West China (Yunnan province)
Light
Sun / part shade
Water
Low
Care
Easy
Safety
Pet & child safe
Setting
Indoor
In short A cheerful, compact houseplant with perfectly round coin-like leaves on upright stems — easy to care for and easy to share via the pups it freely produces. Happy in any bright Tunisian room year-round.
Light
Bright indirect light; avoid direct midday sun to prevent leaf scorch.
Water
Allow soil to dry out halfway between waterings; water about once a week in summer, less in winter.
Soil
Well-draining potting mix; add perlite for extra drainage.
Temperature
Ideal 15–25 °C; tolerates down to 10 °C briefly.
Humidity
Tolerates average household humidity; no misting needed.
Feeding
Every 2–4 weeks in spring–summer with a diluted balanced fertilizer; none in winter.
Pruning & grooming
Remove yellowing leaves; no pruning needed. Rotate pot weekly for even growth.
Propagation
Separate the pups (offshoots) at the base once they have a few leaves; pot individually.
Mature size
20–40 cm tall and wide.
Growth rate
Moderate.
Lifespan
Several years; indefinite if divided regularly.
Bloom / fruit season
Small inconspicuous white flowers in spring; not the main attraction.
Hardiness (min temp)
Not frost-hardy; keep above 10 °C.
Toxicity & safety
Non-toxic to humans and pets.
Fertilizer (Tunisia) A diluted balanced fertilizer (20-20-20) every 3–4 weeks in spring and summer is plenty; over-fertilizing causes yellowing.  Find fertilizer →

Troubleshooting
SymptomLikely causeWhat to do
Yellowing leavesOver-watering or too much direct sun.Let soil dry out more; move away from direct sun.
Leaves curling downwardUnderwatering or very dry air.Water thoroughly and increase ambient humidity slightly.
Leggy stems reaching for lightInsufficient light.Move to a brighter spot and rotate pot weekly.
Fungus gnats in soilSoil kept too moist.Let the topsoil dry out more between waterings; use a sticky trap if needed.
Availability: Increasingly available in Tunisian plant shops and online; easy to obtain from other growers via pup sharing.
